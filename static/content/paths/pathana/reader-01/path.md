## reading - बालकः पठति

**Source:** Controlled prose (Level 1)

@deva[बालकः पठति।]

*bālakaḥ paṭhati* — "The boy reads."

---

## concept - Word-by-Word: बालकः पठति

Each word parsed:

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[बालकः] | @deva[बालक] + @deva[सु] (nom. sg. m.) | the boy |
| @deva[पठति] | @deva[पठ्] + @deva[शप्] + @deva[ति] (3sg. pres.) | reads / is reading |

**Nominative @deva[-ः]** marks the subject (see @ref[4.1.2]).

**@deva[पठति]** follows Class 1 (@deva[भ्वादि]): @deva[पठ्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति] = @deva[पठति]. The @deva[अ] is the @term[vikaraṇa] (@deva[शप्], @ref[3.1.68]).

---

## reading - राजा ग्रामं गच्छति

**Source:** Controlled prose (Level 1)

@deva[राजा ग्रामं गच्छति।]

*rājā grāmaṃ gacchati* — "The king goes to the village."

---

## concept - Word-by-Word: राजा ग्रामं गच्छति

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[राजा] | @deva[राजन्] + @deva[सु] → @deva[राजा] (nom. sg. m.) | the king |
| @deva[ग्रामम्] | @deva[ग्राम] + @deva[अम्] (acc. sg. m.) | the village |
| @deva[गच्छति] | @deva[गम्] (special stem @deva[गच्छ्]) + @deva[ति] (3sg. pres.) | goes |

**Accusative @deva[-म्]** marks the goal of motion — where the king is going.

**@deva[गच्छति]**: The root @deva[गम्] takes the special class stem @deva[गच्छ] before @deva[लट्] endings. This is listed in the @deva[भ्वादि] gaṇa with an irregular stem.

**Note on @deva[राजा]**: The stem @deva[राजन्] is a consonant stem (n-stem). In nominative singular, the @deva[-न्] is dropped and the vowel lengthens: @deva[राजन् + सु] → @deva[राजा].

---

## concept - Reading Strategy

When reading Sanskrit, look for these signals:

1. **@deva[-ः] at end** → subject (@deva[प्रथमा])
2. **@deva[-म्] at end** → object (@deva[द्वितीया])
3. **@deva[-ति] at end** → 3rd person singular present
4. **@deva[-न्ति] at end** → 3rd person plural present

The **verb usually comes last** in Sanskrit sentences, but word order is flexible.
