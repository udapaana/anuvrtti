## concept - What You'll Learn

@deva[लट्] (@term[vartamāna]) is the most common verb form in Sanskrit. By the end of this path, you'll understand:

- How @deva[भू] becomes @deva[भवति] "he is"
- How @deva[पच्] becomes @deva[पचति] "he cooks"
- The 18 personal endings and how they organize
- When to use @term[parasmaipada]{परस्मैपद} vs @term[ātmanepada]{आत्मनेपद}

Here's a preview of the derivation you'll learn to trace:

@prakriya[bhU + laT + tip]

Let's start with the rule that introduces present tense.

---

## 3.2.123 - When लट् Applies

**Key Terms:** vartamAna, laT

@deva[वर्तमाने लट्] — @deva[लट्] comes after a root when the action is happening now (@deva[वर्तमान]).

@deva[वर्तमान] means "begun but not yet ended." It covers:

- **Ongoing actions**: @deva[पचति] "he cooks / he is cooking"
- **Habitual actions**: @deva[गच्छति] "he goes (regularly)"
- **General truths**: @deva[तिष्ठन्ति पर्वताः] "the mountains stand"

The @deva[लट्] is just a placeholder — it gets replaced by actual endings.

---

## concept - The Ten लकाराः

Pāṇini uses ten abstract markers called @deva[लकार] to represent tense/mood:

| @deva[लकार]      | Meaning                           | Example          |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------- | ---------------- |
| @deva[लट्]       | @term[vartamāna] (present)        | @deva[भवति]      |
| @deva[लिट्]      | @term[parokṣa] (perfect)          | @deva[बभूव]      |
| @deva[लुट्]      | @term[anadyatana] (periphr. fut.) | @deva[भविता]     |
| @deva[लृट्]      | @term[bhaviṣyat] (simple future)  | @deva[भविष्यति]  |
| @deva[लोट्]      | imperative                        | @deva[भवतु]      |
| @deva[लङ्]       | @term[anadyatana] @term[bhūta]    | @deva[अभवत्]     |
| @deva[विधिलिङ्]  | @term[vidhiliṅ] (optative)        | @deva[भवेत्]     |
| @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] | @term[āśīrliṅ] (benedictive)      | @deva[भूयात्]    |
| @deva[लुङ्]      | @term[bhūta] (aorist)             | @deva[अभूत्]     |
| @deva[लृङ्]      | conditional                       | @deva[अभविष्यत्] |

The @deva[ल] in each is replaced by personal endings. That's what the next rules establish.

---

## 3.4.77 - ल Becomes Endings

**Key Terms:** lasya, tip

@deva[लस्य] — The @deva[ल] of a @deva[लकार] is replaced by personal endings.

This is an @deva[अधिकार] (heading rule) that governs everything that follows. It tells us: when you see @deva[लट्], @deva[लिट्], etc., the @deva[ल] isn't pronounced — it's a slot for endings.

---

## 3.4.78 - The 18 तिङ् Endings

**Key Terms:** tiN, tip, tas, Ji

The 18 @term[tiṅ]{तिङ्} endings that replace @deva[ल]:

**@deva[परस्मैपद]** (for others / active):
| | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|--|----------|------|--------|
| 3rd | @deva[ति] | @deva[तस्] | @deva[अन्ति] |
| 2nd | @deva[सि] | @deva[थस्] | @deva[थ] |
| 1st | @deva[मि] | @deva[वस्] | @deva[मस्] |

**@deva[आत्मनेपद]** (for oneself / middle):
| | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|--|----------|------|--------|
| 3rd | @deva[ते] | @deva[आते] | @deva[अन्ते] |
| 2nd | @deva[से] | @deva[आथे] | @deva[ध्वे] |
| 1st | @deva[ए] | @deva[वहे] | @deva[महे] |

The raw endings have marker letters (like @deva[तिप्], @deva[तस्], @deva[झि]) that get deleted.

---

## 1.4.99 - परस्मैपद Defined

**Key Terms:** parasmEpadam

@deva[लः परस्मैपदम्] — The first nine endings (@deva[ति] through @deva[मस्]) are called @deva[परस्मैपद] "word for another."

This set is used when the action benefits someone other than the agent, or as the default.

---

## 1.4.100 - आत्मनेपद Defined

**Key Terms:** Atmanepadam

@deva[तङानावात्मनेपदम्] — The nine endings under @deva[तङ्] (@deva[ते] through @deva[महे]) are called @deva[आत्मनेपद] "word for oneself."

Used when the action benefits the agent, or with certain roots by convention.

---

## concept - Person and Number

The endings organize into a 3×3 grid:

## 1.4.101 - Three Persons

**Key Terms:** praTama, maDyama, uttama

@deva[तिङस्त्रीणि त्रीणि प्रथममध्यमोत्तमाः] — The @deva[तिङ्] endings group in threes:

- @deva[प्रथम] (3rd person): he/she/it/they
- @deva[मध्यम] (2nd person): you
- @deva[उत्तम] (1st person): I/we

---

## 1.4.102 - Three Numbers

**Key Terms:** ekavacanam, dvivacanam, bahuvacanam

@deva[तान्येकवचनद्विवचनबहुवचनान्येकशः] — Within each person:

- @deva[एकवचन] (singular): one
- @deva[द्विवचन] (dual): two
- @deva[बहुवचन] (plural): three or more

So @deva[भवति] is 3rd person singular, @deva[भवतः] is 3rd person dual, @deva[भवन्ति] is 3rd person plural.

---

## concept - Adding the विकरण

Between the root and the ending, most present-tense forms need a "class marker" (@deva[विकरण]). For the largest class (Class 1), this is @deva[शप्], which becomes @deva[अ]:

@deva[भू] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति] = @deva[भवति]

---

## 3.1.68 - शप् for Class 1 Roots

**Key Terms:** Sap, vikaraNa, kartari

@deva[कर्तरि शप्] — The affix @deva[शप्] comes after a root when the agent is expressed and a @deva[सार्वधातुक] affix follows.

@deva[शप्] = @deva[अ] (the @deva[श्] and @deva[प्] are markers)

This @deva[अ] triggers important changes:

- @deva[गुण] of the root vowel (@ref[7.3.84])
- Becomes the basis for the "thematic" conjugation

---

## 7.3.84 - गुण Before Certain Affixes

**Key Terms:** guRa, sArvaDAtukaH

@deva[सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः] — @term[guṇa]{गुण} replaces the final @pratyahara[ik] vowel of an @deva[अङ्ग] before @term[sārvadhātuka]{सार्वधातुक} or @term[ārdhadhātuka]{आर्धधातुक} affixes.

This is why:

- @deva[भू] → @deva[भो] → @deva[भव्] (before the @deva[अ] of @deva[शप्])
- @deva[नी] → @deva[ने] → @deva[नय्]
- @deva[तुद्] → @deva[तोद्] (in Class 6)

## 1.3.78 - Default: Use परस्मैपद

**Key Terms:** kartari, parasmEpadam

@deva[शेषात् कर्तरि परस्मैपदम्] — For roots not covered by special @deva[आत्मनेपद] rules (@ref[1.3.12]–@ref[1.3.77]), use @deva[परस्मैपद] when the agent is expressed.

This is the default: most roots take @deva[परस्मैपद] in active sentences.

- @deva[गच्छति] "he goes"
- @deva[पचति] "he cooks"
- @deva[भवति] "he is"

In passive voice, switch to @deva[आत्मनेपद]: @deva[पच्यते] "it is cooked."

---

## concept - Sandhi: इक् → यण्

When vowels meet at morpheme boundaries, sandhi rules apply.

---

## 6.1.77 - इक् Becomes Semivowel Before Vowel

**Key Terms:** ikaH, yaR, aci

@deva[इको यणचि] — An @pratyahara[ik] vowel (@deva[इ], @deva[उ], @deva[ऋ], @deva[ऌ]) becomes @pratyahara[yan] (@deva[य्], @deva[व्], @deva[र्], @deva[ल्]) before another vowel.

| Before     | Becomes   |
| ---------- | --------- |
| @deva[इ/ई] | @deva[य्] |
| @deva[उ/ऊ] | @deva[व्] |
| @deva[ऋ/ॠ] | @deva[र्] |
| @deva[ऌ]   | @deva[ल्] |

Examples:

- @deva[दधि] + @deva[अत्र] = @deva[दध्यत्र]
- @deva[मधु] + @deva[अत्र] = @deva[मध्वत्र]

This also applies inside verb forms when stems end in these vowels.

---

## concept - Putting It Together

Let's trace @deva[भवति] step by step:

1. Root: @deva[भू] "to be"
2. Add @deva[लट्] (present tense): @deva[भू] + @deva[लट्]
3. Replace @deva[ल] with @deva[तिप्] (3sg P): @deva[भू] + @deva[ति]
4. Add @deva[शप्] (= @deva[अ]) as विकरण: @deva[भू] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति]
5. गुण of @deva[ऊ] → @deva[ओ]: @deva[भो] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति]
6. @deva[ओ] + @deva[अ] = @deva[अव्] by @ref[6.1.78]: @deva[भव्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति]
7. Final: @deva[भवति]

## concept - What's Next?

You now understand the basic machinery of present tense:

- @deva[लट्] marks present tense
- 18 @deva[तिङ्] endings (9 परस्मैपद + 9 आत्मनेपद)
- @deva[शप्] (= @deva[अ]) links root to ending for Class 1
- गुण strengthens the root vowel

- **Imperative** (@deva[लोट्]) — commands
- **Imperfect** (@deva[लङ्]) — past tense
- Other verb classes (2-10) with different विकरणs
