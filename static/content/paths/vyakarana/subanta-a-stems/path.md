## concept - The Most Common Declension

@deva[अकारान्त] @term[prātipadika] — stems ending in short @deva[अ] — are the most common noun type in Sanskrit, accounting for roughly 60% of all nouns you will encounter. Mastering this single declension pattern unlocks a huge portion of the language.

This path covers both @term[puṃliṅga] (masculine) and @term[napuṃsakaliṅga] (neuter) @deva[अ]-stems. You will learn:

- The complete masculine paradigm using @deva[देव] "god" as a model
- The complete neuter paradigm using @deva[वन] "forest" as a model
- The specific @deva[सूत्र] rules that produce each form
- How to identify a-stem forms when reading Sanskrit

**Common a-stem nouns you already know:**

| Masculine    | Meaning | Neuter        | Meaning   |
| ------------ | ------- | ------------- | --------- |
| @deva[देव]   | god     | @deva[वन]     | forest    |
| @deva[नर]    | man     | @deva[फल]     | fruit     |
| @deva[अश्व]  | horse   | @deva[पुस्तक] | book      |
| @deva[ग्राम] | village | @deva[कुण्ड]  | pot       |
| @deva[वृक्ष] | tree    | @deva[सुख]    | happiness |

Let's begin with the full masculine paradigm, then learn the rules that derive each form.

---

## concept - The Masculine Paradigm: देव

Here is the complete declension of @deva[देव] "god" (masculine a-stem):

| Case                         | Singular      | Dual              | Plural          |
| ---------------------------- | ------------- | ----------------- | --------------- |
| Nominative (@deva[प्रथमा])   | @deva[देवः]   | @deva[देवौ]       | @deva[देवाः]    |
| Accusative (@deva[द्वितीया]) | @deva[देवम्]  | @deva[देवौ]       | @deva[देवान्]   |
| Instrumental (@deva[तृतीया]) | @deva[देवेन]  | @deva[देवाभ्याम्] | @deva[देवैः]    |
| Dative (@deva[चतुर्थी])      | @deva[देवाय]  | @deva[देवाभ्याम्] | @deva[देवेभ्यः] |
| Ablative (@deva[पञ्चमी])     | @deva[देवात्] | @deva[देवाभ्याम्] | @deva[देवेभ्यः] |
| Genitive (@deva[षष्ठी])      | @deva[देवस्य] | @deva[देवयोः]     | @deva[देवानाम्] |
| Locative (@deva[सप्तमी])     | @deva[देवे]   | @deva[देवयोः]     | @deva[देवेषु]   |

**Things to notice:**

- The dual forms for Dat./Abl./Ins. are all @deva[देवाभ्याम्] — one form for three cases
- Genitive and Locative dual share @deva[देवयोः]
- Dative and Ablative plural share @deva[देवेभ्यः]
- The stem @deva[अ] changes to @deva[ए], @deva[ऐ], or @deva[ओ] before certain endings

Now let's learn the rules that produce these forms, starting with the Nominative.

---

## 6.1.102 - Nominative Singular: देवः

**Key Terms:** @deva[प्रथमा], @deva[पूर्वसवर्ण], @deva[दीर्घ]

@deva[प्रथमयोः पूर्वसवर्णः] — For the first two cases (Nominative and Accusative), when the stem-final vowel and the vowel of the ending are homogeneous (@deva[सवर्ण]), a long vowel of the same class substitutes for both.

Let's trace the Nominative singular of @deva[देव] step by step:

1. **Start:** @deva[देव] + @deva[सु] (Nominative singular ending from @ref[4.1.2])
2. **Apply @ref[6.1.102]:** The stem-final @deva[अ] and the @deva[उ] of @deva[सु] are not homogeneous, so this rule does not combine them. The @deva[उ] of @deva[सु] is actually a @deva[इत्] marker and drops by @ref[1.3.2].
3. **After @deva[इत्]-deletion:** @deva[देव] + @deva[स्]
4. **@deva[रुत्व] by @ref[8.2.66]:** Word-final @deva[स्] becomes @deva[रु] (= @deva[र्])
5. **Visarga by @ref[8.3.15]:** @deva[र्] becomes @deva[ः] at the end of a pause
6. **Result:** @deva[देवः]

For the Accusative singular, @deva[देव] + @deva[अम्] gives @deva[देवम्] — the @deva[अ] of the stem and the @deva[अ] of @deva[अम्] merge to @deva[आ] by this rule (both are @deva[सवर्ण]), then the final @deva[म्] remains. But by convention, the traditional form is @deva[देवम्].

**More examples:**

- @deva[नर] + @deva[सु] = @deva[नरः] "the man"
- @deva[अश्व] + @deva[सु] = @deva[अश्वः] "the horse"
- @deva[वृक्ष] + @deva[सु] = @deva[वृक्षः] "the tree"

---

## 7.1.24 - Neuter Nominative: वनम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[अत्], @deva[अम्], @deva[नपुंसक]

@deva[अतोऽम्] — After a neuter stem ending in @deva[अ], the ending @deva[अम्] substitutes for @deva[सु] (N.sg) and @deva[अम्] (A.sg).

This is why neuter a-stems look different from masculine in the Nominative:

**Masculine:** @deva[देव] + @deva[सु] = @deva[देवः] (visarga ending)

**Neuter:** @deva[वन] + @deva[सु] → @deva[वन] + @deva[अम्] (by this rule) = @deva[वनम्]

The derivation:

1. **Start:** @deva[वन] + @deva[सु]
2. **Apply @ref[7.1.24]:** Since @deva[वन] is neuter and ends in @deva[अ], @deva[अम्] replaces @deva[सु]
3. **@ref[6.1.102]:** @deva[अ] + @deva[अ] → @deva[आ] (homogeneous merge)
4. **Result:** @deva[वनम्]

This means Nominative and Accusative singular are **identical** for neuter a-stems — always ending in @deva[-म्].

**More examples:**

- @deva[फल] + @deva[सु] → @deva[फलम्] "the fruit"
- @deva[कुण्ड] + @deva[सु] → @deva[कुण्डम्] "the pot"
- @deva[सुख] + @deva[सु] → @deva[सुखम्] "happiness"
- @deva[पुस्तक] + @deva[सु] → @deva[पुस्तकम्] "the book"

---

## concept - The Neuter Paradigm: वन

Here is the complete declension of @deva[वन] "forest" (neuter a-stem):

| Case                         | Singular     | Dual             | Plural         |
| ---------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------- | -------------- |
| Nominative (@deva[प्रथमा])   | @deva[वनम्]  | @deva[वने]       | @deva[वनानि]   |
| Accusative (@deva[द्वितीया]) | @deva[वनम्]  | @deva[वने]       | @deva[वनानि]   |
| Instrumental (@deva[तृतीया]) | @deva[वनेन]  | @deva[वनाभ्याम्] | @deva[वनैः]    |
| Dative (@deva[चतुर्थी])      | @deva[वनाय]  | @deva[वनाभ्याम्] | @deva[वनेभ्यः] |
| Ablative (@deva[पञ्चमी])     | @deva[वनात्] | @deva[वनाभ्याम्] | @deva[वनेभ्यः] |
| Genitive (@deva[षष्ठी])      | @deva[वनस्य] | @deva[वनयोः]     | @deva[वनानाम्] |
| Locative (@deva[सप्तमी])     | @deva[वने]   | @deva[वनयोः]     | @deva[वनेषु]   |

**Key difference from masculine:** Only Nominative and Accusative forms differ. Cases 3 through 7 are **identical** to the masculine pattern.

|        | Masculine @deva[देव] | Neuter @deva[वन] |
| ------ | -------------------- | ---------------- |
| N. sg. | @deva[देवः]          | @deva[वनम्]      |
| N. du. | @deva[देवौ]          | @deva[वने]       |
| N. pl. | @deva[देवाः]         | @deva[वनानि]     |
| A. sg. | @deva[देवम्]         | @deva[वनम्]      |
| A. du. | @deva[देवौ]          | @deva[वने]       |
| A. pl. | @deva[देवान्]        | @deva[वनानि]     |
| I. sg. | @deva[देवेन]         | @deva[वनेन]      |
| (rest) | (same pattern)       | (same pattern)   |

Neuter forms are governed by special rules: @ref[7.1.24] for singular, @ref[7.1.19] for dual, and @ref[7.1.20] for plural. Let's learn the masculine rules first, then come back to these.

## 7.1.9 - Instrumental Plural: देवैः

**Key Terms:** @deva[अत्], @deva[भिस्], @deva[ऐस्]

@deva[अतो भिस ऐस्] — After a stem ending in @deva[अ], @deva[ऐस्] substitutes for the case-ending @deva[भिस्] (Instrumental plural).

Without this rule, we would expect \*@deva[देवभिः]. Instead:

1. **Start:** @deva[देव] + @deva[भिस्]
2. **Apply @ref[7.1.9]:** @deva[ऐस्] replaces @deva[भिस्]
3. **@deva[अ] + @deva[ऐ] merge:** @deva[देवैस्]
4. **@deva[स्] → @deva[ः] at pause:** @deva[देवैः]

This is one of the most distinctive a-stem forms. The @deva[-ऐः] ending appears only in this one cell of the paradigm.

**More examples:**

- @deva[वृक्षैः] "by the trees"
- @deva[नरैः] "by the men"
- @deva[फलैः] "by the fruits" (neuter — same form!)
- @deva[अश्वैः] "by the horses"

---

## 7.3.102 - Genitive/Locative Dual: देवयोः

**Key Terms:** @deva[सुप्], @deva[अत्], @deva[ओ]

@deva[सुपि च] — Before certain @deva[सुप्] (case) endings, the final @deva[अ] of the stem becomes @deva[ओ].

This rule works together with @ref[7.3.101] (@deva[अतो दीर्घो यञि]) to produce the Genitive and Locative dual forms:

1. **Start:** @deva[देव] + @deva[ओस्] (G./L. dual ending)
2. **Apply @ref[7.3.102]:** The @deva[अ] of @deva[देव] becomes @deva[ओ]
3. **@deva[ओ] + @deva[ओ] merge:** by @ref[6.1.101], homogeneous vowels yield the long form — but here @deva[ओ] is already the substitute
4. **Result:** @deva[देवयोः]

The @deva[य्] appears through @ref[6.1.78] (@deva[एचोऽयवायावः]) — the diphthong @deva[ओ] becomes @deva[अव्] before the following vowel, then simplifies.

**More examples:**

- @deva[नरयोः] "of/in the two men"
- @deva[अश्वयोः] "of/in the two horses"
- @deva[वनयोः] "of/in the two forests"

---

## 7.3.103 - Stem Change in Plural: देवेभ्यः, देवेषु

**Key Terms:** @deva[बहुवचन], @deva[झल्], @deva[एत्]

@deva[बहुवचने झल्येत्] — In the plural, before endings that begin with a @deva[झल्] consonant, the stem-final @deva[अ] becomes @deva[ए].

The @deva[झल्] consonants include @deva[भ्] and @deva[ष्], which is why this rule affects the Dative/Ablative plural and Locative plural:

**Dative/Ablative plural:**

1. @deva[देव] + @deva[भ्यस्]
2. @deva[भ्] is @deva[झल्]-initial, and the ending is plural
3. @deva[अ] → @deva[ए]: @deva[देवेभ्यस्]
4. Final @deva[स्] → @deva[ः]: @deva[देवेभ्यः]

**Locative plural:**

1. @deva[देव] + @deva[सुप्] (the L.pl ending, not to be confused with the @deva[सुप्] collection name)
2. @deva[ष्] is @deva[झल्]-initial (the @deva[स्] of @deva[सु] becomes @deva[ष्] after @deva[ए] by @ref[8.3.59])
3. @deva[अ] → @deva[ए]: @deva[देवेषु]

**More examples:**

- @deva[वृक्षेभ्यः] "to/from the trees"
- @deva[नरेषु] "among the men"
- @deva[ग्रामेभ्यः] "to/from the villages"
- @deva[फलेषु] "in the fruits"

---

## 7.3.105 - Locative Singular: देवे

**Key Terms:** @deva[आङ्], @deva[ए]

@deva[आङि चापः] — Before @deva[ङि] (the Locative singular ending), the stem-final @deva[अ] becomes @deva[ए].

This is a straightforward substitution:

1. **Start:** @deva[देव] + @deva[ङि]
2. **Apply @ref[7.3.105]:** @deva[अ] → @deva[ए]
3. **Drop @deva[ङ्] (marker):** only @deva[इ] remains of the ending
4. **@deva[ए] + @deva[इ] merge:** the @deva[ए] absorbs @deva[इ] by @ref[6.1.104]
5. **Result:** @deva[देवे]

**More examples:**

- @deva[वने] "in the forest"
- @deva[ग्रामे] "in the village"
- @deva[नरे] "in the man"
- @deva[वृक्षे] "in the tree"

Note: The Locative singular @deva[देवे] and the Neuter Nominative/Accusative dual @deva[वने] look identical in form, but arise from different rules. Context determines which is meant.

---

## 7.1.19 - Neuter Dual: वने

**Key Terms:** @deva[नपुंसक], @deva[ई]

@deva[नपुंसकाच्च] — After a neuter stem, @deva[ई] substitutes for the dual endings @deva[औ] and @deva[औट्] (Nominative dual and Accusative dual).

In the masculine, N./A. dual is @deva[देवौ] (with @deva[औ]). But for neuters:

1. **Start:** @deva[वन] + @deva[औ]
2. **Apply @ref[7.1.19]:** @deva[ई] replaces @deva[औ]
3. **@deva[अ] + @deva[ई]:** by @ref[6.1.87] (@deva[आद्गुणः]), @deva[अ] + @deva[ई] = @deva[ए]
4. **Result:** @deva[वने]

**More examples:**

- @deva[फले] "the two fruits"
- @deva[कुण्डे] "the two pots"
- @deva[पुस्तके] "the two books"
- @deva[सुखे] "the two happinesses"

---

## 7.1.20 - Neuter Plural: वनानि

**Key Terms:** @deva[जस्], @deva[शस्], @deva[शि]

@deva[जश्शसोः शिः] — After a neuter stem, @deva[शि] substitutes for @deva[जस्] (N.pl) and @deva[शस्] (A.pl).

After the @deva[श्] marker drops, we are left with @deva[इ]. Combined with the @deva[नुम्] augment from @ref[7.1.72], this produces the distinctive neuter plural:

1. **Start:** @deva[वन] + @deva[जस्]
2. **Apply @ref[7.1.20]:** @deva[शि] (= @deva[इ]) replaces @deva[जस्]
3. **Apply @ref[7.1.72]:** @deva[नुम्] augment is inserted (see next rule)
4. **@deva[अ] + @deva[नुम्] + @deva[इ]:** @deva[वनानि]

The result: @deva[-आनि] is the unmistakable neuter plural ending for a-stems.

**More examples:**

- @deva[फलानि] "fruits"
- @deva[कुण्डानि] "pots"
- @deva[पुस्तकानि] "books"
- @deva[सुखानि] "happinesses"

---

## 7.1.72 - The नुम् Augment

**Key Terms:** @deva[नपुंसक], @deva[झल्], @deva[नुम्]

@deva[नपुंसकस्य झलचः] — For a neuter stem, when the @deva[शि] ending (from @ref[7.1.20]) follows, the augment @deva[नुम्] is inserted after the last vowel of the stem.

This augment adds the @deva[न्] that makes neuter plurals so distinctive:

1. **Start:** @deva[वन] + @deva[इ] (from @deva[शि])
2. **Apply @ref[7.1.72]:** @deva[नुम्] inserts after the final @deva[अ] of the stem
3. **@deva[नुम्] = @deva[न्]** (the @deva[उ] and @deva[म्] are markers)
4. **@deva[वन] + @deva[न्] + @deva[इ]:** @deva[अ] + @deva[इ] = @deva[ए] by @ref[6.1.87] would apply, but the @deva[न्] intervenes
5. **@ref[6.1.101]:** @deva[अ] + @deva[अ] (of @deva[नुम्]'s position) = @deva[आ]
6. **Result:** @deva[वनानि]

The @deva[-आनि] pattern applies to all neuter a-stems:

- @deva[फल] → @deva[फलानि]
- @deva[कुण्ड] → @deva[कुण्डानि]
- @deva[जल] → @deva[जलानि] "waters"

## concept - Reading Practice

Let's apply what you've learned to real Sanskrit sentences. Identify every a-stem form, its case, number, and meaning.

**Sentence 1:**

@deva[देवः वने फलं खादति]

| Word        | Stem       | Case       | Number   | Meaning              |
| ----------- | ---------- | ---------- | -------- | -------------------- |
| @deva[देवः] | @deva[देव] | Nominative | Singular | "the god" (subject)  |
| @deva[वने]  | @deva[वन]  | Locative   | Singular | "in the forest"      |
| @deva[फलम्] | @deva[फल]  | Accusative | Singular | "the fruit" (object) |

Translation: "The god eats a fruit in the forest."

**Sentence 2:**

@deva[नरैः ग्रामेषु वृक्षाः रक्ष्यन्ते]

| Word            | Stem         | Case         | Number | Meaning               |
| --------------- | ------------ | ------------ | ------ | --------------------- |
| @deva[नरैः]     | @deva[नर]    | Instrumental | Plural | "by the men"          |
| @deva[ग्रामेषु] | @deva[ग्राम] | Locative     | Plural | "in the villages"     |
| @deva[वृक्षाः]  | @deva[वृक्ष] | Nominative   | Plural | "the trees" (subject) |

Translation: "The trees are protected by the men in the villages."

**Sentence 3:**

@deva[देवाय फलानि नरः ग्रामात् आनयति]

| Word            | Stem         | Case       | Number   | Meaning             |
| --------------- | ------------ | ---------- | -------- | ------------------- |
| @deva[देवाय]    | @deva[देव]   | Dative     | Singular | "for the god"       |
| @deva[फलानि]    | @deva[फल]    | Accusative | Plural   | "fruits" (object)   |
| @deva[नरः]      | @deva[नर]    | Nominative | Singular | "the man" (subject) |
| @deva[ग्रामात्] | @deva[ग्राम] | Ablative   | Singular | "from the village"  |

Translation: "The man brings fruits from the village for the god."

**Practice:** For each form above, can you identify which rule produced it? For example, @deva[नरैः] uses @ref[7.1.9] (@deva[ऐस्] for @deva[भिस्]), and @deva[ग्रामेषु] uses @ref[7.3.103] (@deva[अ] → @deva[ए] before @deva[झल्] in plural).

## concept - What's Next

You've mastered the a-stem declension — the largest and most important noun pattern in Sanskrit. Every other declension is learned by comparison to this one.

**Next up: @deva[आ]-stems** (feminine nouns like @deva[सेना] "army," @deva[विद्या] "knowledge," @deva[कन्या] "girl"). You'll see that:

- Cases 3-7 follow the same patterns as a-stems with minor adjustments
- The Nominative and Accusative have their own distinctive forms
- New rules like @ref[7.3.106] handle the Vocative
- The @deva[आ]-stem is the primary feminine pattern, just as the @deva[अ]-stem is the primary masculine/neuter pattern

Together, a-stems and @deva[आ]-stems account for the vast majority of noun forms you will encounter in classical Sanskrit texts.
