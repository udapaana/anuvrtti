## concept - Sanskrit Compounds (@deva[समास])

Compounding is one of the most distinctive features of Sanskrit. Two or more words combine into a single unit with case endings dropped internally. Sanskrit compounds can be extremely long — an entire sentence can become a single word.

**The four major compound types:**

| Type             | Sanskrit      | Head          | Meaning pattern    | Example                                    |
| ---------------- | ------------- | ------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| @deva[तत्पुरुष]  | Determinative | second member | "X's Y", "Y of X"  | @deva[राजपुरुषः] "king's man"              |
| @deva[बहुव्रीहि] | Possessive    | external      | "having X-Y"       | @deva[नीलकण्ठः] "blue-necked one" (= Śiva) |
| @deva[द्वन्द्व]  | Copulative    | both          | "X and Y"          | @deva[रामलक्ष्मणौ] "Rāma and Lakṣmaṇa"     |
| @deva[अव्ययीभाव] | Adverbial     | first member  | "in relation to X" | @deva[उपकृष्णम्] "near Kṛṣṇa"              |

Additional sub-types: @deva[कर्मधारय] (appositional tatpuruṣa: @deva[नीलोत्पलम्] "blue lotus"), @deva[दिगु] (numeral: @deva[पञ्चगवम्] "five cows"), @deva[नञ्तत्पुरुष] (negative: @deva[अब्राह्मणः] "non-Brahmin").

---

## @ref[2.1.4] - सह सुपा

**Key Terms:** @deva[सह], @deva[सुप्], @deva[समास]

The words @deva[सह सुपा] ("with a case-inflected word") govern the succeeding compound rules. This establishes the fundamental principle: compounds are formed from words that carry case relations.

Before compounding:

- @deva[राज्ञः पुरुषः] "the man of the king" (two separate words, genitive relation)

After compounding:

- @deva[राजपुरुषः] "the king's man" (single compound, case ending dropped)

The case relation is understood but not overtly expressed in the compound.

## @ref[2.1.22] - तत्पुरुषः

**Key Terms:** @deva[तत्पुरुष]

This @deva[अधिकार] establishes @deva[तत्पुरुष] as the name for determinative compounds from here through @ref[2.2.23]. In @deva[तत्पुरुष], the **second** member is primary (the "head") and the first member qualifies it.

**Sub-types by case relation:**

| Case of first member | Example           | Analysis             | Meaning             |
| -------------------- | ----------------- | -------------------- | ------------------- |
| Accusative (2nd)     | @deva[ग्रामगतः]   | @deva[ग्रामं गतः]    | gone to the village |
| Instrumental (3rd)   | @deva[मातृसदृशः]  | @deva[मात्रा सदृशः]  | similar to mother   |
| Dative (4th)         | @deva[यूपदारु]    | @deva[यूपाय दारु]    | wood for the post   |
| Ablative (5th)       | @deva[चोरभयम्]    | @deva[चोरात् भयम्]   | fear from a thief   |
| Genitive (6th)       | @deva[राजपुरुषः]  | @deva[राज्ञः पुरुषः] | the king's man      |
| Locative (7th)       | @deva[कूपमण्डूकः] | @deva[कूपे मण्डूकः]  | a frog in a well    |

## @ref[2.2.6] - नञ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[नञ्], @deva[तत्पुरुष]

The negative particle @deva[नञ्] compounds with a case-inflected word to form a @deva[तत्पुरुष]:

| Positive         | Negative compound | Meaning         |
| ---------------- | ----------------- | --------------- |
| @deva[ब्राह्मणः] | @deva[अब्राह्मणः] | non-Brahmin     |
| @deva[धर्मः]     | @deva[अधर्मः]     | unrighteousness |
| @deva[सत्यम्]    | @deva[असत्यम्]    | untruth         |
| @deva[विद्या]    | @deva[अविद्या]    | ignorance       |
| @deva[ज्ञानम्]   | @deva[अज्ञानम्]   | non-knowledge   |
| @deva[हिंसा]     | @deva[अहिंसा]     | non-violence    |

Before a vowel, @deva[नञ्] → @deva[अन्]: @deva[अनर्थः] "meaningless," @deva[अनाचारः] "misconduct."

## @ref[2.2.24] - अनेकमन्यपदार्थे

**Key Terms:** @deva[अनेक], @deva[अन्यपदार्थ], @deva[बहुव्रीहि]

Two or more words compound to denote something **other than** what either member individually means — this is @deva[बहुव्रीहि] (possessive/exocentric compound). The compound describes an external entity that "has" the quality expressed.

**Examples:**

| Compound           | Members                     | Literal        | Actual meaning                |
| ------------------ | --------------------------- | -------------- | ----------------------------- |
| @deva[नीलकण्ठः]    | @deva[नील] + @deva[कण्ठ]    | blue-throat    | Śiva (who has a blue throat)  |
| @deva[दशरथः]       | @deva[दश] + @deva[रथ]       | ten-chariot    | one who has ten chariots      |
| @deva[चतुर्भुजः]   | @deva[चतुर्] + @deva[भुज]   | four-arm       | Viṣṇu (who has four arms)     |
| @deva[महात्मा]     | @deva[महत्] + @deva[आत्मन्] | great-self     | great-souled one              |
| @deva[पीताम्बरः]   | @deva[पीत] + @deva[अम्बर]   | yellow-garment | one wearing yellow (= Viṣṇu)  |
| @deva[प्राप्तोदकः] | @deva[प्राप्त] + @deva[उदक] | arrived-water  | (village) to which water came |

The key test: if the compound refers to something **outside** its members, it is @deva[बहुव्रीहि].

## @ref[2.2.29] - चार्थे द्वन्द्वः

**Key Terms:** @deva[च], @deva[अर्थ], @deva[द्वन्द्व]

When words stand in a relation expressible by "and" (@deva[च]), they form a @deva[द्वन्द्व] (copulative compound).

**Types of @deva[द्वन्द्व]:**

| Type             | Description     | Example                       | Meaning                     |
| ---------------- | --------------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| @deva[इतरेतरयोग] | each individual | @deva[रामलक्ष्मणौ] (dual)     | Rāma and Lakṣmaṇa           |
| @deva[समाहार]    | collective      | @deva[पाणिपादम्] (neuter sg.) | hands and feet (as a group) |

**@deva[इतरेतरयोग] examples:**

- @deva[रामकृष्णौ] "Rāma and Kṛṣṇa" (dual, two people)
- @deva[धर्मार्थकामाः] "dharma, artha, and kāma" (plural, three things)
- @deva[देवासुराः] "gods and demons" (plural)

**@deva[समाहार] examples:**

- @deva[अहोरात्रम्] "day-and-night" (as a unit, neuter singular)
- @deva[पाणिपादम्] "hands-and-feet" (collective)

## @ref[2.1.6] - अव्ययं विभक्तिसमीपसमृद्धि...

**Key Terms:** @deva[अव्ययीभाव]

An @deva[अव्यय] (indeclinable) forms a compound in which the **first** member is head — unique among Sanskrit compounds. The result is always neuter singular and functions as an adverb.

| Compound          | Analysis                  | Meaning              |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------- |
| @deva[उपकृष्णम्]  | @deva[उप] + @deva[कृष्ण]  | near Kṛṣṇa           |
| @deva[यथाशक्ति]   | @deva[यथा] + @deva[शक्ति] | according to ability |
| @deva[प्रतिदिनम्] | @deva[प्रति] + @deva[दिन] | every day            |
| @deva[अनुरूपम्]   | @deva[अनु] + @deva[रूप]   | according to form    |
| @deva[निर्जलम्]   | @deva[निस्] + @deva[जल]   | without water        |

---

## @ref[2.1.57] - विशेषणं विशेष्येण बहुलम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[विशेषण], @deva[विशेष्य], @deva[कर्मधारय]

An adjective optionally compounds with its substantive — this is @deva[कर्मधारय] (appositional @deva[तत्पुरुष]). Both members refer to the same entity and share the same case:

| Compound          | Members                   | Meaning      |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- | ------------ |
| @deva[नीलोत्पलम्] | @deva[नील] + @deva[उत्पल] | blue lotus   |
| @deva[महाराजः]    | @deva[महा] + @deva[राज]   | great king   |
| @deva[प्रियसखा]   | @deva[प्रिय] + @deva[सखा] | dear friend  |
| @deva[परमेश्वरः]  | @deva[परम] + @deva[ईश्वर] | supreme lord |

---

## @ref[2.1.61] - सन्महत्परमोत्तमोत्कृष्टाः पूज्यमानैः

**Key Terms:** @deva[सत्], @deva[महत्], @deva[दिगु]

Words like @deva[सत्], @deva[महत्], @deva[परम] compound with honorific nouns. Related: @deva[दिगु] — compounds with a numeral as first member:

| @deva[दिगु] compound | Meaning            |
| -------------------- | ------------------ |
| @deva[पञ्चगवम्]      | group of five cows |
| @deva[त्रिलोकी]      | the three worlds   |
| @deva[सप्तर्षयः]     | the seven seers    |
| @deva[चतुर्युगम्]    | the four ages      |

---

## @ref[2.4.71] - सुपो धातुप्रातिपदिकयोः

**Key Terms:** @deva[सुप्], @deva[धातु], @deva[लुक्]

There is @deva[लुक्] (deletion) of the case-affix when a word enters a compound. This is the mechanical rule that drops the case endings:

- @deva[राज्ञः] (Gen.) + @deva[पुरुषः] → @deva[राज] + @deva[पुरुष] → @deva[राजपुरुषः]
- @deva[नीलम्] (Nom.) + @deva[उत्पलम्] → @deva[नील] + @deva[उत्पल] → @deva[नीलोत्पलम्]

Only the last member retains its case ending (reflecting the compound's role in the sentence).

---

## @ref[6.2.1] - बहुव्रीहौ प्रकृत्या पूर्वपदम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[बहुव्रीहि], @deva[पूर्वपद]

In @deva[बहुव्रीहि], the first member retains its original form (accent, etc.). This preservation rule ensures the first member doesn't undergo unwanted modifications.

## concept - Identifying Compound Types

When reading, use this decision tree:

1. Does the compound mean "X **and** Y"? → @deva[द्वन्द्व]
2. Is the first member an indeclinable? → @deva[अव्ययीभाव]
3. Does the compound describe something **outside** its members ("one who has...")? → @deva[बहुव्रीहि]
4. Is the second member primary? → @deva[तत्पुरुष] (or @deva[कर्मधारय] if appositional)

**Practice — classify these compounds:**

| Compound         | Type             | Reasoning                                        |
| ---------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| @deva[राजपुत्रः] | @deva[तत्पुरुष]  | "king's son" — second member primary             |
| @deva[लम्बकर्णः] | @deva[बहुव्रीहि] | "long-eared one" — describes an external entity  |
| @deva[सीतारामौ]  | @deva[द्वन्द्व]  | "Sītā and Rāma"                                  |
| @deva[यथाविधि]   | @deva[अव्ययीभाव] | "according to rule" — @deva[यथा] is indeclinable |
| @deva[घनश्यामः]  | @deva[कर्मधारय]  | "cloud-dark one" — adjective + noun              |
| @deva[चक्रपाणिः] | @deva[बहुव्रीहि] | "disc-handed" — refers to Viṣṇu                  |
