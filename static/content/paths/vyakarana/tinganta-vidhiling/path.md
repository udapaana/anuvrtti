## concept - @deva[विधिलिङ्] — The Optative

@deva[विधिलिङ्] is one of the most versatile moods in Sanskrit, covering commands, invitations, wishes, possibilities, and polite requests. It corresponds roughly to English "should," "would," "may," "let him," or "ought to."

**Paradigm of @deva[भू] (be) — @deva[विधिलिङ्]:**

|     | Singular      | Dual           | Plural        |
| --- | ------------- | -------------- | ------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[भवेत्]  | @deva[भवेताम्] | @deva[भवेयुः] |
| 2nd | @deva[भवेः]   | @deva[भवेतम्]  | @deva[भवेत]   |
| 1st | @deva[भवेयम्] | @deva[भवेव]    | @deva[भवेम]   |

**Paradigm of @deva[कृ] (do) — @deva[विधिलिङ्]:**

|     | Singular        | Dual              | Plural         |
| --- | --------------- | ----------------- | -------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[कुर्यात्] | @deva[कुर्याताम्] | @deva[कुर्युः] |
| 2nd | @deva[कुर्याः]  | @deva[कुर्यातम्]  | @deva[कुर्यात] |
| 1st | @deva[कुर्याम्] | @deva[कुर्याव]    | @deva[कुर्याम] |

**Paradigm of @deva[कृ] (do) — @deva[विधिलिङ्] @deva[आत्मनेपद]:**

|     | Singular         | Dual                | Plural             |
| --- | ---------------- | ------------------- | ------------------ |
| 3rd | @deva[कुर्वीत]   | @deva[कुर्वीयाताम्] | @deva[कुर्वीरन्]   |
| 2nd | @deva[कुर्वीथाः] | @deva[कुर्वीयाथाम्] | @deva[कुर्वीध्वम्] |
| 1st | @deva[कुर्वीय]   | @deva[कुर्वीवहि]    | @deva[कुर्वीमहि]   |

The characteristic marker is @deva[यासुट्] (realized as @deva[-या-] in P. or @deva[-ई-] in Ā.) between the stem and the ending.

## @ref[3.3.161] - विधिनिमन्त्रणामन्त्रणाधीष्टसम्प्रश्नप्रार्थनेषु लिङ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[विधि], @deva[लिङ्], @deva[निमन्त्रण]

@deva[लिङ्] comes after a verb for six functions:

| Function         | Sanskrit    | Meaning          | Example                                      |
| ---------------- | ----------- | ---------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| @deva[विधि]      | command     | "should do"      | @deva[कटं कुर्यात्] "he should make the mat" |
| @deva[निमन्त्रण] | invitation  | "please do"      | @deva[इह भवान् भुञ्जीत] "you may dine here"  |
| @deva[आमन्त्रण]  | permission  | "you may"        | @deva[गच्छेत्] "he may go"                   |
| @deva[अधीष्ट]    | polite wish | "kindly do"      | @deva[पठेत्] "he should study"               |
| @deva[सम्प्रश्न] | question    | "should one...?" | @deva[किं कुर्यात्] "what should he do?"     |
| @deva[प्रार्थना] | prayer      | "may it be"      | @deva[जीवेत्] "may he live"                  |

This wide range makes @deva[विधिलिङ्] the go-to mood for prescriptions, advice, and general statements of duty.

## @ref[3.4.103] - यासुट् परस्मैपदेषूदात्तो ङिच्च

**Key Terms:** @deva[यासुट्], @deva[परस्मैपद], @deva[ङित्]

@deva[यासुट्] augments @deva[परस्मैपद] endings in @deva[लिङ्]. The endings are treated as @deva[ङित्], which prevents @deva[गुण] and @deva[वृद्धि].

The process for @deva[कृ] + @deva[लिङ्] + @deva[तिप्]:

1. @deva[कृ] + @deva[शप्] (= @deva[अ]) → not applicable (@deva[कृ] is 8th class, uses @deva[उ])
2. @deva[कृ] + @deva[उ] → @deva[कुरु] (by @deva[सम्प्रसारण])
3. - @deva[यासुट्] + @deva[त्] → @deva[कुर्यात्]

The @deva[स्] of @deva[यासुट्] is later deleted by @ref[7.2.79].

## @ref[3.4.116] - लिङाशिषि

**Key Terms:** @deva[लिङ्], @deva[आशिष्], @deva[आर्धधातुक]

In benediction (@deva[आशीर्लिङ्]), @deva[लिङ्] substitutes are @deva[आर्धधातुक]. This creates a distinct set of forms used exclusively for blessings.

**@deva[विधिलिङ्] vs. @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] comparison:**

| Root        | @deva[विधिलिङ्]             | @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]               | Context  |
| ----------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------------ | -------- |
| @deva[भू]   | @deva[भवेत्] "should be"    | @deva[भूयात्] "may it be!"     | blessing |
| @deva[कृ]   | @deva[कुर्यात्] "should do" | @deva[क्रियात्] "may he do!"   | blessing |
| @deva[जीव्] | @deva[जीवेत्] "should live" | @deva[जीव्यात्] "may he live!" | blessing |

@deva[विधिलिङ्] is @deva[सार्वधातुक] (takes vikaraṇa); @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] is @deva[आर्धधातुक] (no vikaraṇa, takes @deva[इट्]).

---

## @ref[3.4.104] - किदाशिषि

**Key Terms:** @deva[कित्], @deva[आशिष्], @deva[यासुट्]

In benediction, @deva[यासुट्] is treated as @deva[कित्] (having indicatory @deva[क्]). Both @deva[ङित्] (from @ref[3.4.103]) and @deva[कित्] block @deva[गुण] and @deva[वृद्धि], but they differ in other effects.

- In @deva[विधिलिङ्]: @deva[वच्] → @deva[वच्यात्] (no @deva[सम्प्रसारण] because not @deva[कित्])
- In @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]: @deva[वच्] → @deva[उच्यात्] (@deva[सम्प्रसारण] applies because @deva[कित्])

---

## @ref[3.4.105] - झस्य रन्

**Key Terms:** @deva[झ], @deva[रन्], @deva[लिङ्]

@deva[रन्] replaces @deva[झ] (3rd plural @deva[आत्मनेपद]) in @deva[लिङ्]. Normally @deva[झ] becomes @deva[अन्त] (by @ref[7.1.3]), but in @deva[लिङ्] it becomes @deva[रन्]:

- @deva[पचेरन्] (not \*@deva[पचेयान्त])
- @deva[कुर्वीरन्]
- @deva[सेवेरन्]

This is a special replacement specific to the optative mood.

---

## @ref[3.4.106] - इटोऽत्

**Key Terms:** @deva[इट्], @deva[अत्], @deva[आत्मनेपद]

Short @deva[अ] replaces @deva[इ] of @deva[आत्मनेपद] 1st singular in @deva[लिङ्]:

- @deva[पचेय] (not \*@deva[पचेयि])
- @deva[कुर्वीय]
- @deva[सेवेय]

---

## @ref[7.2.79] - लिङः सलोपोऽनन्त्यस्य

**Key Terms:** @deva[लिङ्], @deva[सलोप], @deva[अनन्त्य]

In @deva[सार्वधातुक] @deva[लिङ्] (@deva[विधिलिङ्]), non-final @deva[स्] (from @deva[यासुट्]) is elided.

@deva[यासुट्] = @deva[यास्]. The @deva[स्] is non-final, so it drops:

- @deva[कृ] + @deva[उ] + @deva[यास्] + @deva[त्] → @deva[कुर्यात्] (non-final @deva[स्] drops)
- But @deva[कृ] + @deva[उ] + @deva[यास्] + @deva[उस्] → @deva[कुर्युः] (final @deva[स्] remains in @deva[उस्])

In @deva[आशीर्लिङ्], @deva[स्] is retained: @deva[क्रियास्ताम्].

---

## @ref[7.1.5] - आत्मनेपदेष्वनतः

**Key Terms:** @deva[आत्मनेपद], @deva[अनतः]

@deva[आत्मनेपद] endings not ending in @deva[अ] receive the @deva[सीयुट्] augment in @deva[विधिलिङ्], producing the @deva[-ई-] marker:

- @deva[कुर्वीत] (3rd sg. Ā.)
- @deva[कुर्वीथाः] (2nd sg. Ā.)
- @deva[कुर्वीय] (1st sg. Ā.)

## concept - @deva[विधिलिङ्] in Literature

The optative is pervasive in Sanskrit literature, especially in:

**1. Legal/prescriptive texts (@deva[धर्मशास्त्र]):**

- @deva[सत्यं ब्रूयात्] "One should speak truth" (Manu)
- @deva[अहिंसा परमो धर्मः — न हिंस्यात् सर्वभूतानि] "One should not harm any beings"

**2. Philosophical discourse:**

- @deva[आत्मानं विद्यात्] "One should know the Self"
- @deva[ब्रह्म जिज्ञासेत] "One should inquire into Brahman"

**3. Conditional clauses (@deva[यदि]...@deva[तर्हि]):**

- @deva[यदि गच्छेत् तर्हि पश्येत्] "If he were to go, he would see"
- @deva[यदि पठेत् तर्हि जानीयात्] "If he studied, he would know"

**4. General truths with @deva[सदा] / @deva[नित्यम्]:**

- @deva[धर्मं चरेत्] "One should practice dharma"
- @deva[सत्सङ्गं सेवेत] "One should keep good company"

**Common optative forms:**

| Form            | Root        | Meaning           |
| --------------- | ----------- | ----------------- |
| @deva[भवेत्]    | @deva[भू]   | should be, may be |
| @deva[कुर्यात्] | @deva[कृ]   | should do         |
| @deva[गच्छेत्]  | @deva[गम्]  | should go         |
| @deva[पठेत्]    | @deva[पठ्]  | should read       |
| @deva[ब्रूयात्] | @deva[ब्रू] | should say        |
| @deva[जानीयात्] | @deva[ज्ञा] | should know       |
| @deva[दद्यात्]  | @deva[दा]   | should give       |
| @deva[तिष्ठेत्] | @deva[स्था] | should stand      |
| @deva[पश्येत्]  | @deva[दृश्] | should see        |
| @deva[सेवेत]    | @deva[सेव्] | should serve (Ā.) |
