## concept - The Aorist (@deva[लुङ्])

The aorist (@deva[लुङ्]) is the general past tense, referring to action in past time without specifying when. It is the most complex @deva[लकार] because **seven different formation types** exist, each applying to different root classes.

**The seven aorist types:**

| Type | Name                          | Marker         | Example          | From                |
| ---- | ----------------------------- | -------------- | ---------------- | ------------------- |
| 1    | Root (@deva[लुक्])            | no @deva[सिच्] | @deva[अदात्]     | @deva[दा] "give"    |
| 2    | @deva[अ]-type (@deva[अङ्])    | @deva[अ]       | @deva[अगमत्]     | @deva[गम्] "go"     |
| 3    | Reduplicated (@deva[चङ्])     | reduplication  | @deva[अजीगमत्]   | @deva[गम्] (causal) |
| 4    | @deva[स]-aorist (@deva[सिच्]) | @deva[स्]      | @deva[अकार्षीत्] | @deva[कृ] "do"      |
| 5    | @deva[इष्]-aorist             | @deva[इ + स्]  | @deva[अपाविषुः]  | @deva[पू] "purify"  |
| 6    | @deva[सिष्]-aorist            | @deva[सि + स्] | @deva[अयासिषम्]  | @deva[या] "go"      |
| 7    | @deva[स]-aorist (Ā.)          | @deva[स्]      | @deva[अलाविषि]   | @deva[लू] "cut"     |

**Paradigm of @deva[भू] (be) — Root aorist (@deva[लुङ्]):**

|     | Singular      | Dual           | Plural        |
| --- | ------------- | -------------- | ------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[अभूत्]  | @deva[अभूताम्] | @deva[अभूवन्] |
| 2nd | @deva[अभूः]   | @deva[अभूतम्]  | @deva[अभूत]   |
| 1st | @deva[अभूवम्] | @deva[अभूव]    | @deva[अभूम]   |

**Paradigm of @deva[कृ] (do) — @deva[सिच्]-aorist:**

|     | Singular         | Dual               | Plural          |
| --- | ---------------- | ------------------ | --------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[अकार्षीत्] | @deva[अकार्ष्टाम्] | @deva[अकार्षुः] |
| 2nd | @deva[अकार्षीः]  | @deva[अकार्ष्टम्]  | @deva[अकार्ष्ट] |
| 1st | @deva[अकार्षम्]  | @deva[अकार्ष्व]    | @deva[अकार्ष्म] |

## @ref[3.2.110] - लुङ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[लुङ्], @deva[भूत]

The affix @deva[लुङ्] comes after the verb in the sense of past time. The aorist refers to past action generally, without specifying whether it was witnessed or remote.

**Comparison of past tenses:**

| @deva[लकार] | Name      | Usage                    |
| ----------- | --------- | ------------------------ |
| @deva[लङ्]  | Imperfect | general past, narrative  |
| @deva[लिट्] | Perfect   | remote past, unwitnessed |
| @deva[लुङ्] | Aorist    | indefinite past          |

In Classical Sanskrit, the three past tenses are often used interchangeably, but in Vedic they have distinct functions.

## @ref[6.4.71] - लुङ्लङ्लृङ्क्ष्वडुदात्तः

**Key Terms:** @deva[अट्], @deva[लुङ्]

@deva[अट्] (= @deva[अ]) prefixes the verbal stem in the aorist (and imperfect and conditional). All aorist forms begin with this augment:

- @deva[अ] + @deva[भूत्] → @deva[अभूत्] "he was/became"
- @deva[अ] + @deva[कार्षीत्] → @deva[अकार्षीत्] "he did"
- @deva[अ] + @deva[गमत्] → @deva[अगमत्] "he went"

---

## @ref[3.1.43] - च्लि लुङि

**Key Terms:** @deva[च्लि], @deva[लुङ्]

When @deva[लुङ्] follows, the affix @deva[च्लि] is added to the root. @deva[च्लि] is an abstract placeholder that gets replaced by the specific aorist marker for each root type.

The @deva[च्लि] → specific marker substitution is what creates the seven different aorist types.

---

## @ref[3.1.44] - च्लेः सिच्

**Key Terms:** @deva[च्लि], @deva[सिच्]

@deva[सिच्] (realized as @deva[स्]) substitutes for @deva[च्लि] as the default. This creates the **sigmatic aorist** — the most common type:

- @deva[कृ] + @deva[सिच्] → @deva[अ] + @deva[कार्] + @deva[स्] + @deva[ईत्] → @deva[अकार्षीत्]
- @deva[नी] + @deva[सिच्] → @deva[अ] + @deva[नै] + @deva[ष्] + @deva[ईत्] → @deva[अनैषीत्]
- @deva[भिद्] + @deva[सिच्] → @deva[अ] + @deva[भैत्] + @deva[स्] + @deva[ईत्] → @deva[अभैत्सीत्]

The @deva[स्] of @deva[सिच्] is the "sigma" (= s) that gives this aorist its name.

---

## @ref[7.2.1] - सिचि वृद्धिः परस्मैपदेषु

**Key Terms:** @deva[सिच्], @deva[वृद्धि], @deva[परस्मैपद]

Before @deva[सिच्] in @deva[परस्मैपद], @deva[वृद्धि] substitutes for the final @deva[इक्] vowel:

| Root      | @deva[गुण] | @deva[वृद्धि] (aorist) | Aorist form           |
| --------- | ---------- | ---------------------- | --------------------- |
| @deva[चि] | @deva[चे]  | @deva[चै]              | @deva[अचैषीत्]        |
| @deva[नी] | @deva[ने]  | @deva[नै]              | @deva[अनैषीत्]        |
| @deva[कृ] | @deva[कर्] | @deva[कार्]            | @deva[अकार्षीत्]      |
| @deva[भू] | @deva[भो]  | @deva[भौ]              | (root aorist instead) |

---

## @ref[2.4.77] - गातिस्थाघुपाभूभ्यः सिचः परस्मैपदेषु

**Key Terms:** @deva[सिच्], @deva[लुक्], @deva[गा]

@deva[सिच्] is **deleted** (@deva[लुक्]) in @deva[परस्मैपद] after @deva[गा], @deva[स्था], @deva[घु]-verbs (@deva[दा], @deva[धा]), @deva[पा], and @deva[भू]. This produces the **root aorist** (Type 1):

| Root        | Aorist 3rd sg. | Meaning   |
| ----------- | -------------- | --------- |
| @deva[भू]   | @deva[अभूत्]   | he became |
| @deva[दा]   | @deva[अदात्]   | he gave   |
| @deva[धा]   | @deva[अधात्]   | he placed |
| @deva[स्था] | @deva[अस्थात्] | he stood  |
| @deva[गा]   | @deva[अगात्]   | he went   |
| @deva[पा]   | @deva[अपात्]   | he drank  |

These are among the most common aorist forms in the language.

---

## @ref[3.1.45] - शल इगुपधादनिटः क्सः

**Key Terms:** @deva[क्स], @deva[शल्], @deva[इक्]

After a verb ending in @deva[शल्] (= @deva[श], @deva[ष], @deva[स], @deva[ह]) with @deva[इक्] penultimate and @deva[अनिट्], @deva[क्स] substitutes for @deva[च्लि]. This is the @deva[क्स]-aorist:

- @deva[दिश्] → @deva[अदिक्षत्]
- @deva[लिह्] → @deva[अलिक्षत्]

---

## @ref[3.1.48] - णिश्रिद्रुस्रुभ्यः कर्तरि चङ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[चङ्], @deva[णि], @deva[श्रि]

After causative (@deva[णि]) roots and after @deva[श्रि], @deva[द्रु], @deva[स्रु], @deva[चङ्] substitutes for @deva[च्लि]. This creates the **reduplicated aorist** (Type 3):

- @deva[गम्] (causal) → @deva[अजीगमत्] "he made go"
- @deva[पठ्] (causal) → @deva[अपीपठत्] "he made read"
- @deva[कृ] (causal) → @deva[अचीकरत्] "he made do"

The @deva[ङ्] of @deva[चङ्] prevents @deva[गुण] and @deva[वृद्धि].

---

## @ref[3.1.52] - अस्यतिवक्तिख्यातिभ्योऽङ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[अङ्], @deva[अस्], @deva[वच्]

After @deva[अस्] "to throw," @deva[वच्] "to speak," and @deva[ख्या] "to tell," @deva[अङ्] substitutes for @deva[च्लि]:

- @deva[वच्] → @deva[अवोचत्] "he spoke"

---

## @ref[3.1.55] - पुषादिद्युताद्यॢदितः परस्मैपदेषु

**Key Terms:** @deva[अङ्], @deva[पुष्], @deva[द्युत्]

@deva[अङ्] also substitutes after @deva[पुष्]-class, @deva[द्युत्]-class, and @deva[ऌदित्] roots in @deva[परस्मैपद]. This creates the **@deva[अ]-aorist** (Type 2):

- @deva[गम्] → @deva[अगमत्] "he went"
- @deva[पुष्] → @deva[अपुषत्] "he nourished"
- @deva[द्युत्] → @deva[अद्युतत्] "he shone"

---

## @ref[7.3.96] - अस्तिसिचोऽपृक्ते

**Key Terms:** @deva[अस्ति], @deva[सिच्], @deva[ईट्]

A single-consonant @deva[सार्वधातुक] affix gets the augment @deva[ईट्] after @deva[अस्] and after @deva[सिच्]. This produces the characteristic @deva[-ईत्] ending of the sigmatic aorist:

- @deva[अकार्ष्] + @deva[ईत्] → @deva[अकार्षीत्]
- @deva[अनैष्] + @deva[ईत्] → @deva[अनैषीत्]

---

## @ref[6.4.104] - चिणो लुक्

**Key Terms:** @deva[चिण्], @deva[लुक्]

Personal endings are deleted after @deva[चिण्] — the passive aorist 3rd singular. This creates the distinctive passive aorist form:

- @deva[अकारि] "it was done" (from @deva[कृ])
- @deva[अहारि] "it was taken" (from @deva[हृ])
- @deva[अपाचि] "it was cooked" (from @deva[पच्])
- @deva[अज्ञायि] "it was known" (from @deva[ज्ञा])
