## concept - The Vocabulary of Grammar

The first @term[pāda] of the Aṣṭādhyāyī (1.1.1–75) is primarily a **vocabulary lesson**. Before Pāṇini can state his operational rules, he must define his terms. What exactly is @term[guṇa]? What counts as @term[savarṇa]? When is something @term[avyaya]?

These definitions are called @deva[संज्ञा] — **technical names**. They create a precise metalanguage where every term has an exact, unambiguous meaning. Without this vocabulary, the grammar cannot function.

This @term[prakaraṇa] walks through the major @term[saṃjñā] categories in 1.1, showing how Pāṇini builds his terminological foundation.

---

## concept - The Opening Definitions: Vowel Grades

The Aṣṭādhyāyī famously opens not with a rule about language, but with definitions of vowel grades.

---

## 1.1.1 - @deva[वृद्धिरादैच्]

**Key Terms:** vṛddhi, āt, aic

@deva[वृद्धिः आत् ऐच्] — The sounds @deva[आ], @deva[ऐ], and @deva[औ] are called @term[vṛddhi].

@sutra[1.1.1]

This is the very first rule. @term[vṛddhi] ("growth, increase") names the strongest vowel grade:

| Short | @term[guṇa] | @term[vṛddhi] |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| @deva[अ] | @deva[अ] | @deva[आ] |
| @deva[इ/ई] | @deva[ए] | @deva[ऐ] |
| @deva[उ/ऊ] | @deva[ओ] | @deva[औ] |
| @deva[ऋ/ॠ] | @deva[अर्] | @deva[आर्] |

@term[vṛddhi] appears in causatives (@deva[भू] → @deva[भावयति]), certain derivations, and specific rule environments.

---

## 1.1.2 - @deva[अदेङ् गुणः]

**Key Terms:** guṇa, at, eṅ

@deva[अत् एङ् गुणः] — The sounds @deva[अ], @deva[ए], and @deva[ओ] are called @term[guṇa].

@sutra[1.1.2]

@term[guṇa] ("quality, attribute") names the middle vowel grade. It's the most common strengthening:

- @deva[नी] + @deva[अ] → @deva[नय] (root vowel takes @term[guṇa])
- @deva[भू] + @deva[अ] → @deva[भव] (root vowel takes @term[guṇa])

The relationship: @pratyahara[ik] vowels (@deva[इ उ ऋ ऌ]) strengthen to @term[guṇa] or @term[vṛddhi] depending on the triggering affix.

---

## 1.1.3 - @deva[इको गुणवृद्धी]

**Key Terms:** ik, guṇa, vṛddhi

@deva[इकः गुणवृद्धी] — @term[guṇa] and @term[vṛddhi] replace @pratyahara[ik] vowels.

@sutra[1.1.3]

This crucial @term[paribhāṣā] (interpretive rule) tells us: when the grammar says "let there be @term[guṇa]" or "let there be @term[vṛddhi]", these replace @pratyahara[ik] vowels (@deva[इ उ ऋ ऌ]) specifically.

The vowel @deva[अ] doesn't change to @term[guṇa] — it already is @term[guṇa]. So @deva[पठ्] + @term[guṇa] environment = @deva[पठ्] (no change), but @deva[लिख्] + @term[guṇa] environment = @deva[लेख्].

---

## 1.1.5 - @deva[क्ङिति च]

**Key Terms:** kit, ṅit

@deva[क्ङिति च] — [Guṇa and vṛddhi do NOT apply] when the affix is @term[kit] or @term[ṅit].

@sutra[1.1.5]

This restriction (continuing from 1.1.4) is why @deva[कृ] + @deva[क्त] = @deva[कृत] (not *@deva[कर्त]). The @deva[क्] of @deva[क्त] is @term[it], making the affix @term[kit]. @term[kit] and @term[ṅit] affixes block vowel strengthening.

This single rule governs thousands of forms. It's why past participles often keep the root vowel unchanged.

---

## concept - Sound Classifications

After vowel grades, Pāṇini defines key phonetic categories.

---

## 1.1.7 - @deva[हलोऽनन्तराः संयोगः]

**Key Terms:** hal, anantara, saṃyoga

@deva[हलः अनन्तराः संयोगः] — Consecutive consonants (@deva[हल्] without intervening vowels) are called @term[saṃyoga].

@sutra[1.1.7]

A @term[saṃyoga] (consonant cluster) is any sequence like @deva[स्त्र], @deva[क्ष], @deva[ङ्क्त]. This matters for rules about syllable weight, consonant behavior, and sandhi.

---

## 1.1.8 - @deva[मुखनासिकावचनोऽनुनासिकः]

**Key Terms:** mukha, nāsikā, anunāsika

@deva[मुखनासिकावचनः अनुनासिकः] — A sound produced through both mouth and nose is @term[anunāsika].

@sutra[1.1.8]

@term[anunāsika] sounds are nasalized vowels (like the @deva[अँ] in some Vedic texts). This is distinct from @deva[अनुस्वार] (the @deva[ं]). @term[anunāsika] vowels serve as markers in the @term[Dhātupāṭha].

---

## 1.1.9 - @deva[तुल्यास्यप्रयत्नं सवर्णम्]

**Key Terms:** tulya, āsya, prayatna, savarṇa

@deva[तुल्यास्यप्रयत्नम् सवर्णम्] — Sounds with the same place of articulation (@term[āsyaprayatna]) are @term[savarṇa].

@sutra[1.1.9]

@term[savarṇa] ("same class") groups sounds by articulation point:

| Place | Vowels | Consonants |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Guttural | @deva[अ आ] | @deva[क ख ग घ ङ] |
| Palatal | @deva[इ ई] | @deva[च छ ज झ ञ] |
| Retroflex | @deva[ऋ ॠ] | @deva[ट ठ ड ढ ण] |
| Dental | @deva[ऌ] | @deva[त थ द ध न] |
| Labial | @deva[उ ऊ] | @deva[प फ ब भ म] |

This classification drives sandhi rules — @term[savarṇa] sounds interact in specific ways.

---

## 1.1.10 - @deva[नाज्झलौ]

**Key Terms:** ac, hal

@deva[न अच् हलौ] — A vowel and a consonant are NOT @term[savarṇa] [with each other].

@sutra[1.1.10]

This exception to 1.1.9 clarifies: even if @deva[अ] and @deva[क] share the guttural place, they're not @term[savarṇa] because one is a vowel and one is a consonant.

---

## concept - The Pragṛhya Exception

Rules 1.1.11–19 define @term[pragṛhya] — vowels that don't undergo sandhi.

---

## 1.1.11 - @deva[ईदूदेद्द्विवचनं प्रगृह्यम्]

**Key Terms:** pragṛhya, dvivacana

@deva[ईत् ऊत् एत् द्विवचनम् प्रगृह्यम्] — Dual endings in @deva[ई], @deva[ऊ], or @deva[ए] are @term[pragṛhya].

@sutra[1.1.11]

@term[pragṛhya] means "exempt from sandhi." The dual endings @deva[ई] (as in @deva[कवी] "two poets"), @deva[ऊ] (@deva[गुरू] "two teachers"), and @deva[ए] (@deva[वने] "in two forests") keep their final vowels even before other vowels:

@deva[कवी इमौ] → @deva[कवी इमौ] (not *@deva[कवीमौ])

This preserves grammatical clarity — you can still hear it's dual.

---

## concept - Nominal Classifications

A series of rules define important noun/pronoun categories.

---

## 1.1.20 - @deva[दाधा घ्वदाप्]

**Key Terms:** ghu

@deva[दाधा घु अदाप्] — The roots @deva[दा] and @deva[धा] (except @deva[दाप्]) are called @term[ghu].

@sutra[1.1.20]

The @term[ghu] designation triggers specific behaviors in these common roots, including their treatment before certain affixes.

---

## 1.1.22 - @deva[तरप्तमपौ घः]

**Key Terms:** gh, tarap, tamap

@deva[तरप् तमप् घः] — The suffixes @deva[तरप्] and @deva[तमप्] are called @term[gh].

@sutra[1.1.22]

These comparative and superlative suffixes (@deva[तर] and @deva[तम]) get a special designation because they trigger specific sandhi and accent behaviors.

---

## 1.1.23 - @deva[बहुगणवतुडति संख्या]

**Key Terms:** saṅkhyā

@deva[बहुगणवतुडति संख्या] — @deva[बहु], words with @deva[गण], words with @deva[वतुप्], and @deva[डति] words are called @term[saṅkhyā].

@sutra[1.1.23]

@term[saṅkhyā] (numerals and quantity words) includes:
- @deva[बहु] ("many")
- Words like @deva[पञ्चगण] ("fivefold")
- Words with the @deva[वतुप्] suffix
- Certain pronoun forms

---

## 1.1.26 - @deva[क्तक्तवतू निष्ठा]

**Key Terms:** niṣṭhā, kta, ktavatu

@deva[क्त क्तवतू निष्ठा] — The affixes @deva[क्त] and @deva[क्तवतु] are called @term[niṣṭhā].

@sutra[1.1.26]

@term[niṣṭhā] names the past participle suffixes:
- @deva[क्त] → @deva[त] (as in @deva[कृतम्] "done")
- @deva[क्तवतु] → @deva[तवत्] (as in @deva[कृतवान्] "one who did")

The designation lets other rules target these suffixes collectively.

---

## 1.1.27 - @deva[सर्वादीनि सर्वनामानि]

**Key Terms:** sarvanāman

@deva[सर्वादीनि सर्वनामानि] — @deva[सर्व] and the words following it [in the gaṇa] are called @term[sarvanāman].

@sutra[1.1.27]

The @term[sarvanāman] class includes pronouns and pronominal adjectives:
- @deva[सर्व] ("all"), @deva[विश्व] ("every"), @deva[उभ] ("both")
- @deva[तद्], @deva[एतद्], @deva[इदम्], @deva[अदस्] (demonstratives)
- @deva[यद्], @deva[किम्] (interrogative/relative)

These words take special endings in certain cases (the @term[sarvanāmasthāna] endings).

---

## concept - The Avyaya Class

Rules 1.1.37–41 define indeclinables.

---

## 1.1.37 - @deva[स्वरादिनिपातमव्ययम्]

**Key Terms:** avyaya, nipāta, svara

@deva[स्वरादिनिपातम् अव्ययम्] — @deva[स्वर्] and following items in the @term[nipāta] list are @term[avyaya].

@sutra[1.1.37]

@term[avyaya] ("indeclinable") words don't take case endings:
- @deva[स्वर्] ("heaven")
- Particles like @deva[च], @deva[वा], @deva[ह]
- Adverbs and conjunctions

---

## 1.1.38 - @deva[तद्धितश्चासर्वविभक्तिः]

**Key Terms:** taddhita, avyaya

@deva[तद्धितः च असर्वविभक्तिः] — A @term[taddhita] derivative that doesn't take all cases is @term[avyaya].

@sutra[1.1.38]

Some @term[taddhita] suffixes create indeclinables. For example, @deva[तत्र] ("there") from @deva[तद्] + @deva[त्रल्] doesn't decline — it's @term[avyaya].

---

## 1.1.40 - @deva[क्त्वातोसुन्कसुनः]

**Key Terms:** ktvā, tosun, kasun, avyaya

@deva[क्त्वा तोसुन् कसुन्] — [Words ending in] @deva[क्त्वा], @deva[तोसुन्], and @deva[कसुन्] are @term[avyaya].

@sutra[1.1.40]

This makes absolutives (@deva[कृत्वा] "having done", @deva[गत्वा] "having gone") indeclinable. The @deva[क्त्वा] suffix produces a fixed form.

---

## concept - Technical Terms for Operations

The next group defines terms used in rule operations.

---

## 1.1.44 - @deva[न वेति विभाषा]

**Key Terms:** vibhāṣā

@deva[न वा इति विभाषा] — [When a rule uses] @deva[न] ("not") or @deva[वा] ("or"), it indicates @term[vibhāṣā].

@sutra[1.1.44]

@term[vibhāṣā] means "optionality." When Pāṇini says a rule applies @deva[वा], both the changed and unchanged forms are correct.

---

## 1.1.45 - @deva[इग्यणः सम्प्रसारणम्]

**Key Terms:** samprasāraṇa, ik, yaṇ

@deva[इक् यणः सम्प्रसारणम्] — The replacement of @pratyahara[yaṇ] by @pratyahara[ik] is called @term[samprasāraṇa].

@sutra[1.1.45]

@term[samprasāraṇa] is when semivowels become their corresponding vowels:
- @deva[य्] → @deva[इ]
- @deva[व्] → @deva[उ]
- @deva[र्] → @deva[ऋ]
- @deva[ल्] → @deva[ऌ]

This occurs in certain root modifications, like @deva[वच्] → @deva[उक्त] (the @deva[व्] becomes @deva[उ]).

---

## concept - Paribhāṣā Rules: How to Interpret

Rules 1.1.46–55 are crucial @term[paribhāṣā] — they tell us HOW to interpret other rules.

---

## 1.1.46 - @deva[आद्यन्तौ टकितौ]

**Key Terms:** ādi, anta, ṭit, kit, āgama

@deva[आद्यन्तौ टकितौ] — A @term[ṭit] augment attaches at the beginning; a @term[kit] augment attaches at the end.

@sutra[1.1.46]

When the grammar adds an @term[āgama] (augment):
- If the augment is @term[ṭit] (has @deva[ट्] as @term[it]), it goes at the START
- If @term[kit] (has @deva[क्] as @term[it]), it goes at the END

Example: @deva[अट्] (the past tense augment) is @term[ṭit], so it attaches at the beginning: @deva[अट्] + @deva[भवत्] → @deva[अभवत्].

---

## 1.1.47 - @deva[मिदचोऽन्त्यात्परः]

**Key Terms:** mit, ac

@deva[मित् अचः अन्त्यात् परः] — A @term[mit] augment follows the last vowel.

@sutra[1.1.47]

Augments marked with @deva[म्] as @term[it] insert after the final vowel. This is how @deva[नुम्] works: @deva[भुज्] + @deva[नुम्] → @deva[भुनज्] (the @deva[न] inserts after @deva[उ]).

---

## 1.1.49 - @deva[षष्ठी स्थानेयोगा]

**Key Terms:** ṣaṣṭhī, sthānin

@deva[षष्ठी स्थानेयोगा] — The genitive case indicates "in the place of" [when specifying substitution].

@sutra[1.1.49]

When a rule says "X-genitive becomes Y", it means "Y replaces X." This interpretive rule governs how substitution rules are read throughout the grammar.

---

## 1.1.50 - @deva[स्थानेऽन्तरतमः]

**Key Terms:** sthāna, antaratama

@deva[स्थाने अन्तरतमः] — In substitution, the closest [sound] applies.

@sutra[1.1.50]

When multiple substitutes are possible, choose the one most similar to the original. This prevents arbitrary choices in sandhi and other operations.

---

## 1.1.52 - @deva[अलोऽन्त्यस्य]

**Key Terms:** al, antya, ādeśa

@deva[अलः अन्त्यस्य] — A single-sound substitute replaces the final [sound of the original].

@sutra[1.1.52]

When one sound replaces a multi-sound unit, it replaces only the last sound. This is the default for single-sound substitutions.

---

## 1.1.55 - @deva[अनेकाल्शित्सर्वस्य]

**Key Terms:** anekāl, śit, sarva

@deva[अनेकाल् शित् सर्वस्य] — A multi-sound substitute, or one marked @term[śit], replaces the whole [original].

@sutra[1.1.55]

This complements 1.1.52: when the substitute has multiple sounds OR is @term[śit] (has @deva[श्] as @term[it]), it replaces the entire original, not just the final sound.

---

## concept - The Lopa System

Rules 1.1.60–63 define deletion terms.

---

## 1.1.60 - @deva[अदर्शनं लोपः]

**Key Terms:** lopa, adarśana

@deva[अदर्शनम् लोपः] — Non-appearance is called @term[lopa].

@sutra[1.1.60]

@term[lopa] (deletion) means "replacement by nothing." The deleted element is treated as if it was replaced by zero. This is crucial: the element existed (and triggered rules) but now isn't pronounced.

---

## 1.1.61 - @deva[प्रत्ययस्य लुक्श्लुलुपः]

**Key Terms:** luk, ślu, lup, pratyaya

@deva[प्रत्ययस्य लुक् श्लु लुपः] — For affixes, [the types of deletion are] @term[luk], @term[ślu], and @term[lup].

@sutra[1.1.61]

Three specific deletion types for affixes:
- @term[luk]: complete deletion
- @term[ślu]: deletion with specific consequences
- @term[lup]: deletion preserving certain properties

Each type has different effects on the remaining word.

---

## concept - Position Terms

---

## 1.1.64 - @deva[अचोऽन्त्यादि टि]

**Key Terms:** ṭi, ac, antya

@deva[अचः अन्त्यादि टि] — @term[ṭi] is [the portion] beginning from the final vowel.

@sutra[1.1.64]

@term[ṭi] designates "from the last vowel to the end." In @deva[राम], the @term[ṭi] is @deva[आम]. This term lets rules target the final syllable.

---

## 1.1.65 - @deva[अलोऽन्त्यात् पूर्व उपधा]

**Key Terms:** upadhā, al, antya

@deva[अलः अन्त्यात् पूर्वः उपधा] — @term[upadhā] is the sound before the final.

@sutra[1.1.65]

@term[upadhā] (penultimate) is the second-to-last sound. In @deva[पच्], the @term[upadhā] is @deva[अ]. Many rules strengthen or modify the @term[upadhā].

---

## concept - Interpretive Principles

---

## 1.1.66 - @deva[तस्मिन्निति निर्दिष्टे पूर्वस्य]

**Key Terms:** tasmin, pūrva

@deva[तस्मिन् इति निर्दिष्टे पूर्वस्य] — When [a condition is stated] in the locative, [the operation applies to] the preceding [element].

@sutra[1.1.66]

A locative condition ("when X") triggers an operation on what comes BEFORE. This governs how to read conditional rules.

---

## 1.1.67 - @deva[तस्मादित्युत्तरस्य]

**Key Terms:** tasmāt, uttara

@deva[तस्मात् इति उत्तरस्य] — When [a condition is stated] in the ablative, [the operation applies to] the following [element].

@sutra[1.1.67]

An ablative condition ("after X") triggers an operation on what comes AFTER. Together with 1.1.66, this establishes the grammar's left-right directionality.

---

## 1.1.71 - @deva[आदिरन्त्येन सहेता]

**Key Terms:** pratyāhāra, ādi, antya, it

@deva[आदिः अन्त्येन सह इता] — An initial [sound] together with a final @term[it] [forms a pratyāhāra].

@sutra[1.1.71]

This defines the @term[pratyāhāra] system — Pāṇini's compression mechanism. @pratyahara[ac] means "from @deva[अ] to the @deva[च्] marker" = all vowels. @pratyahara[hal] means "from @deva[ह] to the @deva[ल्] marker" = all consonants.

This single rule enables the Śivasūtras to encode sound classes in just 14 lines.

---

## 1.1.72 - @deva[येन विधिस्तदन्तस्य]

**Key Terms:** vidhi, tadanta

@deva[येन विधिः तदन्तस्य] — A rule [stated] with respect to X applies to [words] ending in X.

@sutra[1.1.72]

When Pāṇini says "after @deva[इ]", he means "after words ending in @deva[इ]." This @term[paribhāṣā] expands the scope of rules from individual sounds to complete words.

---

## 1.1.73 - @deva[वृद्धिर्यस्याचामादिस्तद् वृद्धम्]

**Key Terms:** vṛddha

@deva[वृद्धिः यस्य अचाम् आदिः तत् वृद्धम्] — A word whose first vowel is @term[vṛddhi] is called @term[vṛddha].

@sutra[1.1.73]

@term[vṛddha] ("old, venerable") describes words beginning with @deva[आ], @deva[ऐ], or @deva[औ]. This designation triggers specific @term[taddhita] formations — certain suffixes attach only to @term[vṛddha] bases.

## concept - The Complete Saṃjñā System

The first @term[pāda] establishes Pāṇini's vocabulary:

| Category | Rules | Examples |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Vowel grades | 1.1.1–6 | @term[vṛddhi], @term[guṇa] |
| Sound classes | 1.1.7–10 | @term[saṃyoga], @term[savarṇa], @term[anunāsika] |
| Exempt vowels | 1.1.11–19 | @term[pragṛhya] |
| Nominal classes | 1.1.20–43 | @term[ghu], @term[sarvanāman], @term[avyaya] |
