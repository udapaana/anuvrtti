## concept - @term[sat] — Present Participles

@term[sat] (@deva[शतृ] and @deva[शानच्]) forms are verbal adjectives describing ongoing action. They combine a verb's meaning with adjective-like declension, enabling complex sentences that would otherwise require relative clauses.

**Two types based on @term[pada]:**

| @term[pratyaya] | @term[pada]         | Ending                      | Example                   | Translation      |
| --------------- | ------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------- | ---------------- |
| @deva[शतृ]      | @term[parasmaipada] | @deva[-अत्] / @deva[-अन्त्] | @deva[पचत्] → @deva[पचन्] | cooking (active) |
| @deva[शानच्]    | @term[ātmanepada]   | @deva[-मान]                 | @deva[पचमानः]             | cooking (middle) |

**Common active participles (@deva[शतृ]):**

| Root        | Present       | Participle (Nom. M.) | Meaning         |
| ----------- | ------------- | -------------------- | --------------- |
| @deva[भू]   | @deva[भवति]   | @deva[भवन्]          | being, becoming |
| @deva[गम्]  | @deva[गच्छति] | @deva[गच्छन्]        | going           |
| @deva[पठ्]  | @deva[पठति]   | @deva[पठन्]          | reading         |
| @deva[कृ]   | @deva[करोति]  | @deva[कुर्वन्]       | doing           |
| @deva[नी]   | @deva[नयति]   | @deva[नयन्]          | leading         |
| @deva[वद्]  | @deva[वदति]   | @deva[वदन्]          | speaking        |
| @deva[हस्]  | @deva[हसति]   | @deva[हसन्]          | laughing        |
| @deva[धाव्] | @deva[धावति]  | @deva[धावन्]         | running         |

The @deva[शतृ] stem ends in @deva[-अत्] (weak) / @deva[-अन्त्] (strong), following the consonant-stem declension like @deva[भगवत्].

## @ref[3.2.124] - लटः शतृशानचावप्रथमासमानाधिकरणे

**Key Terms:** @deva[लट्], @deva[शतृ], @deva[शानच्]

The affixes @deva[शतृ] and @deva[शानच्] substitute for @deva[लट्] when the participle agrees with a word **not** in the Nominative case (i.e., when it serves as an adjective in oblique cases).

**Key point:** The participle replaces the finite verb when describing a noun in a non-nominative role:

- @deva[पचन्तं देवदत्तं पश्य] "Behold Devadatta **who is cooking**" (Acc.)
- @deva[पचता कृतम्] "Done **by one who is cooking**" (Inst.)
- @deva[गच्छन्तं बालं रक्ष] "Protect the child **who is going**" (Acc.)
- @deva[हसतः नरस्य] "Of the man **who is laughing**" (Gen.)

Without the participle, you'd need a relative clause: @deva[यः पचति तं देवदत्तं पश्य].

---

## @ref[3.2.125] - सम्बोधने च

**Key Terms:** @deva[शतृ], @deva[शानच्], @deva[सम्बोधन]

@deva[शतृ] and @deva[शानच्] substitute for @deva[लट्] even with Nominative case when in Vocative address.

- @deva[हे पचन्] "O thou who art cooking"
- @deva[हे पचमान] "O thou who art cooking" (middle)
- @deva[हे गच्छन्] "O thou who art going"

---

## @ref[1.4.99] - लः परस्मैपदम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[शतृ], @deva[परस्मैपद]

The substitutes of @deva[ल] are called @deva[परस्मैपद]. @deva[शतृ] creates **active** participles and is used with @deva[परस्मैपद] verbs:

- @deva[भू] (P.) → @deva[भवत्] "being"
- @deva[गम्] (P.) → @deva[गच्छत्] "going"
- @deva[पठ्] (P.) → @deva[पठत्] "reading"
- @deva[कृ] (U.) → @deva[कुर्वत्] "doing" (P. participle)

---

## @ref[1.4.100] - तङानावात्मनेपदम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[शानच्], @deva[आत्मनेपद]

Affixes ending in @deva[आन] (@deva[शानच्] and @deva[कानच्]) are called @deva[आत्मनेपद]. @deva[शानच्] creates **middle** participles:

- @deva[सेव्] (Ā.) → @deva[सेवमानः] "serving"
- @deva[लभ्] (Ā.) → @deva[लभमानः] "obtaining"
- @deva[कृ] (U.) → @deva[कुर्वाणः] "doing" (Ā. participle)
- @deva[मन्] (Ā.) → @deva[मन्वानः] "thinking"

The @deva[शानच्] participle always ends in @deva[-मान] / @deva[-आन] and declines like a regular @deva[अ]-stem adjective.

---

## @ref[3.2.127] - तौ सत्

**Key Terms:** @deva[शतृ], @deva[शानच्], @deva[सत्]

These two — @deva[शतृ] and @deva[शानच्] — are collectively called @deva[सत्]. This technical name allows other rules to refer to both participles together.

They replace not only Present tense but sometimes **Future** too:

- Present: @deva[कुर्वन्] "doing (now)"
- Future: @deva[करिष्यन्] "about to do, going to do"
- Present middle: @deva[कुर्वाणः] "doing (now)"
- Future middle: @deva[करिष्यमाणः] "about to do"

---

## @ref[6.4.98] - गमहनजनखनघसां लोपः क्ङित्यनङि

**Key Terms:** @deva[गम्], @deva[हन्], @deva[लोप]

The root-vowel of @deva[गम्], @deva[हन्], @deva[जन्], @deva[खन्], and @deva[घस्] is elided before an affix with indicatory @deva[क्] or @deva[ङ्] (but not before Aorist @deva[अङ्]).

This explains why:

- @deva[गम्] → @deva[गच्छत्] (not \*@deva[गमत्]) — the present stem @deva[गच्छ] is used, not the bare root
- @deva[हन्] → @deva[घ्नत्] (in certain forms, the @deva[अ] drops)
- @deva[जन्] → @deva[जायमानः] (middle participle uses the special present stem)

---

## @ref[6.1.67] - वेरपृक्तस्य

**Key Terms:** @deva[वि], @deva[लोप]

There is elision of the affix @deva[वि] when reduced to a single letter. This affects certain @deva[क्विप्] / @deva[क्विन्] derivatives but not the standard @deva[शतृ] / @deva[शानच्] forms.

---

## @ref[7.3.84] - सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः

**Key Terms:** @deva[गुण], @deva[शतृ]

@deva[गुण] substitutes for the final @deva[इक्] vowel of a stem before @deva[सार्वधातुक] and @deva[आर्धधातुक] affixes. Both @deva[शतृ] and @deva[शानच्] are @deva[सार्वधातुक], so @deva[गुण] applies:

- @deva[भू] + @deva[शतृ] → @deva[भो] → @deva[भवत्] (by @deva[गुण] and @deva[अयादेश])
- @deva[नी] + @deva[शतृ] → @deva[ने] → @deva[नयत्] (by @deva[गुण] and @deva[अयादेश])
- @deva[कृ] + @deva[शतृ] → @deva[कुर्वत्] (8th class, special stem)

## concept - Declension of @deva[शतृ] Participles

The @deva[शतृ] participle declines like a consonant stem with strong/weak alternation:

**@deva[भवत्] "being" — Masculine:**

| Case  | Singular       | Dual              | Plural         |
| ----- | -------------- | ----------------- | -------------- |
| Nom.  | @deva[भवन्]    | @deva[भवन्तौ]     | @deva[भवन्तः]  |
| Acc.  | @deva[भवन्तम्] | @deva[भवन्तौ]     | @deva[भवतः]    |
| Inst. | @deva[भवता]    | @deva[भवद्भ्याम्] | @deva[भवद्भिः] |
| Gen.  | @deva[भवतः]    | @deva[भवतोः]      | @deva[भवताम्]  |

Strong cases (Nom., Voc., Acc. Sg.): @deva[-अन्त्]
Weak cases: @deva[-अत्]

**@deva[भवत्] — Feminine (@deva[-अन्ती] / @deva[-अती]):**

- Nom. Sg.: @deva[भवन्ती] "she who is being"
- Acc. Sg.: @deva[भवन्तीम्]

**@deva[भवत्] — Neuter:**

- Nom./Acc. Sg.: @deva[भवत्]
- Nom./Acc. Pl.: @deva[भवन्ति]

## concept - Participles in Sanskrit Prose

Participles are the backbone of Sanskrit prose style, used far more frequently than relative clauses:

**1. Adjectival use:**

- @deva[गच्छन् नरः पतति] "The **going** man falls" (= "The man who is going falls")
- @deva[हसन्ती बाला खेलति] "The **laughing** girl plays"

**2. Circumstantial (absolute) use:**

- @deva[पठन् जानाति] "**While reading**, he knows" (= "He learns by reading")
- @deva[गच्छन् पश्यति] "**While going**, he sees"

**3. With @deva[सति] (locative absolute):**

- @deva[सूर्ये उदयति] "**When the sun is rising**" (locative participle)
- @deva[राजनि गच्छति] "**When the king is going**"

**4. As nouns:**

- @deva[भवन्] / @deva[भवान्] "your honor" (the "being one" → polite "you")
- @deva[सेवमानः] "the one who serves, the worshipper"
