## concept - Commands, Requests, and Blessings

The imperative (@deva[लोट्]) expresses commands, requests, invitations, and blessings — any situation where the speaker wants the action to happen:

- **Command**: @deva[गच्छ] "go!" @deva[पठ] "read!"
- **Polite request**: @deva[भवान् आगच्छतु] "please come"
- **Invitation**: @deva[अत्र उपविशतु भवान्] "please sit here"
- **Blessing**: @deva[चिरं जीव] "live long!"

Unlike @deva[लट्] (present), @deva[लोट्] doesn't describe what's happening — it expresses what the speaker wants to happen.

## concept - The Imperative Paradigm

Here's @deva[भू] "to be" in the imperative:

**@deva[परस्मैपद]:**
| | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|--|----------|------|--------|
| 3rd | @deva[भवतु] | @deva[भवताम्] | @deva[भवन्तु] |
| 2nd | @deva[भव] | @deva[भवतम्] | @deva[भवत] |
| 1st | @deva[भवानि] | @deva[भवाव] | @deva[भवाम] |

Compare with @deva[लट्]:
| | @deva[लट्] | @deva[लोट्] | Change |
|--|------|------|--------|
| 3sg | @deva[भवति] | @deva[भवतु] | @deva[इ] → @deva[उ] |
| 2sg | @deva[भवसि] | @deva[भव] | @deva[सि] → @deva[हि] → deleted |
| 3pl | @deva[भवन्ति] | @deva[भवन्तु] | @deva[इ] → @deva[उ] |

The pattern: final @deva[इ] becomes @deva[उ], and the 2nd singular loses its ending entirely (for a-stems).

## @ref[3.3.162] - लोट् च

**Key Terms:** @deva[लोट्], @deva[विधि]

@deva[लोट् च] — @deva[लोट्] comes after a root to express commands and related senses.

@sutra[3.3.162]

This rule introduces the @deva[लोट्] lakāra. Like @deva[लट्], it's an abstract marker — the @deva[ल] gets replaced by endings, and the derivation proceeds from there.

@prakriya[bhU + loT + tip]{collapsed}

## @ref[3.4.86] - एरुः

**Key Terms:** @deva[इ], @deva[उ]

@deva[एरुः] — In @deva[लोट्], @deva[उ] replaces the final @deva[इ] of endings.

@sutra[3.4.86]

This is the core transformation that distinguishes @deva[लोट्] from @deva[लट्]:

| @deva[लट्] ending | @deva[लोट्] ending | Form                        |
| ----------------- | ------------------ | --------------------------- |
| @deva[ति]         | @deva[तु]          | @deva[भवतु] "let him be"    |
| @deva[अन्ति]      | @deva[अन्तु]       | @deva[भवन्तु] "let them be" |
| @deva[मि]         | —                  | (different rule)            |

The @deva[इ] → @deva[उ] change applies broadly to all @deva[लोट्] endings that end in @deva[इ].

## @ref[3.4.87] - सेर्ह्यपिच्च

**Key Terms:** @deva[सि], @deva[हि], @deva[अपित्]

@deva[सेर्ह्यपिच्च] — In @deva[लोट्], @deva[हि] replaces @deva[सि] (2nd singular), and this @deva[हि] is @deva[अपित्] (lacks @deva[प्] as marker).

@sutra[3.4.87]

The 2nd singular is where imperatives differ most from other tenses. Instead of @deva[सि], you get @deva[हि]:

- @deva[नी] + @deva[हि] → @deva[नय] → wait, @deva[हि] is @deva[अपित्], so it's @deva[ङित्] by @ref[1.2.4]
- Being @deva[ङित्], it blocks @deva[गुण]: @deva[लुनीहि] "cut!" @deva[कुरुहि] "do!"

But this @deva[हि] often disappears...

## @ref[6.4.105] - अतो हेः

**Key Terms:** @deva[अ], @deva[हि], @deva[लोप]

@deva[अतो हेः] — After a stem ending in short @deva[अ], @deva[हि] is elided.

@sutra[6.4.105]

This is why most Class 1 imperatives have no ending at all in 2nd singular:

- @deva[भव] + @deva[हि] → @deva[भव] (the @deva[हि] drops after @deva[अ])
- @deva[पच] + @deva[हि] → @deva[पच] "cook!"
- @deva[गच्छ] + @deva[हि] → @deva[गच्छ] "go!"
- @deva[पठ] + @deva[हि] → @deva[पठ] "read!"

But after other vowels, @deva[हि] stays:

- @deva[कुरु] + @deva[हि] → @deva[कुरु] (Class 8 — @deva[हि] drops after @deva[उ] too by related rules)
- @deva[लुनीहि] "cut!" (Class 9 — @deva[ई], so @deva[हि] stays)
- @deva[जुहुधि] "sacrifice!" (Class 3)

## @ref[3.4.85] - लोटो लङ्वत्

**Key Terms:** @deva[लोट्], @deva[लङ्वत्]

@deva[लोटो लङ्वत्] — @deva[लोट्] endings behave like @deva[लङ्] endings (in certain respects).

@sutra[3.4.85]

Some @deva[लोट्] forms use the same ending substitutions as the imperfect (@deva[लङ्]):

- @deva[ताम्] replaces @deva[तस्] (3rd dual) → @deva[भवताम्]
- @deva[तम्] replaces @deva[थस्] (2nd dual) → @deva[भवतम्]
- @deva[त] replaces @deva[थ] (2nd plural) → @deva[भवत]

But @deva[लोट्] does **not** take the @deva[अट्] augment that @deva[लङ्] takes. No \*@deva[अभवतु].

## @ref[7.1.35] - तुह्योस्तातङाशिषि

**Key Terms:** @deva[तु], @deva[हि], @deva[तातङ्], @deva[आशिष्]

@deva[तुह्योस्तातङाशिषि] — In benedictions (@deva[आशिष्]), @deva[तातङ्] optionally replaces @deva[तु] and @deva[हि].

@sutra[7.1.35]

This creates special blessing forms:

- @deva[जीवतु] or @deva[जीवतात्] "may he live!"
- @deva[जीव] or @deva[जीवतात्] "may you live!"

The @deva[तात्] form has an archaic, formal quality and appears in blessings, prayers, and ritual language.

## @ref[3.4.89] - मा लोटि

**Key Terms:** @deva[मा], @deva[लोट्]

@deva[मा लोटि] — The particle @deva[मा] with @deva[लोट्] expresses prohibition.

@sutra[3.4.89]

Negative commands use @deva[मा] + imperative:

- @deva[मा गच्छ] "don't go!"
- @deva[मा भवतु] "let it not be"
- @deva[मा कुरु] "don't do (it)!"

Note: @deva[मा] can also be used with @deva[लुङ्] and @deva[लिङ्] for prohibition.

## concept - Common Imperative Forms

Forms you'll encounter most often:

| Root               | 3sg           | 2sg         | 3pl              |
| ------------------ | ------------- | ----------- | ---------------- |
| @deva[भू] "be"     | @deva[भवतु]   | @deva[भव]   | @deva[भवन्तु]    |
| @deva[गम्] "go"    | @deva[गच्छतु] | @deva[गच्छ] | @deva[गच्छन्तु]  |
| @deva[कृ] "do"     | @deva[करोतु]  | @deva[कुरु] | @deva[कुर्वन्तु] |
| @deva[दा] "give"   | @deva[ददातु]  | @deva[देहि] | @deva[ददतु]      |

| @deva[वद्] "speak" | @deva[वदतु]   | @deva[वद]   | @deva[वदन्तु]    |

## concept - What's Next

The imperative is one of the four @deva[सार्वधातुक] tenses (present, imperative, imperfect, optative) that share the same @deva[विकरण] system. Having learned @deva[लट्] and @deva[लोट्], you're ready for:

- **Imperfect** (@deva[लङ्]) — past tense with the @deva[अट्] augment
- **Optative** (@deva[विधिलिङ्]) — "should, would, may"
