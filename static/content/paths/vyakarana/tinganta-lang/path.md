## concept - @deva[लङ्] — The Narrative Past

@deva[लङ्] is the most common @term[bhūta] (past) @deva[लकार] in Sanskrit narrative. While @deva[लट्] describes what's happening now, @deva[लङ्] describes what happened — specifically, past actions **not of the current day** (@term[anadyatana]):

- @deva[अभवत्] "he was, he became"
- @deva[अपचत्] "he cooked"
- @deva[अगच्छत्] "he went"

Two features distinguish @deva[लङ्] from @deva[लट्]:

1. An **augment @deva[अ]** is prefixed to the verb
2. The endings are **slightly modified** (shorter forms)

---

## concept - The @deva[लङ्] Paradigm

Here's @deva[भू] "to be" in @deva[लङ्]:

**@deva[परस्मैपद]:**
| | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|--|----------|------|--------|
| 3rd | @deva[अभवत्] | @deva[अभवताम्] | @deva[अभवन्] |
| 2nd | @deva[अभवः] | @deva[अभवतम्] | @deva[अभवत] |
| 1st | @deva[अभवम्] | @deva[अभवाव] | @deva[अभवाम] |

Compare with @deva[लट्]:
| | @deva[लट्] | @deva[लङ्] | Changes |
|--|------|------|---------|
| 3sg | @deva[भवति] | @deva[अभवत्] | adds @deva[अ], drops final @deva[इ] |
| 2sg | @deva[भवसि] | @deva[अभवः] | adds @deva[अ], @deva[सि] → @deva[स्] → visarga |
| 1sg | @deva[भवामि] | @deva[अभवम्] | adds @deva[अ], @deva[मि] → @deva[अम्] |
| 3pl | @deva[भवन्ति] | @deva[अभवन्] | adds @deva[अ], @deva[झि] → @deva[जुस्] → @deva[अन्] |

The pattern: add @deva[अ] at the front, and endings lose their final vowel.

## @ref[3.2.111] - अनद्यतने लङ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[अनद्यतन], @deva[लङ्]

@deva[अनद्यतने लङ्] — @deva[लङ्] comes after a root for past action not of the current day.

@sutra[3.2.111]

@deva[अनद्यतन] means "not of today" — the action happened before the current day. For actions earlier today, Sanskrit uses @deva[लुङ्] (aorist) in strict Pāṇinian usage, though in later Sanskrit @deva[लङ्] covers all past tenses.

@prakriya[bhU + laN + tip]{collapsed}

## @ref[6.4.71] - लुङ्लङ्लृङ्क्ष्वडुदात्तः

**Key Terms:** @deva[अट्], @deva[उदात्त], @deva[लङ्]

@deva[लुङ्लङ्लृङ्क्ष्वडुदात्तः] — The augment @deva[अट्] (= @deva[अ]) is prefixed to the verb in @deva[लुङ्], @deva[लङ्], and @deva[लृङ्].

@sutra[6.4.71]

This is the defining feature of the imperfect — the prefixed @deva[अ], traditionally called the "augment":

- @deva[भवति] → @deva[**अ**भवत्]
- @deva[पचति] → @deva[**अ**पचत्]
- @deva[करोति] → @deva[**अ**करोत्]

When the root itself begins with a vowel, the augment merges by sandhi:

- @deva[अस्ति] → @deva[**आ**सीत्] (@deva[अ] + @deva[अ] = @deva[आ] by @ref[6.1.87])

## @ref[6.4.72] - आडजादीनाम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[आट्], @deva[अजादि]

@deva[आडजादीनाम्] — For stems beginning with a vowel, @deva[आट्] (= @deva[आ]) is the augment instead of @deva[अट्].

@sutra[6.4.72]

When the root starts with a vowel, the augment is @deva[आ] and @deva[वृद्धि] applies:

- @deva[इ] (root @deva[इ] "to go") → @deva[ऐ]: @deva[ऐत्] "he went"
- @deva[उ] (root @deva[उष्] "to burn") → @deva[औ]: @deva[औषत्] "it burned"

## @ref[3.4.100] - इतश्च

**Key Terms:** @deva[इ], @deva[लोप]

@deva[इतश्च] — The final @deva[इ] of @deva[परस्मैपद] endings is elided when they replace a @deva[ल] marked with @deva[ङ्].

@sutra[3.4.100]

@deva[लङ्] has @deva[ङ्] as @deva[इत्] (the @deva[ङ्] in @deva[लङ्]). So this rule applies: the final @deva[इ] of parasmaipada endings drops:

| @deva[लट्] ending | @deva[लङ्] ending   | Why                    |
| ----------------- | ------------------- | ---------------------- |
| @deva[ति]         | @deva[त्]           | @deva[इ] dropped       |
| @deva[सि]         | @deva[स्] → visarga | @deva[इ] dropped       |
| @deva[मि]         | —                   | replaced by @deva[अम्] |
| @deva[अन्ति]      | @deva[अन्]          | @deva[इ] dropped       |

This explains why imperfect forms end in consonants: @deva[अभवत्], @deva[अभवन्], @deva[अभवः].

## @ref[3.4.101] - तस्थस्थमिपां तांतंतामः

**Key Terms:** @deva[तस्], @deva[थस्], @deva[थ], @deva[मिप्]

@deva[तस्थस्थमिपां तांतंतामः] — For @deva[ल] marked with @deva[ङ्], @deva[ताम्], @deva[तम्], @deva[त], @deva[अम्] replace @deva[तस्], @deva[थस्], @deva[थ], @deva[मिप्].

@sutra[3.4.101]

Additional ending replacements for @deva[लङ्]:

| @deva[लट्] ending | @deva[लङ्] ending | Person/Number |
| ----------------- | ----------------- | ------------- |
| @deva[तस्]        | @deva[ताम्]       | 3rd dual      |
| @deva[थस्]        | @deva[तम्]        | 2nd dual      |
| @deva[थ]          | @deva[त]          | 2nd plural    |
| @deva[मिप्]       | @deva[अम्]        | 1st singular  |

So: @deva[अभवताम्] "they two were," @deva[अभवतम्] "you two were," @deva[अभवत] "you all were," @deva[अभवम्] "I was."

## @ref[3.4.108] - झेर्जुस्

**Key Terms:** @deva[झि], @deva[जुस्]

@deva[झेर्जुस्] — @deva[जुस्] replaces @deva[झि] (3rd plural P) in @deva[लङ्] and @deva[विधिलिङ्].

@sutra[3.4.108]

In @deva[लट्], 3rd plural is @deva[भवन्ति] (ending @deva[झि] → @deva[अन्ति]). In @deva[लङ्], @deva[झि] is replaced by @deva[जुस्] (= @deva[अन्] after marker deletion):

- @deva[अभव] + @deva[अन्] → @deva[अभवन्] "they were"
- @deva[अपच] + @deva[अन्] → @deva[अपचन्] "they cooked"

## concept - Reading Imperfect in Context

The imperfect is the default narrative tense. Epic and story literature uses it constantly:

> @deva[अथ रामः वनम् अगच्छत्। तत्र सीतां अपश्यत्। स ताम् अभाषत।]

"Then Rāma went to the forest. There he saw Sītā. He spoke to her."

Every main verb is @deva[लङ्]: @deva[अगच्छत्], @deva[अपश्यत्], @deva[अभाषत]. The @deva[अ] prefix is a reliable visual marker — whenever you see a verb beginning with @deva[अ] that isn't a root starting with @deva[अ], it's likely imperfect (or aorist/conditional).

## concept - What's Next

You now know three of the four @deva[सार्वधातुक] tenses:

- @deva[लट्] (present) — @deva[भवति]
- @deva[लोट्] (imperative) — @deva[भवतु]
- @deva[लङ्] (imperfect) — @deva[अभवत्]

The fourth is the **optative** (@deva[विधिलिङ्]) — @deva[भवेत्] "he should be" — which uses a different ending mechanism altogether.
