## concept - Possessive Derivation with @deva[मतुप्]

The suffix @deva[मतुप्] is one of the most productive in Sanskrit. It creates adjectives meaning "possessing X" or "endowed with X" from noun stems. The resulting words decline like consonant stems in @deva[-मत्] / @deva[-वत्].

**Core pattern:** noun + @deva[मतुप्] → adjective "having that noun"

| Base        | Meaning  | + @deva[मतुप्] | Nom. Sg. M.     | Meaning                 |
| ----------- | -------- | -------------- | --------------- | ----------------------- |
| @deva[गो]   | cow      | @deva[गोमत्]   | @deva[गोमान्]   | having cows             |
| @deva[बल]   | strength | @deva[बलवत्]   | @deva[बलवान्]   | strong                  |
| @deva[धी]   | wisdom   | @deva[धीमत्]   | @deva[धीमान्]   | wise                    |
| @deva[श्री] | glory    | @deva[श्रीमत्] | @deva[श्रीमान्] | glorious                |
| @deva[हनु]  | jaw      | @deva[हनुमत्]  | @deva[हनुमान्]  | having (prominent) jaws |
| @deva[भग]   | fortune  | @deva[भगवत्]   | @deva[भगवान्]   | blessed                 |

The @deva[म्] of @deva[मतुप्] alternates with @deva[व्] under specific phonological conditions (see @ref[8.2.9] below), yielding the familiar @deva[-वत्] / @deva[-वान्] pattern.

---

## @ref[5.2.94] - तदस्यास्त्यस्मिन्निति मतुप्

**Key Terms:** @deva[मतुप्], @deva[अस्ति], @deva[अस्मिन्]

The affix @deva[मतुप्] comes in the sense of "whose it is" or "in whom it is" after a word in the Nominative case.

This rule defines the core semantic: @deva[मतुप्] encodes possession or inherent presence.

**Two senses:**

1. **"whose it is"** (@deva[तदस्य अस्ति]) — external possession:
   - @deva[गावः अस्य सन्ति] = @deva[गोमान्] "one who has cows"
   - @deva[धनम् अस्य अस्ति] = @deva[धनवान्] "one who has wealth"

2. **"in whom it is"** (@deva[तदस्मिन् अस्ति]) — inherent quality:
   - @deva[बुद्धिः अस्मिन् अस्ति] = @deva[बुद्धिमान्] "one in whom intelligence exists"
   - @deva[वृक्षाः अस्मिन् सन्ति] = @deva[वृक्षवान्] (a place) "where trees exist"

## @ref[8.2.9] - मादुपधायाश्च मतोर्वोऽयवादिभ्यः

**Key Terms:** @deva[मतुप्], @deva[वत्], @deva[उपधा]

For the @deva[म्] of @deva[मत्], @deva[व्] substitutes when the base:

- ends in @deva[अ] / @deva[आ], or
- has @deva[म्] in penultimate position, or
- belongs to the @deva[अयवादि] group.

This is why most common forms use @deva[-वत्] rather than @deva[-मत्]:

| Condition            | Base       | Result        | Explanation                             |
| -------------------- | ---------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| @deva[अ]-final       | @deva[बल]  | @deva[बलवत्]  | stem ends in @deva[अ] → @deva[व्]       |
| @deva[अ]-final       | @deva[धन]  | @deva[धनवत्]  | stem ends in @deva[अ] → @deva[व्]       |
| @deva[अ]-final       | @deva[भग]  | @deva[भगवत्]  | stem ends in @deva[अ] → @deva[व्]       |
| @deva[म]-penultimate | @deva[गम]  | @deva[गमवत्]  | @deva[म] in penultimate → @deva[व्]     |
| Other                | @deva[गो]  | @deva[गोमत्]  | not @deva[अ]-final → @deva[म्] retained |
| Other                | @deva[धी]  | @deva[धीमत्]  | not @deva[अ]-final → @deva[म्] retained |
| Other                | @deva[हनु] | @deva[हनुमत्] | @deva[उ]-final → @deva[म्] retained     |

**Rule of thumb:** @deva[अ]-stems → @deva[-वत्]; other vowel stems → @deva[-मत्].

## @ref[6.4.138] - अचः

**Key Terms:** @deva[अच्], @deva[मतुप्]

Before @deva[मतुप्], stems ending in vowels follow specific sandhi rules. The final vowel of the base undergoes adjustments before the suffix.

**Feminine formation:** The @deva[-मत्] / @deva[-वत्] stems form feminines with @deva[ङीप्] (= @deva[ई]):

| Masculine       | Feminine       | Meaning  |
| --------------- | -------------- | -------- |
| @deva[बलवान्]   | @deva[बलवती]   | strong   |
| @deva[धीमान्]   | @deva[धीमती]   | wise     |
| @deva[श्रीमान्] | @deva[श्रीमती] | glorious |
| @deva[भगवान्]   | @deva[भगवती]   | blessed  |

The neuter nominative singular ends in @deva[-मत्] / @deva[-वत्] directly: @deva[बलवत्] (neuter), @deva[धीमत्] (neuter).

## @ref[7.1.70] - उगिदचां सर्वनामस्थानेऽधातोः

**Key Terms:** @deva[मतुप्], @deva[सर्वनामस्थान], @deva[नुम्]

In @deva[सर्वनामस्थान] (strong) cases — Nominative, Vocative, and Accusative singular — stems ending in @deva[मतुप्] take the @deva[नुम्] augment.

This is what produces the characteristic @deva[-मान्] / @deva[-वान्] ending:

- @deva[बलवत्] + @deva[नुम्] + @deva[सु] → @deva[बलवान्] (Nom. Sg.)
- @deva[धीमत्] + @deva[नुम्] + @deva[सु] → @deva[धीमान्]

**Strong vs. weak stems:**

| Case              | @deva[बल] + @deva[मतुप्] | @deva[धी] + @deva[मतुप्] |
| ----------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------ |
| Nom. Sg. (strong) | @deva[बलवान्]            | @deva[धीमान्]            |
| Acc. Sg. (strong) | @deva[बलवन्तम्]          | @deva[धीमन्तम्]          |
| Inst. Sg. (weak)  | @deva[बलवता]             | @deva[धीमता]             |
| Nom. Pl. (strong) | @deva[बलवन्तः]           | @deva[धीमन्तः]           |
| Inst. Pl. (weak)  | @deva[बलवद्भिः]          | @deva[धीमद्भिः]          |

The paradigm follows the consonant-stem declension pattern, with @deva[नुम्] in strong cases and direct @deva[-त्] in weak cases.

## @ref[7.3.84] - सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः

**Key Terms:** @deva[गुण], @deva[मतुप्]

@deva[गुण] substitutes for @deva[इक्] vowels before suffixes marked @deva[सार्वधातुक] or @deva[आर्धधातुक]. Though @deva[मतुप्] itself is not directly affected by this rule, it interacts with stems that have undergone @deva[गुण] in their base form.

Note that @deva[धी] + @deva[मतुप्] = @deva[धीमत्] (no @deva[गुण] here because @deva[मतुप्] is not @deva[सार्वधातुक] or @deva[आर्धधातुक]). The @deva[ई] stays long.

---

## @ref[6.1.68] - हलः

**Key Terms:** @deva[हल्], @deva[मतुप्]

After consonant-final stems, @deva[मतुप्] is added directly without sandhi complications.

Examples with consonant-final stems:

- @deva[विद्युत्] + @deva[मतुप्] → @deva[विद्युत्वत्] (having lightning)
- @deva[पयस्] + @deva[मतुप्] → @deva[पयस्वत्] (having water/milk)

---

## @ref[8.2.10] - वान्तो य्यि प्रत्यये

**Key Terms:** @deva[वत्], @deva[पद]

At word boundaries, the @deva[व्] in @deva[वत्] forms may undergo changes before certain following suffixes. This is relevant for compound formation and sentence sandhi.

## concept - Common @deva[मतुप्] Words in Literature

Many of the most familiar Sanskrit words are @deva[मतुप्]-derivatives:

| Word            | Base                 | Meaning                 | Famous usage              |
| --------------- | -------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------- |
| @deva[भगवान्]   | @deva[भग] (fortune)  | the Blessed One         | Bhagavad Gītā, Bhāgavata  |
| @deva[हनुमान्]  | @deva[हनु] (jaw)     | having (prominent) jaws | Rāmāyaṇa                  |
| @deva[श्रीमान्] | @deva[श्री] (glory)  | glorious                | honorific title           |
| @deva[धीमान्]   | @deva[धी] (wisdom)   | wise                    | epithets of sages         |
| @deva[बलवान्]   | @deva[बल] (strength) | strong                  | descriptions of warriors  |
| @deva[गुणवान्]  | @deva[गुण] (quality) | virtuous                | descriptions of character |
| @deva[रूपवती]   | @deva[रूप] (beauty)  | beautiful (f.)          | descriptions of women     |

**Reading practice:** Identify the base noun in each @deva[मतुप्]-derivative:

1. @deva[पुत्रवान्] → @deva[पुत्र] (son) → "having a son"
2. @deva[विद्यावान्] → @deva[विद्या] (knowledge) → "learned"
3. @deva[तेजस्वी] → @deva[तेजस्] (brilliance) → "brilliant"
4. @deva[मेधावी] → @deva[मेधा] (intellect) → "intelligent"
