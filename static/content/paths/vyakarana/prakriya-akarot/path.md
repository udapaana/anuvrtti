## concept - The Imperfect: Witnessed Past

The @term[laṅ] (imperfect) expresses past action witnessed by the speaker — recent past, narrative immediacy:

@deva[अहं तत्र अगच्छम्] — "I went there" (I saw it happen)

The imperfect is distinctive for two features:
1. **@deva[अट्] augment** — a prefix @deva[अ] added before the root
2. **Secondary endings** — different from present-tense endings

This path traces @deva[अकरोत्] "he did" from the Class 8 root @deva[कृ].

@prakriya[kR + laN + t]

---

## concept - Step 1: The Input

We start with:

1. **Root**: @deva[कृ] "to do, make" (Class 8, तनादिगण)
2. **Meaning**: past tense (witnessed), 3rd singular
3. **Special features**: Class 8 uses @deva[उ] class affix, and takes the augment

Note: @deva[कृ] is listed as @deva[डुकृञ्] in the Dhātupāṭha. The @deva[डु] and @deva[ञ्] are @term[it] markers.

---

## 3.2.111 - Add the Imperfect Marker

**Key Terms:** anadyatana, bhūta, laṅ

@deva[अनद्यतने लङ्] — The affix @deva[लङ्] comes for past action not of today.

This adds the @deva[लङ्] marker:

> @deva[कृ] + @deva[लङ्]

@deva[लङ्] signals "imperfect tense." The @deva[ङ्] is @term[it], leaving @deva[ल] as the meaningful part.

---

## 3.4.78 - Select the Secondary Ending

**Key Terms:** tiṅ, t

For @deva[लङ्], we don't use @deva[तिप्] (primary ending). Instead, @ref[3.4.100] prescribes secondary endings:

@deva[इतश्च] — After @deva[इ] [in the lakāra name], [secondary endings are used].

@deva[लङ्] ends in @deva[ङ्] (which follows @deva[अ], not @deva[इ]), but the secondary endings apply to the past tenses. The 3sg secondary ending is @deva[त्]:

> @deva[कृ] + @deva[त्]

Note: @deva[त्] (secondary) vs @deva[ति] (primary). The secondary ending lacks the final @deva[इ].

---

## 6.4.71 - Add the Augment

**Key Terms:** luṅ, laṅ, lṛṅ, aṭ

@deva[लुङ्लङ्लृङ्क्ष्वडुदात्तः] — Before @deva[लुङ्], @deva[लङ्], and @deva[लृङ्], the augment @deva[अट्] (= @deva[अ]) is added with @term[udātta] accent.

This is the characteristic past-tense prefix:

> @deva[अट्] + @deva[कृ] + @deva[त्]

The @deva[ट्] in @deva[अट्] is @term[it] (by @ref[1.3.3]). By @ref[1.1.46], @term[ṭit] augments attach at the BEGINNING:

> @deva[अ] + @deva[कृ] + @deva[त्]

This @deva[अ] is the hallmark of past tenses in Sanskrit (and across Indo-European).

---

## 3.1.79 - Insert the Class Affix

**Key Terms:** tana, u

@deva[तनादिकृञ्भ्य उः] — The roots @deva[तन्], etc., and @deva[कृ] take @deva[उ] as class affix.

@deva[कृ] is Class 8 (@deva[तनादि]), so it takes @deva[उ] (not @deva[शप्]):

> @deva[अ] + @deva[कृ] + @deva[उ] + @deva[त्]

This @deva[उ] sits between root and ending.

---

## 7.3.84 - Apply Guṇa

**Key Terms:** guṇa, sārvadhātuka

@deva[सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः] — An @pratyahara[ik] vowel takes @term[guṇa] before @term[sārvadhātuka] affixes.

The @deva[उ] class affix is @term[sārvadhātuka]. So the root vowel @deva[ऋ] takes guṇa:

> @deva[ऋ] → @deva[अर्] (guṇa of @deva[ऋ])

Now we have:

> @deva[अ] + @deva[कर्] + @deva[उ] + @deva[त्]

---

## 7.3.89 - Strengthen to ओ

**Key Terms:** u, ṇit, guṇa

@deva[उदोष्ठ्यपूर्वस्य] — For [a stem ending in] @deva[उ] preceded by a labial or @deva[ऋ]-derived vowel, [further strengthening occurs].

Actually, let me reconsider. The Class 8 @deva[उ] combines with the stem in a specific way.

After guṇa, we have @deva[कर्] + @deva[उ]. The @deva[अर्] + @deva[उ] needs resolution.

---

## 6.1.77 - Semivowel Sandhi

**Key Terms:** ik, yaṇ, aci

Before applying this, let's trace more carefully:

@deva[कर्] ends in @deva[र्] (consonant). The @deva[उ] follows. No vowel sandhi needed yet.

> @deva[अ] + @deva[कर्] + @deva[उ] + @deva[त्] = @deva[अकरुत्]?

But the actual form is @deva[अकरोत्]. The @deva[उ] → @deva[ओ]. Let's find the rule.

---

## 7.3.84 (continued) - Guṇa of the Class Affix

The @deva[उ] itself takes guṇa before the ending @deva[त्]!

@deva[त्] is @term[sārvadhātuka] (it's a @term[tiṅ] ending). So by @ref[7.3.84], the preceding @deva[उ] (which is the @term[aṅga]-final vowel) takes guṇa:

> @deva[उ] → @deva[ओ]

Now we have:

> @deva[अ] + @deva[कर्] + @deva[ओ] + @deva[त्] = @deva[अकरोत्]

---

## 8.4.56 - Final Consonant

**Key Terms:** avasāna

The final @deva[त्] remains as is (no further changes in this derivation).

| # | Rule | Operation |
|---|------|-----------|
| 1 | @ref[3.2.111] | Add @deva[लङ्] (imperfect marker) |
| 2 | @ref[3.4.100] | Select secondary ending @deva[त्] |
| 3 | @ref[6.4.71] | Add augment @deva[अ] at beginning |
| 4 | @ref[3.1.79] | Insert class affix @deva[उ] |
| 5 | @ref[7.3.84] | Guṇa of root: @deva[ऋ] → @deva[अर्] |
| 6 | @ref[7.3.84] | Guṇa of @deva[उ]: @deva[उ] → @deva[ओ] |
| 7 | @ref[1.3.9] | Delete @term[it] markers |

Final form: **@deva[अकरोत्]** — "he did, he made"

## concept - The Augment Pattern

The @deva[अ]-augment is consistent across past tenses:

| Tense | Root | With Augment | 3sg Form |
|-------|------|--------------|----------|
| Imperfect (@deva[लङ्]) | @deva[भू] | @deva[अ] + @deva[भू] | @deva[अभवत्] |
| Imperfect (@deva[लङ्]) | @deva[गम्] | @deva[अ] + @deva[गम्] | @deva[अगच्छत्] |
| Imperfect (@deva[लङ्]) | @deva[कृ] | @deva[अ] + @deva[कृ] | @deva[अकरोत्] |
| Aorist (@deva[लुङ्]) | @deva[भू] | @deva[अ] + @deva[भू] | @deva[अभूत्] |
| Conditional (@deva[लृङ्]) | @deva[भू] | @deva[अ] + @deva[भू] | @deva[अभविष्यत्] |

The augment attaches at the very beginning, even before prefixes in most cases.

---

## concept - Primary vs Secondary Endings

| Person | Primary (@deva[लट्]) | Secondary (@deva[लङ्]) |
|--------|---------------------|------------------------|
| 3sg | @deva[ति] | @deva[त्] |
| 3du | @deva[तः] | @deva[ताम्] |
| 3pl | @deva[अन्ति] | @deva[अन्] |
| 2sg | @deva[सि] | @deva[स्] |
| 1sg | @deva[मि] | @deva[अम्] |

The secondary endings are "shorter" — they lack the final vowel. This is why @deva[अकरोत्] ends in @deva[त्] while @deva[करोति] ends in @deva[ति].

## concept - The Indo-European Augment

The @deva[अ]-augment is inherited from Proto-Indo-European:

| Language | "he was going" |
|----------|----------------|
| Sanskrit | @deva[अगच्छत्] |
| Greek | ἔ-φερε (e-phere) |
| Armenian | e-ber |

The @deva[ए]/ἔ prefix marks past tense across the family. Pāṇini's @ref[6.4.71] encodes this ancient feature.

## concept - What's Next

Now you've seen the augment and secondary endings. Explore:

- **Aorist** (@deva[लुङ्]): Multiple formation types, same augment
- **Conditional** (@deva[लृङ्]): "Would have" — augment + future stem
- **Prohibitive** (@deva[माङ्योगे लुङ्]): @deva[मा] + aorist for negative commands

The past tense system is one of Sanskrit's most complex areas, with the augment as its consistent marker.
