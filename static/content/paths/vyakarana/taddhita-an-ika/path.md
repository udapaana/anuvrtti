## concept - Relational Derivation with @deva[अण्] and @deva[ठक्]

The suffixes @deva[अण्] (= @deva[अ] with @deva[वृद्धि]) and @deva[ठक्] (= @deva[इक]) are the primary @deva[तद्धित] affixes for expressing relationships — "belonging to X," "descended from X," "coming from X," or "relating to X."

**Core mechanism:** The base noun undergoes @deva[वृद्धि] of its first vowel, then receives the suffix.

| Type       | Base              | Suffix     | Result             | Meaning               |
| ---------- | ----------------- | ---------- | ------------------ | --------------------- |
| Patronymic | @deva[वसुदेव]     | @deva[अण्] | @deva[वासुदेवः]    | son of Vasudeva       |
| Patronymic | @deva[दक्ष]       | @deva[इञ्] | @deva[दाक्षिः]     | descendant of Daksha  |
| Patronymic | @deva[गर्ग]       | @deva[यञ्] | @deva[गार्ग्यः]    | of the Garga lineage  |
| Place      | @deva[मथुरा]      | @deva[अण्] | @deva[माथुरः]      | of Mathura            |
| Place      | @deva[पाटलिपुत्र] | @deva[अण्] | @deva[पाटलिपुत्रः] | of Pātaliputra        |
| Relation   | @deva[वेद]        | @deva[ठक्] | @deva[वैदिकः]      | relating to the Vedas |
| Relation   | @deva[नौ] (boat)  | @deva[ठक्] | @deva[नाविकः]      | boatman               |
| Relation   | @deva[इतिहास]     | @deva[ठक्] | @deva[ऐतिहासिकः]   | historical            |

Note how @deva[वृद्धि] of the first vowel is the hallmark: @deva[अ] → @deva[आ], @deva[इ] → @deva[ऐ], @deva[उ] → @deva[औ].

---

## @ref[4.1.83] - प्राग्दीव्यतोऽण्

**Key Terms:** @deva[अण्], @deva[तद्धित]

The affix @deva[अण्] governs the rules up to @ref[4.4.2]. This is an @deva[अधिकार] (governing rule) that establishes @deva[अण्] as the default relational suffix throughout the @deva[तद्धित] section.

@deva[अण्] has indicatory @deva[ण्], which triggers @deva[वृद्धि] of the first vowel (by @ref[7.2.117]). The realized form is just @deva[अ]:

- @deva[शिव] + @deva[अण्] = @deva[शैवः] "relating to Śiva"
- @deva[विष्णु] + @deva[अण्] = @deva[वैष्णवः] "relating to Viṣṇu"
- @deva[उपगु] + @deva[अण्] = @deva[औपगवः] "descendant of Upagu"

## @ref[4.1.92] - तस्यापत्यम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[अपत्य], @deva[अण्]

The affix @deva[अण्] and those following denote "the descendant of someone" (@deva[अपत्य]).

This is one of the most important @deva[तद्धित] functions: creating **patronymics** (family names, lineage markers).

**Examples:**

- @deva[उपगोः अपत्यम्] = @deva[औपगवः] "descendant of Upagu"
- @deva[वसुदेवस्य अपत्यम्] = @deva[वासुदेवः] "son of Vasudeva" (= Kṛṣṇa)
- @deva[द्रुपदस्य अपत्यम्] = @deva[द्रौपदी] "daughter of Drupada"
- @deva[कुन्तीपुत्रस्य अपत्यम्] = @deva[कौन्तेयः] "son of Kuntī" (= Arjuna)
- @deva[पाण्डोः अपत्यम्] = @deva[पाण्डवः] "son of Pāṇḍu"

---

## @ref[7.2.117] - तद्धितेष्वचामादेः

**Key Terms:** @deva[तद्धित], @deva[वृद्धि]

@deva[वृद्धि] substitutes for the **first vowel** of the stem when a @deva[तद्धित] affix with indicatory @deva[ञ्] or @deva[ण्] follows.

This is the mechanical heart of the @deva[अण्] derivation:

| Base          | First vowel         | @deva[वृद्धि] | Result                       |
| ------------- | ------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------- |
| @deva[शिव]    | @deva[इ] → @deva[ऐ] | @deva[शैव]    | + @deva[अ] = @deva[शैवः]     |
| @deva[गर्ग]   | @deva[अ] → @deva[आ] | @deva[गार्ग]  | + @deva[य] = @deva[गार्ग्यः] |
| @deva[दक्ष]   | @deva[अ] → @deva[आ] | @deva[दाक्ष]  | + @deva[इ] = @deva[दाक्षिः]  |
| @deva[विष्णु] | @deva[इ] → @deva[ऐ] | @deva[वैष्णु] | + @deva[अ] = @deva[वैष्णवः]  |
| @deva[सूर्य]  | @deva[ऊ] → @deva[औ] | @deva[सौर्य]  | = @deva[सौरः]                |
| @deva[बुद्ध]  | @deva[उ] → @deva[औ] | @deva[बौद्ध]  | + @deva[अ] = @deva[बौद्धः]   |

Note: Only the **first** vowel undergoes @deva[वृद्धि], not all vowels in the stem.

## @ref[4.1.95] - अत इञ्

**Key Terms:** @deva[अत्], @deva[इञ्]

The affix @deva[इञ्] (= @deva[इ]) comes after stems ending in short @deva[अ] to denote a descendant. This **debars** @deva[अण्] for these stems.

@deva[इञ्] also has indicatory @deva[ञ्], so @deva[वृद्धि] still applies:

- @deva[दक्षस्य अपत्यम्] = @deva[दाक्षिः] "descendant of Daksha"
- @deva[अत्रेः अपत्यम्] = @deva[आत्रेयः] "descendant of Atri"

Multiple patronymic suffixes exist; which one applies depends on the stem ending and specific exceptions.

---

## @ref[4.3.120] - तस्येदम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[इदम्], @deva[अण्]

After a word in the Genitive case, an affix comes in the sense of "this belongs to him" or "this relates to him."

This covers **possessive/relational** (not patronymic) derivatives:

- @deva[उपगोः इदम्] = @deva[औपगवम्] "belonging to Upagu"
- @deva[शिवस्य इदम्] = @deva[शैवम्] "of/relating to Śiva"
- @deva[राज्ञः इदम्] = @deva[राजकीयम्] "royal, of the king"

---

## @ref[4.3.53] - तत्र भवः

**Key Terms:** @deva[भव], @deva[ठक्]

An affix comes after a word in the Locative case in the sense of "who stays/originates there." This produces **place-of-origin derivatives**.

**Examples:**

- @deva[स्रुघ्ने भवः] = @deva[स्रौघ्नः] "one from Srughna"
- @deva[मथुरायां भवः] = @deva[माथुरः] "one from Mathura"
- @deva[पाटलिपुत्रे भवः] = @deva[पाटलिपुत्रकः] "one from Pātaliputra"
- @deva[मगधेषु भवः] = @deva[मागधः] "one from Magadha"

---

## @ref[6.4.148] - यस्येति च

**Key Terms:** @deva[य], @deva[अण्]

Final @deva[अ] of a stem is deleted before @deva[अण्] and similar vowel-initial suffixes. This is how the stem and suffix combine smoothly:

- @deva[शिव] + @deva[अ] → @deva[शिव्] + @deva[अ] → @deva[शैवः] (after @deva[वृद्धि])
- @deva[विष्णु] + @deva[अ] → @deva[वैष्णवः]

---

## @ref[4.2.80] - वुञ्छण्कठजिलसेनिरढञ्...

**Key Terms:** @deva[ठक्], @deva[वुञ्]

Various suffixes like @deva[ठक्] (= @deva[इक]) create professional, topical, and relational terms. @deva[ठक्] is especially productive for creating adjectives meaning "relating to X":

| Base             | + @deva[ठक्]      | Meaning                       |
| ---------------- | ----------------- | ----------------------------- |
| @deva[नौ] (boat) | @deva[नाविकः]     | boatman, sailor               |
| @deva[वेद]       | @deva[वैदिकः]     | Vedic, relating to the Vedas  |
| @deva[इतिहास]    | @deva[ऐतिहासिकः]  | historical                    |
| @deva[व्याकरण]   | @deva[वैयाकरणः]   | grammarian                    |
| @deva[धर्म]      | @deva[धार्मिकः]   | righteous, relating to dharma |
| @deva[शास्त्र]   | @deva[शास्त्रीयः] | scientific, śāstric           |
| @deva[लोक]       | @deva[लौकिकः]     | worldly, popular              |
| @deva[पौराण]     | @deva[पौराणिकः]   | puranic, relating to Purāṇas  |

Many of these derivatives survive as common words in modern Indian languages.

## concept - Recognizing @deva[तद्धित] Derivatives

When reading Sanskrit, you can identify @deva[अण्] / @deva[ठक्] derivatives by:

1. **@deva[वृद्धि] in the first syllable** — a long vowel (@deva[आ], @deva[ऐ], @deva[औ]) where you'd expect a short one
2. **Ending in @deva[-अ] or @deva[-इक]** — the realized suffix

**Practice — identify the base:**

| Derivative      | Base          | Suffix     | Meaning              |
| --------------- | ------------- | ---------- | -------------------- |
| @deva[कौन्तेयः] | @deva[कुन्ती] | @deva[ढक्] | son of Kuntī         |
| @deva[वासुदेवः] | @deva[वसुदेव] | @deva[अण्] | son of Vasudeva      |
| @deva[पाण्डवः]  | @deva[पाण्डु] | @deva[अण्] | son of Pāṇḍu         |
| @deva[गार्ग्यः] | @deva[गर्ग]   | @deva[यञ्] | of the Garga lineage |
| @deva[वैदिकः]   | @deva[वेद]    | @deva[ठक्] | Vedic                |
| @deva[बौद्धः]   | @deva[बुद्ध]  | @deva[अण्] | Buddhist             |
| @deva[शैवः]     | @deva[शिव]    | @deva[अण्] | Śaiva                |
| @deva[वैष्णवः]  | @deva[विष्णु] | @deva[अण्] | Vaiṣṇava             |
| @deva[धार्मिकः] | @deva[धर्म]   | @deva[ठक्] | righteous            |
