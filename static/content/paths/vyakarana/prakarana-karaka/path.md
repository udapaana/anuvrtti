## concept - The Bridge Between Meaning and Form

How does Sanskrit know that @deva[रामेण] means "by Rāma" while @deva[रामाय] means "to Rāma"?

The answer lies in the @term[kāraka] system — Pāṇini's semantic role theory. @term[kāraka] (from @deva[कृ] "to do") literally means "that which brings about the action." It's the conceptual layer between meaning and grammatical form.

Rules 1.4.23–55 define six @term[kāraka] roles. These rules don't directly assign case endings — they identify semantic participants. Other rules (2.3.x) then map @term[kāraka] roles to @term[vibhakti] (case endings).

---

## concept - The Six Kārakas

| @term[kāraka]     | Semantic Role  | Primary Case           |
| ----------------- | -------------- | ---------------------- |
| @term[kartṛ]      | Agent          | @term[prathamā] (nom.) |
| @term[karman]     | Patient/Object | @term[dvitīyā] (acc.)  |
| @term[karaṇa]     | Instrument     | @term[tṛtīyā] (inst.)  |
| @term[sampradāna] | Recipient      | @term[caturthī] (dat.) |
| @term[apādāna]    | Source         | @term[pañcamī] (abl.)  |
| @term[adhikaraṇa] | Locus          | @term[saptamī] (loc.)  |

Note: @term[sambandha] (genitive relation) is NOT a @term[kāraka] — it expresses possession and other relations outside the action structure.

---

## 1.4.23 - @deva[कारके]

**Key Terms:** kāraka, adhikāra

@deva[कारके] — [The following rules apply] in the domain of @term[kāraka].

@sutra[1.4.23]

This @term[adhikāra] (governing rule) sets up the section. Everything from here through 1.4.55 defines @term[kāraka] roles.

## concept - Apādāna: The Source

The first @term[kāraka] defined is @term[apādāna] — the fixed point from which separation occurs.

---

## 1.4.24 - @deva[ध्रुवमपायेऽपादानम्]

**Key Terms:** dhruva, apāya, apādāna

@deva[ध्रुवम् अपाये अपादानम्] — The fixed point (@deva[ध्रुव]) when there is separation (@deva[अपाय]) is @term[apādāna].

@sutra[1.4.24]

This is the core definition. @term[apādāna] is the SOURCE from which something moves away:

@deva[ग्रामाद् आगच्छति] "comes FROM the village"
@deva[वृक्षात् पत्रं पतति] "a leaf falls FROM the tree"

The village and tree are "fixed" — they don't move. The coming/falling entity separates from them.

---

## 1.4.25 - @deva[भीत्रार्थानां भयहेतुः]

**Key Terms:** bhī, trā, bhayahetu, apādāna

@deva[भीत्रार्थानां भयहेतुः] — For verbs meaning "fear" or "protect," the cause of fear is @term[apādāna].

@sutra[1.4.25]

Extended use of @term[apādāna]:

@deva[व्याघ्राद् बिभेति] "fears FROM the tiger"
@deva[चोराद् रक्षति] "protects FROM the thief"

There's no physical separation, but the tiger/thief is conceptualized as a source (of danger) from which one moves away (psychologically or protectively).

---

## 1.4.26 - @deva[पराजेरसोढः]

**Key Terms:** parāji, asah, apādāna

@deva[पराजेः असोढः] — For @deva[पराजि] "be defeated," the unbearable one is @term[apādāna].

@sutra[1.4.26]

@deva[राज्ञः पराजयते] "is defeated BY the king"

The victor is the source from which defeat "comes."

---

## 1.4.27 - @deva[वारणार्थानामीप्सितः]

**Key Terms:** vāraṇa, īpsita, apādāna

@deva[वारणार्थानाम् ईप्सितः] — For verbs meaning "prevent," the desired thing is @term[apādāna].

@sutra[1.4.27]

@deva[ग्रामाद् गां वारयति] "prevents the cow FROM the village"

The village (what the cow wants to reach) is the @term[apādāna] — the cow is being separated from its desired goal.

---

## 1.4.30 - @deva[जनिकर्तुः प्रकृतिः]

**Key Terms:** jan, kartṛ, prakṛti, apādāna

@deva[जनिकर्तुः प्रकृतिः] — The material cause (@deva[प्रकृति]) of what is born is @term[apādāna].

@sutra[1.4.30]

@deva[मृदः घटो जायते] "a pot is born FROM clay"

The clay is the source-material from which the pot emerges.

---

## concept - Sampradāna: The Recipient

---

## 1.4.32 - @deva[कर्मणा यमभिप्रैति स सम्प्रदानम्]

**Key Terms:** karman, abhipraiti, sampradāna

@deva[कर्मणा यम् अभिप्रैति सः सम्प्रदानम्] — The one whom [the agent] approaches through the @term[karman] is @term[sampradāna].

@sutra[1.4.32]

This is the core definition. @term[sampradāna] is the RECIPIENT — where the object is headed:

@deva[विप्राय गां ददाति] "gives a cow TO the brahmin"

The cow (object) moves toward the brahmin (recipient). The brahmin is @term[sampradāna].

---

## 1.4.33 - @deva[रुच्यर्थानां प्रीयमाणः]

**Key Terms:** ruc, prī, sampradāna

@deva[रुच्यर्थानां प्रीयमाणः] — For verbs meaning "please," the pleased one is @term[sampradāna].

@sutra[1.4.33]

@deva[रामाय रोचते] "is pleasing TO Rāma"
@deva[मह्यं प्रीणाति] "gives pleasure TO me"

The experiencer of pleasure is @term[sampradāna].

---

## 1.4.34 - @deva[श्लाघह्नुङ्स्थाशपां ज्ञीप्स्यमानः]

**Key Terms:** ślāgh, hnuṅ, sthā, śap, sampradāna

@deva[श्लाघह्नुङ्स्थाशपां ज्ञीप्स्यमानः] — For "boast/conceal/promise/curse," the one intended to know is @term[sampradāna].

@sutra[1.4.34]

- @deva[तस्मै श्लाघते] "boasts TO him"
- @deva[तस्मै शपते] "curses/swears TO him"

The target audience of the speech act is @term[sampradāna].

---

## 1.4.37 - @deva[क्रुधद्रुहेर्ष्याऽसूयार्थानां यं प्रति कोपः]

**Key Terms:** krudh, druh, īrṣ, asūy, sampradāna

@deva[क्रुधद्रुहेर्ष्याऽसूयार्थानां यं प्रति कोपः] — For verbs meaning "be angry/harm/envy/blame," the target of the emotion is @term[sampradāna].

@sutra[1.4.37]

@deva[रामाय क्रुध्यति] "is angry AT Rāma"
@deva[तस्मै द्रुह्यति] "bears malice TOWARD him"

The object of negative emotion is @term[sampradāna] (dative), not @term[karman] (accusative).

---

## concept - Karaṇa: The Instrument

---

## 1.4.42 - @deva[साधकतमं करणम्]

**Key Terms:** sādhaka, karaṇa

@deva[साधकतमम् करणम्] — The most effective means is @term[karaṇa].

@sutra[1.4.42]

This elegant definition: @term[karaṇa] is whatever is MOST instrumental in accomplishing the action.

@deva[कुठारेण वृक्षं छिनत्ति] "cuts the tree WITH an axe"
@deva[चक्षुषा पश्यति] "sees WITH the eyes"

The axe and eyes are the primary instruments — the @term[sādhakatama] ("most accomplishing") factors.

---

## 1.4.43 - @deva[दिवः कर्म च]

**Key Terms:** div, karaṇa, karman

@deva[दिवः कर्म च] — For @deva[दिव्] "play," [the stake] is also @term[karman].

@sutra[1.4.43]

@deva[अक्षैर् दीव्यति] "plays WITH dice"

Dice can be @term[karaṇa] (instrumental) OR @term[karman] (accusative) — both are valid.

---

## concept - Adhikaraṇa: The Locus

---

## 1.4.45 - @deva[आधारोऽधिकरणम्]

**Key Terms:** ādhāra, adhikaraṇa

@deva[आधारः अधिकरणम्] — The container/locus is @term[adhikaraṇa].

@sutra[1.4.45]

@term[adhikaraṇa] is where the action takes place:

@deva[कटे आस्ते] "sits ON the mat"
@deva[ग्रामे वसति] "lives IN the village"

The mat and village are @term[ādhāra] — they "hold" or contain the action.

---

## concept - Karman: The Object

---

## 1.4.49 - @deva[कर्तुरीप्सिततमं कर्म]

**Key Terms:** kartṛ, īpsitatama, karman

@deva[कर्तुः ईप्सिततमम् कर्म] — What the agent most desires [to affect] is @term[karman].

@sutra[1.4.49]

This is the primary definition. @term[karman] is the PATIENT — what the action is primarily directed at:

@deva[ओदनं पचति] "cooks rice"
@deva[ग्रामं गच्छति] "goes TO the village"

Rice and village are what the agent most wants to reach/affect.

---

## 1.4.50 - @deva[तथायुक्तं चानीप्सितम्]

**Key Terms:** tathāyukta, anīpsita, karman

@deva[तथायुक्तम् च अनीप्सितम्] — Also what is similarly engaged, even if undesired, is @term[karman].

@sutra[1.4.50]

@deva[विषं खादति] "eats poison" (unintentionally)

Even if not desired, if something undergoes the action the same way, it's @term[karman].

---

## 1.4.51 - @deva[अकथितं च]

**Key Terms:** akathita, karman

@deva[अकथितम् च] — Also what is not expressed by another @term[kāraka] is @term[karman].

@sutra[1.4.51]

This catches secondary objects in double-accusative constructions:

@deva[गां दोग्धि पयः] "milks the cow [of] milk"

Both "cow" and "milk" are @term[karman] — the milk is @term[akathita] (not expressed by any other role).

---

## 1.4.52 - @deva[गतिबुद्धिप्रत्यवसानार्थशब्दकर्माकर्मकाणामणि कर्ता स णौ]

**Key Terms:** ṇi, kartṛ, karman

@deva[गतिबुद्धिप्रत्यवसानार्थशब्दकर्माकर्मकाणाम् अणि कर्ता सः णौ] — For verbs of motion/cognition/eating/speaking/intransitives, the non-causative agent becomes @term[karman] in the causative.

@sutra[1.4.52]

In causatives, the original agent gets "demoted":

@deva[देवदत्तः गच्छति] → @deva[देवदत्तं गमयति] "makes Devadatta go"

Devadatta was @term[kartṛ] (agent); in the causative, he becomes @term[karman] (object).

---

## concept - Kartṛ: The Agent

---

## 1.4.54 - @deva[स्वतन्त्रः कर्ता]

**Key Terms:** svatantra, kartṛ

@deva[स्वतन्त्रः कर्ता] — The independent one is @term[kartṛ].

@sutra[1.4.54]

This is the definition of agency. @term[kartṛ] is whoever acts independently, with their own will:

@deva[रामः पचति] "Rāma cooks"

Rāma has autonomous control — he's @term[svatantra] ("self-powered").

---

## 1.4.55 - @deva[तत्प्रयोजको हेतुश्च]

**Key Terms:** prayojaka, hetu, kartṛ

@deva[तत्प्रयोजकः हेतुः च] — The instigator is also [kartṛ], and is called @term[hetu].

@sutra[1.4.55]

In causatives, there are two "agents":

- The instigator (@term[hetu]//@term[prayojaka]) — true @term[kartṛ]
- The original agent — now @term[karman] (by 1.4.52)

@deva[यज्ञदत्तः देवदत्तं पाचयति] "Yajñadatta makes Devadatta cook"

Yajñadatta is @term[hetu] (causative agent); Devadatta is @term[karman] (caused agent).

## concept - The Complete Kāraka Architecture

| Rule   | @term[kāraka]     | Core Definition           |
| ------ | ----------------- | ------------------------- |
| 1.4.24 | @term[apādāna]    | Fixed point in separation |
| 1.4.32 | @term[sampradāna] | Recipient of the object   |
| 1.4.42 | @term[karaṇa]     | Most effective means      |
| 1.4.45 | @term[adhikaraṇa] | Container/locus           |
| 1.4.49 | @term[karman]     | Most desired to affect    |
| 1.4.54 | @term[kartṛ]      | Independent agent         |

These six roles capture the semantic structure of any action. The @term[kāraka] system is Pāṇini's contribution to universal semantic theory.

---

## concept - From Kāraka to Vibhakti

@term[kāraka] roles don't directly equal cases. Rules in 2.3 map them:

| @term[kāraka]     | Default Case    | But also...               |
| ----------------- | --------------- | ------------------------- |
| @term[kartṛ]      | @term[prathamā] | @term[tṛtīyā] (passive)   |
| @term[karman]     | @term[dvitīyā]  | @term[prathamā] (passive) |
| @term[karaṇa]     | @term[tṛtīyā]   |                           |
| @term[sampradāna] | @term[caturthī] |                           |
| @term[apādāna]    | @term[pañcamī]  |                           |
| @term[adhikaraṇa] | @term[saptamī]  |                           |

The @term[kāraka] is the meaning; the @term[vibhakti] is the form. The mapping isn't always one-to-one.

---

## concept - See It in Action

@term[kāraka] assignments in real sentences:

**@deva[रामो वनं गच्छति]** "Rāma goes to the forest"

- @deva[रामः] = @term[kartṛ] (agent) → @term[prathamā]
- @deva[वनम्] = @term[karman] (goal) → @term[dvitīyā]

**@deva[रामेण बाणेन रावणो हतः]** "Rāvaṇa was killed by Rāma with an arrow"

- @deva[रामेण] = @term[kartṛ] (agent in passive) → @term[tṛtīyā]
- @deva[बाणेन] = @term[karaṇa] (instrument) → @term[tṛtīyā]
- @deva[रावणः] = @term[karman] (patient in passive) → @term[prathamā]

The @term[kāraka] roles explain WHY the cases are chosen.

## quiz - Check Your Understanding

**Question:** In @deva[विप्राय गां ददाति] "he gives a cow to the brahmin," which @term[kāraka] is @deva[विप्राय]?

**Options:**

- [ ] @term[karman] — the object of the action
- [ ] @term[karaṇa] — the instrument
- [x] @term[sampradāna] — the recipient
- [ ] @term[apādāna] — the source

**Explanation:** The brahmin (@deva[विप्र]) is the recipient toward whom the cow moves. By @ref[1.4.32], the one whom the agent approaches through the @term[karman] is @term[sampradāna]. This takes @term[caturthī] (dative), giving @deva[विप्राय].

## quiz - Identify the Kārakas

**Question:** In @deva[कुठारेण वृक्षं छिनत्ति] "he cuts the tree with an axe," which @term[kāraka] is @deva[कुठारेण]?

**Options:**

- [ ] @term[kartṛ] — the agent
- [x] @term[karaṇa] — the instrument
- [ ] @term[karman] — the object
- [ ] @term[adhikaraṇa] — the locus

**Explanation:** The axe is the most effective means (@term[sādhakatama]) for accomplishing the cutting. By @ref[1.4.42], this makes it @term[karaṇa], which takes @term[tṛtīyā] (instrumental).
