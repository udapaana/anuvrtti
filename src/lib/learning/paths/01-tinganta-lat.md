## concept - What is a lakāra?

Sanskrit grammar does not say "add the ending -ti for 'he does.'" Instead, it works in two stages:

1. **Stage 1 — Prescribe a lakāra:** A rule says "use लट्" (for present action). The lakāra is an abstract placeholder — it is never pronounced.
2. **Stage 2 — Replace it with a personal ending:** Later rules replace लट् with the actual endings like -ति, -तः, -न्ति.

This two-stage system is why Pāṇini's grammar is so powerful: the same set of rules for replacing the lakāra applies across all ten tenses and moods.

**The ten lakāras:**

| Lakāra | Tense / Mood |
|--------|-------------|
| @deva[लट्] | Present (ongoing/habitual action) |
| @deva[लिट्] | Perfect (distant past, not witnessed) |
| @deva[लुट्] | Periphrastic future ("he will certainly do") |
| @deva[लृट्] | Simple future ("he will do") |
| @deva[लोट्] | Imperative ("do it!") |
| @deva[लेट्] | Vedic subjunctive (rare in classical texts) |
| @deva[लङ्] | Imperfect (recent past, or narrative) |
| @deva[लिङ्] | Optative/Benedictive ("he should/may do") |
| @deva[लुङ्] | Aorist (past, uninflected aspect) |
| @deva[लृङ्] | Conditional ("he would have done") |

This path covers @deva[लट्] — the present tense, and the starting point for all Sanskrit verb study.

**Key Terms:** @[lakāra], @deva[लट्], @deva[वर्तमान]

## 3.2.123 - Vartamāne laṭ

**Key Terms:** @deva[वर्तमान], @[lakāra], @[present tense]

@deva[वर्तमाने लट्] — Use @deva[लट्] for a present action.

@deva[वर्तमान] means "ongoing" — an action that has begun but not yet finished. This covers two sub-cases:

**Ongoing action** (something happening right now):
- @deva[पचति] — "he is cooking"
- @deva[पठति] — "he is reading"

**Constant/habitual state** (something always true, or a regular occurrence):
- @deva[तिष्ठन्ति पर्वताः] — "the mountains stand" (they always do)
- @deva[स्रवन्ति नद्यः] — "the rivers flow" (habitually)

After this rule applies, the abstract @deva[लट्] marker must be replaced by an actual personal ending. That replacement is what the next rules govern.

## 3.4.77 - Lasya (the governing rule)

**Key Terms:** @[adhikāra], @deva[लस्य]

@deva[लस्य] — "In place of ल."

This is an @[adhikāra] (governing rule): it sets up the heading "in the following sūtras, affixes will substitute for @deva[ल]." The @deva[ल] refers to all ten lakāras — @deva[लट्], @deva[लिट्], @deva[लुट्], etc.

What comes next (3.4.78) is the list of those substitutes.

## concept - The 18 personal endings

Before the sūtra lists them, here is the full picture.

All 18 endings fall into two groups by **voice**, then into three groups by **person**, then into three by **number**.

**Two voices:**
- @deva[परस्मैपद] (parasmaipada) — "active": used when the action benefits another, or in neutral description
- @deva[आत्मनेपद] (ātmanepada) — "middle": used when the action benefits the doer, or for inherently reflexive verbs

**Three persons** (Pāṇini's naming is the reverse of Western grammar):
- @deva[प्रथम] (first) = 3rd person in English (he / she / it / they)
- @deva[मध्यम] (middle) = 2nd person (you)
- @deva[उत्तम] (highest) = 1st person (I / we)

**Three numbers:** @deva[एकवचन] (singular), @deva[द्विवचन] (dual — exactly two), @deva[बहुवचन] (plural — three or more)

**Parasmaipada endings** (the raw forms Pāṇini lists, then their surface forms):

| | @deva[एकवचन] | @deva[द्विवचन] | @deva[बहुवचन] |
|--|--|--|--|
| @deva[प्रथम] (3rd) | @deva[तिप्] → @deva[-ति] | @deva[तस्] → @deva[-तः] | @deva[झि] → @deva[-न्ति] |
| @deva[मध्यम] (2nd) | @deva[सिप्] → @deva[-सि] | @deva[थस्] → @deva[-थः] | @deva[थ] → @deva[-थ] |
| @deva[उत्तम] (1st) | @deva[मिप्] → @deva[-मि] | @deva[वस्] → @deva[-वः] | @deva[मस्] → @deva[-मः] |

The raw forms (तिप्, मिप्, etc.) have marker letters (anubandhas) that trigger rules then disappear. The surface forms in the right column are what you see in actual Sanskrit.

For most beginner texts, parasmaipada forms are what you need. We will focus on these throughout this path.

**Key Terms:** @deva[परस्मैपद], @deva[आत्मनेपद], @deva[प्रथम], @deva[मध्यम], @deva[उत्तम], @deva[एकवचन], @deva[द्विवचन], @deva[बहुवचन]

## 3.4.78 - Tiptasjhi... (the 18 tiṅ endings)

**Key Terms:** @deva[तिङ्], @[tiṅ], @[anubandha]

The full sūtra is a single long word listing all 18 endings:

@deva[तिप् तस् झि सिप् थस् थ मिप् वस् मस् — त आताम् झ थास् आथाम् ध्वम् इट् वहि महिङ्]

(Parasmaipada first, then ātmanepada.)

These 18 endings collectively form the @[pratyāhāra] @deva[तिङ्] — named from the first (@deva[ति]) and last (@deva[ङ्]) elements. You will see the term @deva[तिङ्] used as shorthand for "any personal verbal ending."

**Marker letters (anubandhas) in the raw endings:**
- The @deva[प्] in @deva[तिप्], @deva[सिप्], @deva[मिप्] marks accent (then drops)
- The @deva[ट्] in @deva[इट्] triggers a special rule for the 1st singular ātmanepada (then drops)
- The @deva[ङ्] in @deva[महिङ्] is what closes the @deva[तिङ्] pratyāhāra (then drops)

After these markers drop, what remains is the ending you actually pronounce.

## 1.4.99 + 1.4.100 - The two voices

**Key Terms:** @deva[परस्मैपद], @deva[आत्मनेपद], @[voice]

Two rules name the two groups:

**@ref[1.4.99]:** The substitutes for @deva[ल] (@deva[तिप्] through @deva[मस्]) are called @deva[परस्मैपद].

**@ref[1.4.100]:** The @deva[तङ्] endings (@deva[त] through @deva[महिङ्]) plus @deva[शानच्] and @deva[कानच्] (participial affixes) are called @deva[आत्मनेपद].

**Which voice to use?** This is determined by:
1. The root itself — some roots are fixed as parasmaipada-only (@[parasmaipadin]), some ātmanepada-only (@[ātmanepadinā]), some can take either (@[ubhayapadinā])
2. Voice/construction — passive always takes ātmanepada
3. A cascade of specific rules (@ref[1.3.12]–@ref[1.3.77]) for special cases

The default rule — covering most roots you will encounter — is @ref[1.3.78]:

> @deva[शेषात् कर्तरि परस्मैपदम्] — For all remaining roots (not specified by earlier rules), use parasmaipada in active voice.

In practice: verbs like @deva[पच्], @deva[गम्], @deva[पठ्], @deva[वद्], @deva[नी], @deva[भू] all take parasmaipada in basic active sentences. You will use this the vast majority of the time.

**What ātmanepada looks like** (so you recognise it in texts):
- @deva[लभते] — "he obtains" (root @deva[लभ्], inherently ātmanepada)
- @deva[मन्यते] — "he thinks" (root @deva[मन्], ātmanepada)
- @deva[वर्तते] — "it exists / proceeds" (root @deva[वृत्], ātmanepada)

The 3rd singular ātmanepada ending is @deva[-ते] (from raw @deva[त]) rather than @deva[-ति]. When you see @deva[-ते] on a verb, it signals either ātmanepada voice or passive construction.

## 1.4.101 + 1.4.102 - Person and number names

**Key Terms:** @deva[प्रथम], @deva[मध्यम], @deva[उत्तम], @deva[एकवचन], @deva[द्विवचन], @deva[बहुवचन]

**@ref[1.4.101]:** The @deva[तिङ्] endings are grouped in threes, named @deva[प्रथम], @deva[मध्यम], @deva[उत्तम].

**@ref[1.4.102]:** Within each person group, the three endings are @deva[एकवचन], @deva[द्विवचन], @deva[बहुवचन].

The result: 3 persons × 3 numbers = 9 forms per voice. With two voices: 18 total.

**Important:** Pāṇini's person-names are the reverse of Western grammar convention:

| Pāṇini | Western | Means |
|--------|---------|-------|
| @deva[प्रथम] (first) | 3rd person | he / she / it / they |
| @deva[मध्यम] (middle) | 2nd person | you |
| @deva[उत्तम] (highest) | 1st person | I / we |

This reversal is consistent throughout the tradition: when Pāṇini says "प्रथम," he means what English grammar calls 3rd person. Keep this in mind when reading Sanskrit grammatical texts.

## concept - Verb classes and vikaraṇas

Between the root and the ending, Sanskrit inserts a **class-marker** called a @[vikaraṇa]. The vikaraṇa signals which class (@[gaṇa]) the root belongs to.

There are ten classes. The class is a property of the root — you look it up in the @[Dhātupāṭha] (the list of roots). Here is the complete picture:

| Class | Name | Vikaraṇa (= what gets inserted) | Example |
|-------|------|--------------------------------|---------|
| 1 | @deva[भ्वादि] | @deva[शप्] = @deva[अ] | @deva[पचति], @deva[भवति] |
| 2 | @deva[अदादि] | (none) | @deva[अत्ति], @deva[दोग्धि] |
| 3 | @deva[जुहोत्यादि] | (root reduplicated) | @deva[जुहोति] |
| 4 | @deva[दिवादि] | @deva[श्यन्] = @deva[य] | @deva[दीव्यति], @deva[नश्यति] |
| 5 | @deva[स्वादि] | @deva[श्नु] = @deva[नु] | @deva[सुनोति], @deva[आप्नोति] |
| 6 | @deva[तुदादि] | @deva[श] = @deva[अ] (short, different from class 1) | @deva[तुदति], @deva[लिखति] |
| 7 | @deva[रुधादि] | @deva[श्नम्] = @deva[न] (infixed into root) | @deva[रुणद्धि] |
| 8 | @deva[तनादि] | @deva[उ] | @deva[तनोति], @deva[करोति] |
| 9 | @deva[क्र्यादि] | @deva[श्ना] = @deva[ना] | @deva[क्रीणाति], @deva[पुनाति] |
| 10 | @deva[चुरादि] | @deva[णिच्] + @deva[शप्] = @deva[अय] | @deva[चोरयति], @deva[गणयति] |

**For this path:** We focus on Class 1 (@deva[भ्वादि]). It is by far the largest class, containing hundreds of common roots. Its vikaraṇa is @deva[शप्], whose actual vowel is simply @deva[अ].

Recognizing the vikaraṇa in a form tells you the class:
- See @deva[-यति] (with the y from the vikaraṇa itself)? Likely Class 4.
- See @deva[-नोति] / @deva[-नुति]? Class 5.
- See @deva[-अति] with no other marker? Class 1 or Class 6.

**Key Terms:** @deva[गण], @[gaṇa], @[vikaraṇa], @deva[विकरण], @deva[भ्वादि], @[Dhātupāṭha]

## 3.1.68 - Kartari śap

**Key Terms:** @deva[शप्], @[śap], @deva[विकरण], @deva[भ्वादि], @deva[सार्वधातुक]

@deva[कर्तरि शप्] — Insert @deva[शप्] after a root when a @deva[सार्वधातुक] ending follows in active voice.

**What is @deva[शप्]?** The actual inserted vowel is @deva[अ] — the श् and प् are marker letters that trigger other rules (@ref[3.4.113], @ref[3.1.4]) then disappear. @deva[शप्] is the vikaraṇa for Class 1 (bhvādi) roots.

**What is @deva[सार्वधातुक]?** By @ref[3.4.113]: the @deva[तिङ्] personal endings and any affix marked with श् are called @deva[सार्वधातुक] ("pertaining to the whole verb"). The present-tense endings (-ति, -तः, etc.) are all @deva[सार्वधातुक].

**Full derivation of @deva[पचति] (he cooks):**

```
पच्                    root (Class 1 — no guṇa needed, consonant-final)
→ पच् + लट्           (3.2.123 — present action)
→ पच् + तिप्          (3.4.78 — tiṅ replaces l; 3.4.77)
→ पच् + शप् + तिप्    (3.1.68 — śap before sārvadhātuka)
→ पच् + अ + ति        (marker letters drop: 1.3.9)
→ पचति                (the stem-final अ and the ति merge: no sandhi change here — the त् is a consonant)
```

Root @deva[पच्] ends in a consonant. No vowel-change is needed. The derivation is: root + @deva[अ] (from śap) + ending.

**Full derivation of @deva[भवति] (he is / becomes):**

Root @deva[भू] ends in a long vowel @deva[ू], an @deva[इक्] vowel. Before a @deva[सार्वधातुक] ending, this strengthens (guṇa, by @ref[7.3.84]):

```
भू                     root (Class 1)
→ भू + लट्
→ भू + तिप्
→ भू + शप् + तिप्      (3.1.68)
→ भू + अ + ति          (markers drop)
→ भो + अ + ति          (7.3.84 — guṇa: ū → o)
→ भव् + अ + ति         (6.1.78 — o before vowel → av)
→ भवति                 (भव् ends in consonant व्; the अ + ति join directly)
```

**What to notice:** The root @deva[भू] transforms its vowel. The sequence ū → o → av is guṇa followed by the semi-vowel sandhi rule. The stem @deva[भव-] that appears in all present-tense forms of this root is the result of this transformation.

## 7.3.84 - Sārvadhātukārdhadhātukayoḥ (the guṇa rule)

**Key Terms:** @deva[गुण], @[guṇa], @deva[इक्], @deva[सार्वधातुक], @deva[आर्धधातुक]

@deva[सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः] — Before @deva[सार्वधातुक] or @deva[आर्धधातुक] affixes, the root-final @deva[इक्] vowel undergoes @deva[गुण].

**@deva[इक्] vowels** (by @ref[1.1.3]) are: @deva[इ], @deva[ई], @deva[उ], @deva[ऊ], @deva[ऋ], @deva[ॠ], @deva[ऌ] — the "short-grade" vowels.

**@deva[गुण] strengthening** (by @ref[1.1.2]): each इक् vowel rises one grade:

| Root vowel | After guṇa |
|------------|-----------|
| @deva[इ] or @deva[ई] | @deva[ए] |
| @deva[उ] or @deva[ऊ] | @deva[ओ] |
| @deva[ऋ] or @deva[ॠ] | @deva[अर्] |
| @deva[ऌ] | @deva[अल्] |

**Critical point:** Guṇa only applies to the **root-final** इक् vowel. If the root ends in a consonant (like @deva[पच्], @deva[लिख्], @deva[वद्]), there is no guṇa — the vowel inside the root is untouched.

**Three full derivations:**

**@deva[नयति] (he leads) — root @deva[नी], Class 1:**
```
नी + शप् + ति
→ नी + अ + ति
→ ने + अ + ति          (7.3.84 — guṇa: ī → e)
→ नय् + अ + ति         (6.1.78 — e before vowel → ay)
→ नयति                 (नय् + अ + ति: the अ joins with the consonant-initial ति — no further sandhi)
```

**@deva[भवति] (he becomes) — root @deva[भू], Class 1:** (recap)
```
भू + अ + ति
→ भो + अ + ति          (7.3.84 — guṇa: ū → o)
→ भव् + अ + ति         (6.1.78 — o before vowel → av)
→ भवति                 (व् is consonant-final; अ + ति join directly)
```

**@deva[तरति] (he crosses) — root @deva[तॄ], Class 1:**
```
तॄ + शप् + ति
→ तॄ + अ + ति
→ तर् + अ + ति         (7.3.84 — guṇa: ṝ → ar)
→ तरति                 (तर् ends in consonant र्; concatenation with अति gives तरति directly)
```

**No guṇa case — @deva[जीवति] (he lives) — root @deva[जीव्], Class 1:**

Root @deva[जीव्] ends in the consonant @deva[व्]. The vowel @deva[ई] is internal, not root-final. Guṇa does not apply:
```
जीव् + अ + ति → जीवति  (simple insertion, no vowel change)
```

## 6.1.77 - Iko yaṇ aci (brief)

**Key Terms:** @deva[इक्], @deva[यण्], @[yaṇ-sandhi]

@deva[इको यणचि] — @deva[इक्] vowels become semivowels before a following (dissimilar) vowel: @deva[इ/ई → य्], @deva[उ/ऊ → व्], @deva[ऋ/ॠ → र्], @deva[ऌ → ल्].

This is the general @[yaṇ-sandhi] rule. In the verb derivations above, the transformations @deva[ओ → अव्] and @deva[ए → अय्] before a vowel come from the closely related rule @ref[6.1.78] (@deva[एचोऽयवायावः]):

> @deva[ए, ओ, ऐ, औ] → their corresponding semivowel + @deva[अ] before a following vowel:
> @deva[ए → अय्], @deva[ओ → अव्], @deva[ऐ → आय्], @deva[औ → आव्]

So in @deva[भवति]: @deva[भो] + @deva[अ] → @deva[भव्] + @deva[अ] (by 6.1.78). In @deva[नयति]: @deva[ने] + @deva[अ] → @deva[नय्] + @deva[अ] (by 6.1.78).

The present rule 6.1.77 handles the simpler case where @deva[इक्] itself meets a vowel directly — seen in sandhi between words and in non-bhvādi verb forms:
- @deva[दधि + अत्र → दध्यत्र] (इ → य् before अ)
- @deva[मधु + अत्र → मध्वत्र] (उ → व् before अ)

## concept - Full present-tense paradigm

With all the rules in place, here is the complete parasmaipada paradigm for three contrasting Class 1 roots:

**How the stem is built:**
- @deva[पच्]: root + @deva[शप्] → @deva[पच] + ending (no guṇa — consonant-final root)
- @deva[नी]: root + guṇa + @deva[शप्] → @deva[ने] + @deva[अ] → @deva[नय] + ending (by 6.1.78)
- @deva[भू]: root + guṇa + @deva[शप्] → @deva[भो] + @deva[अ] → @deva[भव] + ending (by 6.1.78)

**Full paradigm — @deva[लट्] @deva[परस्मैपद]:**

| | @deva[एकवचन] | @deva[द्विवचन] | @deva[बहुवचन] |
|--|--|--|--|
| @deva[प्रथम] (3rd) | @deva[पचति] / @deva[नयति] / @deva[भवति] | @deva[पचतः] / @deva[नयतः] / @deva[भवतः] | @deva[पचन्ति] / @deva[नयन्ति] / @deva[भवन्ति] |
| @deva[मध्यम] (2nd) | @deva[पचसि] / @deva[नयसि] / @deva[भवसि] | @deva[पचथः] / @deva[नयथः] / @deva[भवथः] | @deva[पचथ] / @deva[नयथ] / @deva[भवथ] |
| @deva[उत्तम] (1st) | @deva[पचामि] / @deva[नयामि] / @deva[भवामि] | @deva[पचावः] / @deva[नयावः] / @deva[भवावः] | @deva[पचामः] / @deva[नयामः] / @deva[भवामः] |

**The endings are constant** — @deva[-ति], @deva[-तः], @deva[-न्ति], @deva[-सि], @deva[-थः], @deva[-थ], @deva[-मि], @deva[-वः], @deva[-मः]. Only the stem changes between roots.

**The @deva[आ] in 1st person forms** (@deva[पचामि], @deva[पचावः], @deva[पचामः]): by @ref[7.3.101], the stem-final @deva[अ] (from @deva[शप्]) lengthens before endings beginning with @deva[व्] or @deva[म्]. So @deva[पच] + @deva[अ] + @deva[मि] → @deva[पच] + @deva[आ] + @deva[मि] → @deva[पचामि].

**Key Terms:** @deva[लट्], @deva[परस्मैपद]

## quiz - Identify the form

**Question:** What person, number, and voice is @deva[पठन्ति]?

**Options:**
- 3rd person plural, parasmaipada
- 2nd person plural, parasmaipada
- 3rd person singular, parasmaipada
- 3rd person plural, ātmanepada

**Answer:** 3rd person plural, parasmaipada

**Explanation:** The ending @deva[-न्ति] (from raw @deva[झि]) marks @deva[प्रथम बहुवचन परस्मैपद] — 3rd person plural active. Compare: @deva[पठति] (3rd sg), @deva[पठतः] (3rd du). The stem @deva[पठ-] = root @deva[पठ्] + @deva[शप्]-@deva[अ], with no guṇa since the root is consonant-final.

## quiz - Derive the form

**Question:** Derive @deva[जीवति] ("he lives") step by step from root @deva[जीव्] (Class 1).

**Options:**
- @deva[जीव्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति] → guṇa applies → @deva[जेव्] + @deva[ति] → @deva[जेवति]
- @deva[जीव्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति] → no guṇa (root ends in consonant) → @deva[जीवति]
- @deva[जीव्] + @deva[ति] (no vikaraṇa needed) → @deva[जीवति]
- @deva[जीव्] → guṇa of @deva[ई] → @deva[जेव्] + @deva[ति] → @deva[जेवति]

**Answer:** @deva[जीव्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति] → no guṇa (root ends in consonant) → @deva[जीवति]

**Explanation:** Root @deva[जीव्] ends in the consonant @deva[व्], not in an @deva[इक्] vowel. The vowel @deva[ई] inside the root is **not root-final**. Guṇa (@ref[7.3.84]) targets only the root-final @deva[इक्]. Since @deva[जीव्] ends in a consonant, there is no guṇa. The derivation is simply: @deva[जीव्] + @deva[शप्] + @deva[तिप्] → @deva[जीव्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[ति] → @deva[जीवति]. Compare with @deva[नी] (which ends in the vowel @deva[ई]) → guṇa applies → @deva[नयति].

## quiz - Verb class question

**Question:** @deva[दीव्यति] ("he plays / gambles") has a @deva[-य-] before the ending. Does this mean it is Class 1 (bhvādi)?

**Options:**
- Yes — @deva[-यति] always signals Class 1
- No — the @deva[-य-] here is the Class 4 (divādi) vikaraṇa @deva[श्यन्], not guṇa+sandhi
- No — it is Class 10 (churādi) with @deva[-अयति]
- Yes — all @deva[-यति] forms derive from guṇa of @deva[ई]-final roots

**Answer:** No — the @deva[-य-] here is the Class 4 vikaraṇa @deva[श्यन्], not guṇa+sandhi

**Explanation:** Class 4 (@deva[दिवादि]) roots take the vikaraṇa @deva[श्यन्] (= @deva[य]) by @ref[3.1.69], instead of @deva[शप्] (= @deva[अ]). So: @deva[दिव्] + @deva[श्यन्] + @deva[ति] → @deva[दीव्] + @deva[य] + @deva[ति] → @deva[दीव्यति]. The @deva[-य-] is the vikaraṇa itself. Compare @deva[नयति] (Class 1): there the @deva[-य-] comes from guṇa (@deva[ई]→@deva[ए]) followed by semi-vowel sandhi (@deva[ए] → @deva[अय्] before @deva[अ] by @ref[6.1.78]). Same surface pattern, two different sources.
