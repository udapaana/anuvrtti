## 1.3.1 - Bhūvādayo dhātavaḥ

**Key Terms:** BU, DAtu, gaNa

The roots starting from 'bhū' are called dhātus. The Dhātupāṭha lists ~2000 roots organized into 10 classes (gaṇas).

---

## 3.1.68 - Kartari śap

**Key Terms:** Sap, vikaraRa, BvAdi

The vikarana 'śap' (realized as 'a') is added after bhvādi (1st class) roots before sārvadhātuka endings. bhū + śap + ti → bhavati.

---

## 3.1.69 - Divādibhyaḥ śyan

**Key Terms:** SyaN, divAdi

After divādi (4th class) roots, the vikarana 'śyan' (= ya) is added. div + śyan + ti → dīvyati.

---

## 3.1.73 - Svādibhyaḥ śnu

**Key Terms:** Snu, svAdi

After svādi (5th class) roots, vikarana 'śnu' (= nu/no) is added. su + śnu + ti → sunoti.

---

## 3.1.77 - Tudādibhyaś śa

**Key Terms:** Sa, tudAdi

After tudādi (6th class) roots, vikarana 'śa' (= a, with accent difference) is added. tud + śa + ti → tudati.

---

## 3.1.78 - Rudhādibhyaḥ śnam

**Key Terms:** Snam, ruDAdi

After rudhādi (7th class) roots, vikarana 'śnam' is infixed (inserted before final consonant). rudh → ru-ṇa-dh + ti → ruṇaddhi.

---

## 3.1.79 - Tanādikr̥ñbhya u

**Key Terms:** u, tanAdi

After tanādi (8th class) roots, vikarana 'u' (= o) is added. tan + u + ti → tanoti.

---

## 3.1.80 - Kriyādibhyaḥ ṇā

**Key Terms:** RA, kryAdi

After kryādi (9th class) roots, vikarana 'ṇā' (= nā/nī) is added. krī + ṇā + ti → krīṇāti.

---

## 2.4.72 - Adiprabhṛti... (curādi special)

**Key Terms:** Ric, curAdi

Curādi (10th class) roots take the 'ṇic' suffix, making them formally causatives. cur + ṇi + śap + ti → corayati.

---

## 2.4.75 - Juhotyādiṣu ślu

**Key Terms:** Slu, juhotyAdi

After juhotyādi (3rd class) roots, the vikarana undergoes 'ślu' (deletion), and the root reduplicates. hu → ju-hu + ti → juhoti.

---

## 3.1.81 - Krīṅ juhotyādiṣu ślu

**Key Terms:** Slu, krIN

The root 'krī' (to buy) in juhotyādi class takes ślu. Creates reduplicated forms.

---

## 2.4.74 - Adādikṣu śyanśyaślīnāṁ śluḥ

**Key Terms:** Slu, adAdi

In adādi (2nd class), the vikarana is deleted (ślu). The root directly takes endings. ad + ti → atti.
