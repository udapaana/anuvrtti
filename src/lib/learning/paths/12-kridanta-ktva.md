## 3.4.21 - Samānakartṛkayoḥ pūrvakāle

**Key Terms:** samAnakartFka, pUrvakAle, ktvA

When two actions share the same agent and one precedes the other, ktva is used. "Having eaten, he went."

---

## 3.4.18 - Alakṣaṇe lyap ca

**Key Terms:** lyap, upasarga

After a prefix (upasarga), 'lyap' (= ya) is used instead of ktva. "pra-bhū + ya = prabhūya."

---

## 1.1.40 - Ktvātosunkasunaḥ

**Key Terms:** ktvA, avyaya

Forms ending in ktva are avyaya (indeclinable). They don't take case endings.

---

## 7.2.56 - Udito vā

**Key Terms:** udit, iT, ktvA

For roots marked with 'u' (udit), the augment 'iṭ' is optional before ktva. "uktvā/ukitvā."

---

## 6.4.15 - Yasyeṭi ktvā

**Key Terms:** iT, ktvA

When 'iṭ' is added, specific stem changes follow. Governs consonant behavior.

---

## 6.1.71 - Hrasvasya piti kṛti tuk

**Key Terms:** hrasva, pit, tuk

Before a pit-marked kṛt suffix following a short vowel, 'tuk' (= t) is augmented.

---

## 7.4.40 - Dyatisyatimāsthām...

**Key Terms:** dyati, syati, ktvA

Roots dya, sya, māsthā have special treatment before ktva. Exception handling.

---

## 8.4.17 - Nastaddhite

**Key Terms:** na, tadDite, Ra

The 'n' of a root becomes 'ṇ' in certain ktva formations. Retroflexion rule.
