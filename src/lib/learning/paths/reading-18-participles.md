## concept - What Are Participles?

Participles are **verbs used as adjectives**. They describe nouns by their action:

@deva[गतः नरः] — "the man **who went**" / "the **gone** man"
@deva[पठन् बालकः] — "the boy **who is reading**" / "the **reading** boy"

---

## concept - Past Passive Participle (kta)

The @deva[क्त] suffix creates "was X-ed" or "having X-ed":

| Root | Participle | Meaning |
|------|------------|---------|
| @deva[गम्] | @deva[गत] | gone |
| @deva[कृ] | @deva[कृत] | done, made |
| @deva[उक्त] | @deva[वच्] | said, spoken |
| @deva[दृश्] | @deva[दृष्ट] | seen |
| @deva[श्रु] | @deva[श्रुत] | heard |

These decline like @deva[देव] (masc.), @deva[फल] (neut.), @deva[कन्या] (fem.).

---

## concept - Using kta Participles

**As adjectives:**
- @deva[कृतं कर्म] — "done work"
- @deva[गता रात्रिः] — "the gone night" / "the night that passed"

**As perfect tense:**
- @deva[स गतः] — "he went" / "he has gone"
- @deva[तत् कृतम्] — "that was done"

---

## concept - Present Active Participle (śatṛ)

The @deva[शतृ] suffix creates "-ing" (active):

| Root | Participle | Meaning |
|------|------------|---------|
| @deva[गम्] | @deva[गच्छत्] | going |
| @deva[पठ्] | @deva[पठत्] | reading |
| @deva[खाद्] | @deva[खादत्] | eating |
| @deva[वद्] | @deva[वदत्] | speaking |

Stem ends in @deva[-त्]; declines like @deva[महत्].

---

## concept - śatṛ Forms

| Case | Masculine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Nom. sg. | @deva[गच्छन्] | @deva[गच्छत्] |
| Nom. pl. | @deva[गच्छन्तः] | @deva[गच्छन्ति] |
| Acc. sg. | @deva[गच्छन्तम्] | @deva[गच्छत्] |

**Example:** @deva[गच्छन् नरः पश्यति] — "The going man sees" / "The man, going, sees"

---

## concept - Absolutive (ktvā / ya)

"Having done X, [then] Y":

| Suffix | After | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| @deva[-त्वा] | simple root | @deva[गत्वा] "having gone" |
| @deva[-य] | prefixed root | @deva[आगम्य] "having come" |

@deva[भुक्त्वा गच्छति] — "Having eaten, he goes"
@deva[आगम्य वदति] — "Having come, he speaks"

---

## concept - Participles in Reading

Participles are everywhere in Sanskrit prose:

@deva[स तं दृष्ट्वा उक्तवान्] — "He, **having seen** him, **said**"

Multiple participles can chain:
@deva[आगत्य उपविश्य भुक्त्वा गतः] — "**Having come**, **having sat**, **having eaten**, he went"

## concept - Practice

Parse these:

   - "The reading boy laughs"

2. @deva[कृतं कार्यम्]
   - "The done task" / "The task is done"

3. @deva[गत्वा आगच्छति]
   - "Having gone, he comes [back]"

## concept - Lesson Summary

Three key participles:
1. **kta** (@deva[गत]): past passive, "X-ed"
2. **śatṛ** (@deva[गच्छत्]): present active, "-ing"
3. **ktvā** (@deva[गत्वा]): absolutive, "having X-ed"
