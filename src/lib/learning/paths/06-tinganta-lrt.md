## 3.3.13 - Lṛṭ śeṣe ca

**Key Terms:** lFT, Bavizyan

The suffix 'lṛṭ' expresses future action (bhaviṣyan). This is the simple future tense.

---

## 3.1.33 - Syatāsī lṛluṭoḥ

**Key Terms:** sya, tAsI, lFT

The vikaranas 'sya' (P) and 'tāsī' (Ā) are added after the dhatu in lṛṭ. These create the characteristic future forms.

---

## 7.2.10 - Ekahalmadhye...

**Key Terms:** ekahalmadhye, iT

The augment 'iṭ' is inserted before 'sya' when the dhatu has a single consonant between vowels. Affects form: kar + iṭ + sya → kariṣya.

---

## 1.2.4 - Sārvadhātukam apit

**Key Terms:** sArvaDAtukaH, apit, NidAgama

A sārvadhātuka affix not marked with 'p' (pit) is treated as if marked with 'ṅ'. Affects accent and operations.

---

## 3.4.77 - Lasya tiptas...

**Key Terms:** lasya, tip, Adesha

The 'l' of lakāras is replaced by tiṅ endings. Same mechanism as present tense.

---

## 8.4.1 - Raṣābhyāṁ no ṇaḥ

**Key Terms:** ra, za, Ra

After 'r' or 'ṣ', 'n' becomes 'ṇ' (retroflexion). Applies to future forms like kariṣyanti.

---

## 7.2.35 - Ārdhadhātukasyeḍ valādeḥ

**Key Terms:** ArDaDAtukaH, iT, valAdeH

The augment 'iṭ' is added before an ārdhadhātuka suffix beginning with a consonant cluster. Key for future formation.

---

## 6.4.62 - Syatāsyorakāraḥ

**Key Terms:** sya, tAsi, akAraH

Specific rules for the 'a' in 'sya' and 'tāsī' vikaranas. Governs vowel quality in future.
