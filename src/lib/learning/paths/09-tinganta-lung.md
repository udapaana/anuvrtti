## 3.2.110 - Luṅ

**Key Terms:** luN, BUta

The suffix 'luṅ' expresses past action (bhūta). The aorist - a simple past tense.

---

## 6.4.71 - Luṅlaṅlṛṅṣv aḍ udāttaḥ

**Key Terms:** aT, luN

The augment 'aṭ' (= a) is prefixed to the dhātu in luṅ. Past tense marker shared with laṅ.

---

## 3.1.43 - Cli luṅi

**Key Terms:** cli, luN

The affix 'cli' is added after the dhātu in luṅ. This is the aorist sign.

---

## 3.1.44 - Cleḥ sic

**Key Terms:** cleH, sic

The 'cli' is replaced by 'sic' (= s) in certain conditions. Creates sigmatic aorist.

---

## 2.4.77 - Gātisthāghupābhūbhyaḥ sic ṅit

**Key Terms:** sic, Nit, gA

For roots gā, sthā, ghā, pā, bhū - 'sic' is treated as 'ṅit'. Affects which rules apply.

---

## 3.1.45 - Śliṣṭisīcstidighavyayoḥ kṣaḥ

**Key Terms:** ksa, aorist

For certain roots, 'ksa' replaces 'cli'. Another aorist type.

---

## 3.1.48 - Ṅamo hrasvāt cṅi

**Key Terms:** caN, hrasva

After short-vowel roots ending in 'm' or 'n', the 'caṅ' aorist is formed. Reduplicated aorist.

---

## 3.1.52 - Asmadyuśmadbhyāṁ ṇic sic ca

**Key Terms:** Ric, sic, luN

Roots with 'ṇic' (causative) can take 'sic' in luṅ. Causative aorist formation.

---

## 3.1.55 - Puṣādidyutādibhyaś ca

**Key Terms:** luN, puza, dyuta

Roots of puṣ and dyut classes have special aorist formations.

---

## 7.2.1 - Ayadvidhinye ārdhdhātukasyāsya

**Key Terms:** ArDaDAtukaH, aya, iT

The 'iṭ' augment rules for ārdhadhātuka apply in aorist. Affects consonant clusters.

---

## 7.3.96 - Astisico'pṛkte

**Key Terms:** asti, sic, apFkte

For root 'as' (to be), special rules apply to 'sic' aorist when not prefixed.

---

## 6.4.104 - Luṅi ca

**Key Terms:** luN, ca

Specific stem changes occur in luṅ context. Completes aorist stem formation.
