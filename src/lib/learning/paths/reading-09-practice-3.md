## reading - Learning Gives Humility

**Source:** Traditional Subhāṣita

@deva[विद्या ददाति विनयं विनयाद्याति पात्रताम्]

Parse:
- @deva[विद्या] — "learning" (nom. — subject)
- @deva[ददाति] — "gives" (present)
- @deva[विनयम्] — "humility" (acc. — object)
- @deva[विनयात्] — "from humility" (ablative — we'll learn this)
- @deva[याति] — "comes"
- @deva[पात्रताम्] — "worthiness" (acc.)

**Translation:** "Learning gives humility; from humility comes worthiness."

---

## reading - Continued Verse

@deva[पात्रत्वाद्धनमाप्नोति धनाद्धर्मं ततः सुखम्]

Parse:
- @deva[पात्रत्वात्] — "from worthiness" (ablative)
- @deva[धनम्] — "wealth" (acc.)
- @deva[आप्नोति] — "obtains"
- @deva[धनात्] — "from wealth" (ablative)
- @deva[धर्मम्] — "dharma" (acc.)
- @deva[ततः] — "from that"
- @deva[सुखम्] — "happiness" (acc.)

**Translation:** "From worthiness one obtains wealth; from wealth, dharma; from that, happiness."

---

## concept - The Chain of Causation

This verse shows a causal chain using ablative (source):

विद्या → विनय → पात्रता → धन → धर्म → सुख

Learning → Humility → Worthiness → Wealth → Dharma → Happiness

Each step flows from the previous using "from X comes Y."

## reading - The Test of Friendship

**Source:** Traditional Subhāṣita

@deva[आपदि मित्रपरीक्षा]

- @deva[आपदि] — "in calamity" (locative)
- @deva[मित्रपरीक्षा] — "test of a friend" (compound, nominative)

**Translation:** "In calamity [is] the test of a friend."

---

## reading - More Tests

@deva[शूरपरीक्षा रणाङ्गणे]

- @deva[शूरपरीक्षा] — "test of a hero"
- @deva[रणाङ्गणे] — "on the battlefield" (locative)

**Translation:** "The test of a hero [is] on the battlefield."

---

## concept - Nominal Sentences

Notice: no verb in these sentences! Sanskrit often omits @deva[अस्ति] "is":

@deva[आपदि मित्रपरीक्षा] = @deva[आपदि मित्रपरीक्षा अस्ति]

"In calamity is the test of a friend."

---

## concept - Lesson Summary

You've read real subhāṣitas! Key patterns:
- Ablative chains showing cause/source
- Locative showing circumstances
- Nominal sentences (implied "is")
- Compound words (@deva[मित्रपरीक्षा] = मित्र + परीक्षा)
