## reading - विद्या ददाति विनयम्

**Source:** Subhāṣitaratnabhāṇḍāgāra (popular verse, attr. various)

@deva[विद्या ददाति विनयम्।]
@deva[विनयाद् याति पात्रताम्।]
@deva[पात्रत्वात् धनमाप्नोति।]
@deva[धनात् धर्मस् ततः सुखम्॥]

*vidyā dadāti vinayam | vinayād yāti pātratām | pātrattvāt dhanam āpnoti | dhanāt dharmas tataḥ sukham*

"Knowledge gives humility. From humility comes worthiness. From worthiness one gains wealth. From wealth comes dharma, and then happiness."

---

## concept - Sandhi: Split the Lines

First, identify the sandhi junctions:

**Line 3:** @deva[पात्रत्वात् धनमाप्नोति]

Written: @deva[धनमाप्नोति]
Split: @deva[धनम्] + @deva[आप्नोति]

How? @deva[-म्] before a vowel: @deva[म्] + @deva[आ] — this is anusvāra sandhi. The @deva[म्] stays as @deva[म्] before a vowel, written @deva[ं]. So @deva[धनं] + @deva[आप्नोति] → @deva[धनमाप्नोति] in continuous script.

**Line 4:** @deva[धनात् धर्मस् ततः सुखम्]

No vowel sandhi here — these are separate padas in verse.

---

## concept - Word-by-Word: Line 1

@deva[विद्या ददाति विनयम्]

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[विद्या] | @deva[विद्या] (nom. sg. f.) | knowledge |
| @deva[ददाति] | @deva[दा] (Class 3 @deva[जुहोत्यादि]) + 3sg. pres. | gives |
| @deva[विनयम्] | @deva[विनय] + @deva[अम्] (acc. sg. m.) | humility |

@deva[ददाति]: Root @deva[दा] "give." Class 3 (@deva[जुहोत्यादि]) — reduplicates the root: @deva[दा] → @deva[ददा] + @deva[ति]. This class uses no @deva[शप्].

---

## concept - Word-by-Word: Lines 2–3

**Line 2:** @deva[विनयाद् याति पात्रताम्]

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[विनयात्] | @deva[विनय] + @deva[ङसि] (abl. sg.) → @deva[विनयात्] | from humility |
| @deva[याति] | @deva[या] (Class 2) + 3sg. pres. | comes, goes |
| @deva[पात्रताम्] | @deva[पात्रता] + @deva[अम्] (acc. sg. f.) | worthiness |

**Ablative @deva[-आत्]** (@deva[पञ्चमी]) signals cause or source: "from X comes Y."

**Line 3:** @deva[पात्रत्वात् धनम् आप्नोति]

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[पात्रत्वात्] | @deva[पात्रत्व] + abl. sg. | from worthiness |
| @deva[धनम्] | @deva[धन] + acc. sg. | wealth |
| @deva[आप्नोति] | @deva[आप्] (Class 5) + 3sg. pres. | obtains |

---

## concept - Word-by-Word: Line 4

@deva[धनात् धर्मस् ततः सुखम्]

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[धनात्] | abl. sg. | from wealth |
| @deva[धर्मः] | nom. sg. (here: @deva[धर्मस्] in sandhi position) | dharma (subject) |
| @deva[ततः] | indeclinable | then, from that |
| @deva[सुखम्] | nom./acc. sg. n. | happiness |

**@deva[ततः]** is an @term[avyaya] (indeclinable). It never changes form.

The line has no verb — supply @deva[भवति] or @deva[जायते] ("comes, arises") from context.

---

## concept - The Chain Structure

This verse is a *causal chain* (each outcome becomes the cause of the next):

@deva[विद्या] → @deva[विनय] → @deva[पात्रता] → @deva[धन] → @deva[धर्म] → @deva[सुख]

Notice: Each ablative (@deva[-आत्]) carries the meaning "from X" — a chain of cause and effect.

This pattern (ablative + verb + accusative) is the engine of the verse. Once you can read one line, you can read all four.
