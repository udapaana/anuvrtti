## reading - अस्ति मगधदेशे

**Source:** Pañcatantra 1.1 (adapted prose)

@deva[अस्ति मगधदेशे पाटलिपुत्रं नाम नगरम्।]
@deva[तत्र अमरशक्तिः नाम राजा आसीत्।]
@deva[तस्य त्रयः पुत्राः आसन् — बहुशक्तिः, उग्रशक्तिः, अनन्तशक्तिश्च।]
@deva[ते मूर्खाः आसन्।]
@deva[अतः राजा चिन्तितः अभवत्।]

"There is in the Magadha region a city named Pāṭaliputra. There a king named Amaraśakti lived. He had three sons — Bahuśakti, Ugraśakti, and Anantaśakti. They were fools. So the king became troubled."

---

## concept - Sandhi: Splitting the Text

Before parsing, identify where words join.

**@deva[पाटलिपुत्रं नाम]**: @deva[पाटलिपुत्रम् + नाम] — @deva[-म्] before @deva[न्] becomes @deva[ं] (anusvāra). Split: @deva[पाटलिपुत्रम्] + @deva[नाम].

**@deva[अनन्तशक्तिश्च]**: @deva[अनन्तशक्तिः + च] — visarga sandhi: @deva[ः] before @deva[च] → @deva[श्]. Split: @deva[अनन्तशक्तिः] + @deva[च].

**@deva[चिन्तितः अभवत्]**: Two words, no sandhi (visarga before vowel → but here it stays as @deva[ः] since it's at a word boundary in slow recitation). In connected recitation: @deva[चिन्तितो ऽभवत्] — @deva[ः] → @deva[ओ] before vowel (@ref[8.3.15]).

## concept - Word-by-Word: Lines 1–2

**@deva[अस्ति मगधदेशे पाटलिपुत्रं नाम नगरम्]**

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[अस्ति] | @deva[अस्] (root "be") + 3sg. pres. | there is |
| @deva[मगधदेशे] | @deva[मगधदेश] + loc. sg. | in the Magadha region |
| @deva[पाटलिपुत्रम्] | compound name + acc. sg. | Pāṭaliputra (city name) |
| @deva[नाम] | indeclinable | named, by name |
| @deva[नगरम्] | @deva[नगर] + acc. sg. n. | a city |

**@deva[नाम]** is used after a name to introduce it: "X by name" = "named X."

**@deva[तत्र अमरशक्तिः नाम राजा आसीत्]**

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[तत्र] | indeclinable "there" | there |
| @deva[अमरशक्तिः] | compound proper name, nom. sg. | Amaraśakti |
| @deva[राजा] | @deva[राजन्] nom. sg. | king |
| @deva[आसीत्] | @deva[अस्] + @deva[लङ्], 3sg. | was, lived |

**@deva[आसीत्]** is the past tense (imperfect @deva[लङ्]) of @deva[अस्] "to be." The augment @deva[अ-] + stem @deva[आस्] + ending @deva[-त्] → @deva[आसीत्] (with lengthening).

## concept - Word-by-Word: Lines 3–5

**@deva[तस्य त्रयः पुत्राः आसन्]**

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[तस्य] | @deva[तद्] gen. sg. m. | his, of him |
| @deva[त्रयः] | numeral "three" nom. pl. m. | three |
| @deva[पुत्राः] | @deva[पुत्र] nom. pl. m. | sons |
| @deva[आसन्] | @deva[अस्] + @deva[लङ्] 3pl. | were |

**Genitive @deva[तस्य]** shows possession: "of him" = "his."

**@deva[ते मूर्खाः आसन्]**

@deva[ते] = pronoun "they" (nom. pl. m. of @deva[तद्]). @deva[मूर्खाः] = "fools" (nom. pl. m.). @deva[आसन्] = "were" (3pl. imperfect).

**@deva[अतः राजा चिन्तितः अभवत्]**

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| @deva[अतः] | indeclinable | therefore, so |
| @deva[राजा] | nom. sg. | the king |
| @deva[चिन्तितः] | @deva[चिन्त्] + @deva[क्त] (past participle, nom. sg. m.) | troubled, worried |
| @deva[अभवत्] | @deva[भू] + @deva[लङ्] 3sg. | became |

**@deva[चिन्तितः]** is a @deva[क्त]-participle used predicatively: "the king became [one who is] troubled."

**@deva[अभवत्]** = @deva[अ] (augment) + @deva[भव] (stem) + @deva[त्] (3sg. @deva[लङ्] ending).

## concept - Compounds in This Passage

Three proper names are @term[tatpuruṣa] (@deva[तत्पुरुष]) compounds:

| Compound | Split | Meaning |
|----------|-------|---------|
| @deva[मगधदेश] | @deva[मगध] + @deva[देश] | "the region of Magadha" |
| @deva[पाटलिपुत्र] | @deva[पाटलि] + @deva[पुत्र] | "son of Pāṭali" (city name) |
| @deva[अमरशक्ति] | @deva[अमर] + @deva[शक्ति] | "power of the immortals" (name) |

The sons' names — @deva[बहुशक्ति], @deva[उग्रशक्ति], @deva[अनन्तशक्ति] — follow the same pattern: X + @deva[शक्ति].

---

## concept - The Story Pattern

Pañcatantra prose follows a formula you will see in many stories:

1. **Setting**: @deva[अस्ति ... नाम ...] — "There is a place named..."
2. **Character**: @deva[तत्र ... नाम राजा आसीत्] — "There a king named ... lived"
3. **Situation**: details of family, problem
4. **Resolution**: how the king acts

Once you learn this frame, most Pañcatantra openings parse themselves.

## concept - Lesson Summary

Passage: 5 sentences from Pañcatantra Book 1.

Key grammar encountered:

| Feature | Example | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Imperfect @deva[लङ्] | @deva[आसीत्], @deva[आसन्], @deva[अभवत्] | Past tense |
| Genitive possession | @deva[तस्य] | "his sons" |
| @deva[क्त]-participle | @deva[चिन्तितः] | Past participle as predicate |
| @deva[नाम] | @deva[... नाम ...] | "named" construction |
| Anusvāra sandhi | @deva[पुत्रं] for @deva[पुत्रम्] | Before consonant |
| Visarga sandhi | @deva[शक्तिश्च] | @deva[ः] + @deva[च] → @deva[श्] |

Reading speed will increase once these patterns are automatic. The goal is not just to parse — it is to recognize and proceed.
