## concept - The Most Common @term[kṛdanta] Forms

@term[niṣṭhā] (@deva[क्त] and @deva[क्तवतु]) forms are everywhere in Sanskrit. Open any text and you'll find @deva[कृतम्] "done," @deva[उक्तम्] "said," @deva[गतः] "gone," @deva[दृष्टम्] "seen" on nearly every page. They're more frequent than @term[tiṅanta] forms.

Sanskrit has two @term[niṣṭhā] @term[pratyaya]:

| @term[pratyaya] | Type                     | Meaning               | Example                       |
| --------------- | ------------------------ | --------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| @deva[क्त]      | @term[karmaṇi] (passive) | "that which was X-ed" | @deva[कृतम्] "done/made"      |
| @deva[क्तवतु]   | @term[kartari] (active)  | "one who has X-ed"    | @deva[कृतवान्] "one who made" |

Both are @term[kṛt] @term[pratyaya] — they attach directly to the @term[dhātu] and create adjectives that decline like regular @term[subanta].

Key difference:

- @deva[क्त] is usually **passive**: @deva[कृतम् कर्म] = "the work (that was) done"
- @deva[क्तवतु] is **active**: @deva[कृतवान् रामः] = "Rāma who did (it)"

---

## @ref[3.2.102] - निष्ठा

**Key Terms:** @deva[निष्ठा], @deva[क्त], @deva[क्तवतु]

@deva[निष्ठा] — The affixes @deva[क्त] and @deva[क्तवतु] (collectively called @deva[निष्ठा]) come after a root in the sense of past time.

@sutra[3.2.102]

@deva[निष्ठा] is Pāṇini's collective name for these two affixes. The term will be used in later rules that apply to both.

Examples:

- @deva[कृ] + @deva[क्त] = @deva[कृतम्] "done"
- @deva[कृ] + @deva[क्तवतु] = @deva[कृतवान्] "one who did"
- @deva[भुज्] + @deva[क्त] = @deva[भुक्तम्] "eaten"
- @deva[गम्] + @deva[क्त] = @deva[गतम्] "gone"

## @ref[3.4.114] - आर्धधातुकं शेषः

**Key Terms:** @deva[आर्धधातुक], @deva[क्त]

@deva[आर्धधातुकं शेषः] — Affixes other than @deva[तिङ्] and those with indicatory @deva[श्] are @deva[आर्धधातुक].

@sutra[3.4.114]

Both @deva[क्त] and @deva[क्तवतु] are @deva[आर्धधातुक]. This classification matters because:

- @deva[आर्धधातुक] affixes trigger @deva[गुण] by @ref[7.3.84] — **but** @deva[क्त] and @deva[क्तवतु] have @deva[क्] as @deva[इत्], making them @deva[कित्]
- By @ref[1.1.5] (@deva[क्ङिति च]), @deva[कित्] affixes **block** @deva[गुण]

This is why @deva[भुज्] + @deva[क्त] = @deva[भुक्त] (no guṇa), not \*@deva[भोक्त].

---

## concept - The क Is a Marker

The @deva[क्] in @deva[क्त] is not pronounced — it's @deva[इत्] by @ref[1.3.8]. What remains is just @deva[त]. But that silent @deva[क्] does critical work:

1. It makes the affix @deva[कित्], blocking @deva[गुण/वृद्धि]
2. It triggers @deva[सम्प्रसारण] in certain roots (@ref[6.1.15])

Without this marker, we'd get wrong forms: \*@deva[भोक्त] instead of @deva[भुक्त], \*@deva[वक्त] instead of @deva[उक्त].

## @ref[7.3.84] - सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः

**Key Terms:** @deva[गुण], @deva[क्त]

@deva[सार्वधातुकार्धधातुकयोः] — @deva[गुण] replaces the final @pratyahara[ik] vowel of a stem before @deva[आर्धधातुक] affixes.

@sutra[7.3.84]

This rule **would** apply to @deva[क्त]//@deva[क्तवतु] — but @ref[1.1.5] blocks it because they're @deva[कित्]. Compare:

| Root        | + @deva[क्त] (कित्, no guṇa) | + @deva[तव्य] (not कित्, guṇa) |
| ----------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| @deva[कृ]   | @deva[कृतम्]                 | @deva[कर्तव्यम्]               |
| @deva[भुज्] | @deva[भुक्तम्]               | @deva[भोक्तव्यम्]              |
| @deva[नी]   | @deva[नीतम्]                 | @deva[नेतव्यम्]                |

## @ref[6.1.15] - वचिस्वपियजादीनां किति

**Key Terms:** @deva[वच्], @deva[स्वप्], @deva[सम्प्रसारण]

@deva[वचिस्वपियजादीनां किति] — The semivowels of @deva[वच्], @deva[स्वप्], @deva[यज्], and related roots undergo @deva[सम्प्रसारण] (vowel replacement) before @deva[कित्] affixes.

@sutra[6.1.15]

@deva[सम्प्रसारण] converts a semivowel to its corresponding vowel:

- @deva[व] → @deva[उ], @deva[य] → @deva[इ]

| Root                   | Semivowel → Vowel   | + @deva[क्त]           | Result                     |
| ---------------------- | ------------------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------- |
| @deva[वच्] "speak"     | @deva[व] → @deva[उ] | @deva[उच्] + @deva[त]  | @deva[उक्तम्] "said"       |
| @deva[वप्] "sow"       | @deva[व] → @deva[उ] | @deva[उप्] + @deva[त]  | @deva[उप्तम्] "sown"       |
| @deva[यज्] "sacrifice" | @deva[य] → @deva[इ] | @deva[इज्] + @deva[त]  | @deva[इष्टम्] "sacrificed" |
| @deva[स्वप्] "sleep"   | @deva[व] → @deva[उ] | @deva[सुप्] + @deva[त] | @deva[सुप्तम्] "slept"     |

## @ref[8.2.42] - रदाभ्यां निष्ठातो नः पूर्वस्य च दः

**Key Terms:** @deva[र], @deva[द], @deva[न]

@deva[रदाभ्यां निष्ठातो नः पूर्वस्य च दः] — After @deva[र्] or @deva[द्], the @deva[त्] of @deva[निष्ठा] becomes @deva[न्]. And a preceding @deva[द्] also becomes @deva[न्].

@sutra[8.2.42]

This rule produces the characteristic @deva[न] in many common participles:

**After @deva[र्]:**

- @deva[तॄ] + @deva[क्त] → @deva[तीर्ण] "crossed" (not \*@deva[तीर्त])
- @deva[पॄ] + @deva[क्त] → @deva[पूर्ण] "filled"
- @deva[स्तॄ] + @deva[क्त] → @deva[स्तीर्ण] "spread"

**After @deva[द्]** (both @deva[द्] and @deva[त्] become @deva[न्]):\*\*

- @deva[भिद्] + @deva[क्त] → @deva[भिन्न] "split" (not \*@deva[भित्त])
- @deva[छिद्] + @deva[क्त] → @deva[छिन्न] "cut"
- @deva[विद्] + @deva[क्त] → @deva[विन्न] / @deva[विदित] "known"

## @ref[3.4.72] - गत्यर्थाकर्मकश्लिषशीङ्स्थाऽऽसवसजनरुहजीर्यतिभ्यश्च

**Key Terms:** @deva[क्त], @deva[कर्तृ], @deva[गति]

@deva[गत्यर्थाकर्मक...] — With verbs of motion, intransitive roots, and specific roots like @deva[शीङ्], @deva[स्था], @deva[आस्], @deva[वस्], @deva[जन्], @deva[रुह्], the @deva[क्त] participle can denote the **agent** (not just the object).

@sutra[3.4.72]

Normally @deva[क्त] is passive ("cooked" = "that which was cooked"). But with these roots, it's **active**:

- @deva[गतः] = "one who went" (not "that which was gone to")
- @deva[स्थितः] = "one who stood"
- @deva[जातः] = "one who was born"
- @deva[प्रविष्टः] = "one who entered"

This is why @deva[रामो वनं गतः] means "Rāma who went to the forest" — @deva[गतः] refers to the agent @deva[राम], not the object.

## concept - Common Past Participle Forms

Here are high-frequency forms you'll encounter constantly:

| Root        | Meaning | @deva[क्त] form | @deva[क्तवतु] form |
| ----------- | ------- | --------------- | ------------------ |
| @deva[कृ]   | do      | @deva[कृतम्]    | @deva[कृतवान्]     |
| @deva[गम्]  | go      | @deva[गतम्]     | @deva[गतवान्]      |
| @deva[दृश्] | see     | @deva[दृष्टम्]  | @deva[दृष्टवान्]   |
| @deva[श्रु] | hear    | @deva[श्रुतम्]  | @deva[श्रुतवान्]   |
| @deva[वच्]  | speak   | @deva[उक्तम्]   | @deva[उक्तवान्]    |
| @deva[दा]   | give    | @deva[दत्तम्]   | @deva[दत्तवान्]    |
| @deva[ज्ञा] | know    | @deva[ज्ञातम्]  | @deva[ज्ञातवान्]   |
| @deva[भुज्] | eat     | @deva[भुक्तम्]  | @deva[भुक्तवान्]   |
| @deva[लिख्] | write   | @deva[लिखितम्]  | @deva[लिखितवान्]   |
| @deva[पठ्]  | read    | @deva[पठितम्]   | @deva[पठितवान्]    |

Notice the split: some roots attach @deva[त] directly (@deva[कृतम्], @deva[गतम्]); others insert @deva[इ] (@deva[लिखितम्], @deva[पठितम्]). The @deva[इ] is the @deva[इट्] augment — whether it appears depends on the root.

## concept - What's Next

Past participles are the gateway to more advanced Sanskrit. With @deva[क्त] and @deva[क्तवतु], you can now:

- Read narrative prose (which heavily uses @deva[क्त] forms in place of finite verbs)
- Form compound adjectives: @deva[सुकृतम्] "well-done," @deva[दुरुक्तम्] "badly said"
- Build periphrastic tenses: @deva[कृतवान् अस्ति] ≈ "he has done"

Next: **@deva[क्त्वा]** (absolutive) — another essential @deva[कृत्] affix that creates "having done X" forms.
