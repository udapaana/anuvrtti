## concept - @deva[लिट्] — The Perfect

@deva[लिट्] expresses @term[parokṣa] (unwitnessed) past action. Its most distinctive feature is @term[abhyāsa] (reduplication) — the @term[dhātu] is doubled according to specific rules, producing forms like @deva[चकार] (from @deva[कृ]) and @deva[जगाम] (from @deva[गम्]).

**Paradigm of @deva[कृ] (do) — @deva[लिट्] @deva[परस्मैपद]:**

|     | Singular                 | Dual           | Plural        |
| --- | ------------------------ | -------------- | ------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[चकार]              | @deva[चक्रतुः] | @deva[चक्रुः] |
| 2nd | @deva[चकर्थ]             | @deva[चक्रथुः] | @deva[चक्र]   |
| 1st | @deva[चकार] / @deva[चकर] | @deva[चक्रिव]  | @deva[चक्रिम] |

**Paradigm of @deva[भू] (be) — @deva[लिट्]:**

|     | Singular      | Dual           | Plural        |
| --- | ------------- | -------------- | ------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[बभूव]   | @deva[बभूवतुः] | @deva[बभूवुः] |
| 2nd | @deva[बभूविथ] | @deva[बभूवथुः] | @deva[बभूव]   |
| 1st | @deva[बभूव]   | @deva[बभूविव]  | @deva[बभूविम] |

**Key features:**

1. Reduplication of the root
2. Special endings (@deva[णल्], @deva[अतुस्], @deva[उस्], @deva[थल्], etc.)
3. Strong/weak stem alternation (1st/3rd sg. are "strong")
4. Guttural → palatal in reduplication (@deva[कृ] → @deva[चकृ])

## @ref[3.2.115] - परोक्षे लिट्

**Key Terms:** @deva[परोक्ष], @deva[लिट्]

The affix @deva[लिट्] comes after the verb in the sense of past action that is @deva[परोक्ष] (beyond the eye, unwitnessed) and @deva[अनद्यतन] (not of today).

**Usage context:**

- @deva[लिट्] for events the speaker did not witness: @deva[रामः रावणं जघान] "Rāma slew Rāvaṇa" (narrator was not present)
- @deva[लङ्] for events the speaker witnessed: @deva[अपश्यम्] "I saw"

In Classical Sanskrit, this distinction is often relaxed, and @deva[लिट्] is used freely for narrative past.

---

## @ref[6.1.8] - लिटि धातोरनभ्यासस्य

**Key Terms:** @deva[लिट्], @deva[धातु], @deva[अभ्यास]

When @deva[लिट्] endings follow, there is reduplication of the root (provided it is not already reduplicated). This is the trigger rule for perfect reduplication:

- @deva[कृ] → @deva[कृ-कृ] → (after simplification) @deva[चकृ]
- @deva[भू] → @deva[भू-भू] → @deva[बभू]
- @deva[गम्] → @deva[गम्-गम्] → @deva[जगम्]
- @deva[पच्] → @deva[पच्-पच्] → @deva[पपच्]

---

## @ref[6.1.1] - एकाचो द्वे प्रथमस्य

**Key Terms:** @deva[एकाच्], @deva[द्वे], @deva[प्रथम]

For the first portion containing a single vowel, there are two — the first syllable is reduplicated. This @deva[अधिकार] (governing rule) establishes the basic reduplication mechanism through @ref[6.1.12].

The "first portion" = the initial consonant(s) + first vowel of the root.

---

## @ref[6.1.2] - अजादेर्द्वितीयस्य

**Key Terms:** @deva[अजादि], @deva[द्वितीय]

For a root beginning with a vowel, the second syllable (rather than the first) is reduplicated. This handles vowel-initial roots:

- @deva[अश्] → @deva[आन-अश] → @deva[आनश] (special treatment)

---

## @ref[7.4.59] - ह्रस्वः

**Key Terms:** @deva[ह्रस्व], @deva[अभ्यास]

The vowel of the reduplicate is shortened:

- @deva[भू-भू] → @deva[भु-भू] → @deva[बभू]
- @deva[ची-ची] → @deva[चि-ची] → @deva[चिचे]
- @deva[नी-नी] → @deva[नि-नी] → @deva[निने]

This ensures the reduplicating syllable is always light.

---

## @ref[7.4.60] - हलादिः शेषः

**Key Terms:** @deva[हलादि], @deva[शेष], @deva[अभ्यास]

Of the consonants of the reduplicate, only the **first** is retained; the rest are elided:

- @deva[स्मृ] → @deva[स्म-स्मृ] → @deva[स-स्मृ] → @deva[सस्मार]
- @deva[त्यज्] → @deva[त्य-त्यज्] → @deva[त-त्यज्] → @deva[तत्याज]
- @deva[स्था] → @deva[स्थ-स्था] → @deva[त-स्था] → @deva[तस्थौ]

Consonant clusters in the reduplicate simplify to just the initial consonant.

---

## @ref[7.4.62] - कुहोश्चुः

**Key Terms:** @deva[कु], @deva[हु], @deva[चु], @deva[अभ्यास]

In the reduplicate, gutturals (@deva[क्], @deva[ख्], @deva[ग्], @deva[घ्]) and @deva[ह्] are replaced by their palatal counterparts:

| Root initial        | Reduplicate                | Perfect 3rd sg. |
| ------------------- | -------------------------- | --------------- |
| @deva[क] → @deva[च] | @deva[कृ] → @deva[चकृ]     | @deva[चकार]     |
| @deva[ग] → @deva[ज] | @deva[गम्] → @deva[जगम्]   | @deva[जगाम]     |
| @deva[घ] → @deva[ज] | @deva[घ्रा] → @deva[जघ्रा] | @deva[जघ्रौ]    |
| @deva[ह] → @deva[ज] | @deva[हन्] → @deva[जहन्]   | @deva[जघान]     |
| @deva[ह] → @deva[ज] | @deva[हृ] → @deva[जहृ]     | @deva[जहार]     |

This is one of the most recognizable features of the perfect tense.

---

## @ref[3.4.82] - परस्मैपदानां णलतुसुस्थलथुसणल्वमाः

**Key Terms:** @deva[णल्], @deva[अतुस्], @deva[थल्]

Special @deva[परस्मैपद] endings substitute in @deva[लिट्]:

| Person/Number | Ending       | Realized    |
| ------------- | ------------ | ----------- |
| 3rd sg.       | @deva[णल्]   | @deva[अ]    |
| 3rd du.       | @deva[अतुस्] | @deva[अतुः] |
| 3rd pl.       | @deva[उस्]   | @deva[उः]   |
| 2nd sg.       | @deva[थल्]   | @deva[थ]    |
| 2nd du.       | @deva[अथुस्] | @deva[अथुः] |
| 2nd pl.       | @deva[अ]     | @deva[अ]    |
| 1st sg.       | @deva[णल्]   | @deva[अ]    |
| 1st du.       | @deva[व]     | @deva[व]    |
| 1st pl.       | @deva[म]     | @deva[म]    |

Note: 1st and 3rd singular share the same ending @deva[णल्] (= @deva[अ]), so @deva[चकार] can mean "he did" or "I did."

---

## @ref[3.4.81] - लिटस्तझयोरेशिरेच्

**Key Terms:** @deva[लिट्], @deva[एश्], @deva[इरेच्]

In @deva[लिट्] @deva[आत्मनेपद], @deva[एश्] (= @deva[ए]) and @deva[इरेच्] (= @deva[इरे]) replace @deva[त] (3rd sg. Ā.) and @deva[झ] (3rd pl. Ā.):

- @deva[पच्] → @deva[पेचे] "he cooked" (Ā.)
- @deva[पच्] → @deva[पेचिरे] "they cooked" (Ā.)
- @deva[सेव्] → @deva[सिषेवे] "he served" (Ā.)

---

## @ref[7.1.34] - आत औ णलः

**Key Terms:** @deva[आत्], @deva[औ], @deva[णल्]

@deva[औ] substitutes for @deva[णल्] after roots ending in long @deva[आ]:

- @deva[पा] → @deva[पपौ] "he drank"
- @deva[स्था] → @deva[तस्थौ] "he stood"
- @deva[ग्लै] → @deva[जग्लौ] "he was weary"
- @deva[दा] → @deva[ददौ] "he gave"

---

## @ref[6.4.120] - अत एकहल्मध्येऽनादेशादेर्लिटि

**Key Terms:** @deva[अत्], @deva[एकहल्], @deva[लिट्]

@deva[ए] substitutes for short @deva[अ] when it stands between two single consonants before @deva[कित्] endings of @deva[लिट्], provided no substitution occurred at the beginning. When @deva[ए] is substituted, the reduplicate is dropped:

- @deva[पच्] → @deva[पपच्] → @deva[पेच्] (in weak forms: @deva[पेचतुः], @deva[पेचुः])

---

## @ref[6.4.126] - न शसददवादिगुणानाम्

**Key Terms:** @deva[न], @deva[शस्], @deva[लिट्]

The @deva[ए]-substitution and reduplicate-deletion do NOT apply to @deva[शस्], @deva[दद्], or roots beginning with @deva[व्]:

- @deva[शस्] → @deva[शशंस] (not \*@deva[शेंस])

## concept - Common Perfect Forms

| Root        | Perfect 3rd sg. | Meaning   | Reduplication pattern |
| ----------- | --------------- | --------- | --------------------- |
| @deva[कृ]   | @deva[चकार]     | he did    | @deva[क] → @deva[च]   |
| @deva[भू]   | @deva[बभूव]     | he became | @deva[भ] → @deva[ब]   |
| @deva[गम्]  | @deva[जगाम]     | he went   | @deva[ग] → @deva[ज]   |
| @deva[हन्]  | @deva[जघान]     | he slew   | @deva[ह] → @deva[ज]   |
| @deva[हृ]   | @deva[जहार]     | he took   | @deva[ह] → @deva[ज]   |
| @deva[दा]   | @deva[ददौ]      | he gave   | @deva[द] → @deva[द]   |
| @deva[स्था] | @deva[तस्थौ]    | he stood  | @deva[स्थ] → @deva[त] |
| @deva[पच्]  | @deva[पपाच]     | he cooked | @deva[प] → @deva[प]   |
| @deva[वच्]  | @deva[उवाच]     | he spoke  | vowel-initial         |
| @deva[दृश्] | @deva[ददर्श]    | he saw    | @deva[द] → @deva[द]   |
| @deva[श्रु] | @deva[शुश्राव]  | he heard  | @deva[श] → @deva[शु]  |
| @deva[ज्ञा] | @deva[जज्ञौ]    | he knew   | @deva[ज] → @deva[ज]   |
| @deva[नी]   | @deva[निनाय]    | he led    | @deva[न] → @deva[नि]  |
| @deva[पा]   | @deva[पपौ]      | he drank  | @deva[प] → @deva[प]   |
| @deva[वद्]  | @deva[उवाद]     | he spoke  | vowel-initial         |

The perfect is extremely common in epic and classical narrative. Learning the reduplication patterns for the most frequent roots enables rapid reading.
