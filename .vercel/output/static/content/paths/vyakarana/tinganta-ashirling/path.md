## concept - @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] — The Benedictive

@deva[आशीर्लिङ्] is a special mood used exclusively for expressing blessings, prayers, and solemn wishes. It shares the @deva[लिङ्] designation with @deva[विधिलिङ्] but has fundamentally different formation rules — the endings are @term[ārdhadhātuka] (not @term[sārvadhātuka]), so no @term[vikaraṇa] like @deva[शप्] is used.

**Paradigm of @deva[भू] (be) — @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] @deva[परस्मैपद]:**

|     | Singular       | Dual              | Plural         |
| --- | -------------- | ----------------- | -------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[भूयात्]  | @deva[भूयास्ताम्] | @deva[भूयासुः] |
| 2nd | @deva[भूयाः]   | @deva[भूयास्तम्]  | @deva[भूयास्त] |
| 1st | @deva[भूयासम्] | @deva[भूयास्व]    | @deva[भूयास्म] |

**Paradigm of @deva[कृ] (do) — @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] @deva[परस्मैपद]:**

|     | Singular         | Dual                | Plural           |
| --- | ---------------- | ------------------- | ---------------- |
| 3rd | @deva[क्रियात्]  | @deva[क्रियास्ताम्] | @deva[क्रियासुः] |
| 2nd | @deva[क्रियाः]   | @deva[क्रियास्तम्]  | @deva[क्रियास्त] |
| 1st | @deva[क्रियासम्] | @deva[क्रियास्व]    | @deva[क्रियास्म] |

**@deva[विधिलिङ्] vs. @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] comparison:**

| Feature                          | @deva[विधिलिङ्] (optative) | @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] (benedictive) |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Meaning                          | should, would, may         | may it be! (blessing)          |
| Endings                          | @deva[सार्वधातुक]          | @deva[आर्धधातुक]               |
| Vikaraṇa                         | yes (@deva[शप्], etc.)     | no                             |
| @deva[यासुट्] @deva[स्]-deletion | yes (non-final @deva[स्])  | no (@deva[स्] retained)        |
| @deva[भू] 3rd sg.                | @deva[भवेत्]               | @deva[भूयात्]                  |
| @deva[कृ] 3rd sg.                | @deva[कुर्यात्]            | @deva[क्रियात्]                |

## @ref[3.3.173] - आशिषि लिङ्लोटौ

**Key Terms:** @deva[आशीस्], @deva[लिङ्]

The affixes @deva[लिङ्] and @deva[लोट्] come after a verb when benediction (@deva[आशीस्]) is intended. @deva[आशीस्] means a blessing or solemn wish for the good of others.

**Examples of benedictions:**

- @deva[चिरं जीव्यात्] "May he live long!"
- @deva[भूयात् ते शुभम्] "May there be good for you!"
- @deva[क्रियात् सुकृतम्] "May he do good deeds!"
- @deva[स्वस्ति भूयात्] "May there be well-being!"

When @deva[लोट्] (imperative) is used instead, the sense is similar but less formal: @deva[जीवतु] "let him live" vs. @deva[जीव्यात्] "may he live."

## @ref[3.4.103] - यासुट् परस्मैपदेषूदात्तो ङिच्च

**Key Terms:** @deva[यासुट्], @deva[परस्मैपद], @deva[ङित्]

@deva[यासुट्] augments @deva[परस्मैपद] endings in both @deva[विधिलिङ्] and @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]. The endings are treated as @deva[ङित्] (which blocks @deva[गुण] and @deva[वृद्धि]).

In @deva[आशीर्लिङ्], @deva[यासुट्] produces the @deva[-यास्-] / @deva[-यात्] pattern that is the hallmark of the benedictive.

---

## @ref[3.4.102] - लिङस्सीयुट्

**Key Terms:** @deva[सीयुट्], @deva[आत्मनेपद]

The @deva[तिङ्] endings of @deva[लिङ्] take the augment @deva[सीयुट्]. This primarily applies to @deva[आत्मनेपद] forms:

- @deva[कृ] + @deva[सीयुट्] + @deva[त] → @deva[कृषीष्ट] "may he do" (Ā.)
- @deva[लू] + @deva[सीयुट्] + @deva[त] → @deva[लविसीष्ट] "may he cut" (Ā.)

The @deva[ट्] of @deva[सीयुट्] indicates it is placed before the ending.

---

## @ref[3.4.104] - किदाशिषि

**Key Terms:** @deva[कित्], @deva[आशीस्]

In benediction, @deva[यासुट्] is treated as @deva[कित्] (having indicatory @deva[क्]). This has crucial consequences:

1. Being @deva[कित्] triggers @deva[सम्प्रसारण] for roots like @deva[वच्]:
   - @deva[विधिलिङ्]: @deva[वच्यात्] (no @deva[सम्प्रसारण])
   - @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]: @deva[उच्यात्] (@deva[सम्प्रसारण]: @deva[व] → @deva[उ])

2. Being @deva[कित्] blocks @deva[गुण] (together with @deva[ङित्] from @ref[3.4.103]):
   - @deva[भू] → @deva[भूयात्] (not \*@deva[भोयात्] — no @deva[गुण])
   - @deva[कृ] → @deva[क्रियात्] (not \*@deva[करियात्] — no @deva[गुण])

---

## @ref[3.4.116] - लिङाशिषि

**Key Terms:** @deva[लिङ्], @deva[आशीस्], @deva[आर्धधातुक]

When the sense is benediction, the substitutes of @deva[लिङ्] are @deva[आर्धधातुक]. This is the defining rule that separates @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] from @deva[विधिलिङ्]:

**Consequences of @deva[आर्धधातुक] status:**

1. No vikaraṇa (@deva[शप्]) is added — the root directly takes the endings
2. @deva[इट्] augment may apply (by @ref[7.2.35])
3. The root appears in a more primitive form

Compare:

- @deva[विधिलिङ्] (@deva[सार्वधातुक]): @deva[भू] + @deva[शप्] + @deva[यासुट्] + ending → @deva[भवेत्]
- @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] (@deva[आर्धधातुक]): @deva[भू] + @deva[यासुट्] + ending → @deva[भूयात्]

---

## @ref[6.4.67] - एर्लिङि

**Key Terms:** @deva[ए], @deva[आ], @deva[लिङ्]

@deva[ए] substitutes for the @deva[आ] of certain roots in the Benedictive Active:

- @deva[दा] → @deva[दे] → @deva[देयात्] "may he give"
- @deva[स्था] → @deva[स्थे] → @deva[स्थेयात्] "may he stand"
- @deva[पा] → @deva[पे] → @deva[पेयात्] "may he drink/protect"
- @deva[गा] → @deva[गे] → @deva[गेयात्] "may he go"
- @deva[हा] → @deva[हे] → @deva[हेयात्] "may he abandon"

---

## @ref[7.2.79] - लिङः सलोपोऽनन्त्यस्य

**Key Terms:** @deva[स्], @deva[लोप], @deva[लिङ्]

In @deva[सार्वधातुक] @deva[लिङ्] (@deva[विधिलिङ्]), non-final @deva[स्] of @deva[यासुट्] is elided. But in @deva[आशीर्लिङ्], the @deva[स्] is **retained** because the endings are @deva[आर्धधातुक]:

- @deva[विधिलिङ्]: @deva[कुर्यात्] (@deva[स्] deleted)
- @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]: @deva[क्रियास्ताम्] (@deva[स्] retained)

This retention of @deva[स्] is one of the key distinguishing features of the benedictive.

---

## @ref[7.2.80] - अतो येयः

**Key Terms:** @deva[अत्], @deva[इय्], @deva[या]

After a stem ending in short @deva[अ], @deva[इय्] substitutes for @deva[या] of @deva[यासुट्] in @deva[सार्वधातुक] @deva[लिङ्] (@deva[विधिलिङ्]). This does **not** apply to @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]:

- @deva[विधिलिङ्]: @deva[पच्] + @deva[अ] + @deva[यास्] → @deva[पच्] + @deva[इय्] + ... → @deva[पचेत्]
- @deva[आशीर्लिङ्]: @deva[पच्] + @deva[यास्] → @deva[पक्षीष्ट] (Ā., very rare)

---

## concept - Benedictions in Literature

The benedictive is rare in Classical Sanskrit but culturally significant:

**1. Vedic blessings:**

- @deva[भूयात् ते शान्तिः] "May there be peace for you"
- @deva[जीव्यात् शतं शरदः] "May he live a hundred autumns"

**2. Epic and puranic:**

- @deva[भूयात् कल्याणम्] "May there be auspiciousness"
- @deva[विजयी भूयाः] "May you be victorious"

**3. Daily prayers:**

- @deva[स्वस्ति भूयात्] "May there be well-being"
- @deva[शुभं भूयात्] "May there be good"

**Common benedictive forms:**

| Form            | Root        | Meaning     |
| --------------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| @deva[भूयात्]   | @deva[भू]   | may it be   |
| @deva[जीव्यात्] | @deva[जीव्] | may he live |
| @deva[क्रियात्] | @deva[कृ]   | may he do   |
| @deva[देयात्]   | @deva[दा]   | may he give |
| @deva[गम्यात्]  | @deva[गम्]  | may he go   |
| @deva[भूयासुः]  | @deva[भू]   | may they be |
